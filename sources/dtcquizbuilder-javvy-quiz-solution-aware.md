Quiz Funnels are not only for hard core direct response

They just a fun, interactive way to onboard customers to your product

In the day of minimal dopamine, fast paced reels

Customers need to be entertained at all times

That’s what a quiz funnel does

It promises an outcome

Keeps them hooked

And most importantly, gets them to diagnose & admit to their own problem

While allowing a customised solution based on **their own answers**

Let’s Look at how Javvy is scaling with their quiz

Plus some actionable take-aways

Javvy recently launched their quiz funnel with almost ¼ of all traffic going to it

**The quiz funnel**

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HFKqCstXAAAUoiK?format=png&name=large)

The 1 minute protein coffee quiz

Simple offer

Viewers know what they’re in for

But they keep the fun, engaging brand colors

And leverage authority here to grab users attention with something new

“Where do you get your coffee from”

Since their audience is solution ware, they keep it short: **9-step quiz funnel**

Here's how it works.

**Steps 1–5 are the foreplay.**

"What protein do you use?"

"What are your goals?"

Easy. Low-threat. Zero cognitive load.

They're not segmenting. They're building micro-commitments. Every click is a tiny "yes" that makes the next click easier.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HFKqTUcbQAAKGRn?format=jpg&name=large)

Q4 and Q5 are sneaky

"how many coffees a day?"

and "how much protein per serving?"

look like personalization. They're actually AOV anchors.

You just told them you drink 3 coffees a day and need 2 scoops.

Now the bundle price on the results page feels justified — because you prescribed it to yourself.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HFKqYRrW8AA0GRV?format=png&name=large)

**Step 6 is the pivot.**

Full-screen interstitial. No question. Just agitation.

"What's REALLY in your coffee and protein shakes?"

Sugar. Fake flavors. Junk. Cheap incomplete proteins.

Five clicks deep and NOW they tell you your current routine is broken. Classic problem agitation — but it hits harder because you just spent 2 minutes describing that routine in detail.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HFKqcuPXYAA2DwP?format=jpg&name=large)

**Steps 7–9 are the close.**

Three rapid-fire "YES!" questions. But here's the move…

The "No" options are deliberately embarrassing.

"No, I love my unhealthy habits." "No, I love overpaying for coffee." "No, I love wasting time in lines."

Nobody clicks those. They're not real options.

They're a forcing function.

You click YES because the alternative makes you look like an idiot — even to yourself.

Three yeses in a row. Taste objection — handled. Price objection — handled. Convenience objection — handled.

Then the results page drops. "Personalized recommendation." 58% off. 4 free gifts. Today only.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HFKqhOWWoAEywGO?format=jpg&name=large)

**Two things worth stealing:**

**The shameful no technique.**

Frame your **"no"** answer as an identity nobody wants to claim.

Fence-sitters convert because inaction feels worse than action. Works in any quiz funnel.

**Self-prescribed quantity anchoring.**

Let the quiz do the upselling.

When the customer tells YOU how much they need before seeing the offer… bundle pricing feels like confirmation, not a pitch.

Simple funnel. Smart psychology.
