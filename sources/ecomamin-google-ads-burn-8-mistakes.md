## step 1: dump everything into one PMax campaign

take your entire product catalogue. add it to a single Performance Max campaign. give it a generic asset group. set the bid strategy to maximise conversion value. point the campaign at the homepage. hit publish.

what happens next is predictable.

google's algorithm, with no signal about who your buyer is, no ICP-specific creative, no intent-matched landing pages, and no segmentation, will optimise for the path of least resistance: branded search. people who already knew the brand and would have found the product anyway.

you pay for those clicks. you count those conversions. the ROAS looks acceptable. the account isn't actually doing anything you couldn't have done with a branded search campaign at a fraction of the cost.

the correct version: PMax as a cleanup layer, not the primary mechanism. one campaign per ICP with dedicated asset groups per persona, each with its own creative set, headline strategy, and landing page destination.

## step 2: use broad match on every keyword with no negatives

broad match is not a keyword match type. it's a budget donation to google.

"supplement" on broad match will show your ad to someone searching "supplement store near me", "supplement meaning in hindi", "supplement my income working from home". every one of those impressions is a potential wasted click.

the correct version: build a negative keyword list before the first campaign goes live. pull the search terms report for the first 30 days, add everything irrelevant to a master negative keyword list at the account level. repeat monthly.

## step 3: send all traffic to the homepage or product page

a cold prospect who has never heard of the brand and searched "how to improve sleep quality" is in information-gathering mode. they have a problem. they're looking for a solution category. they have not decided what to buy.

sending that prospect to a product page asking them to spend £45 on a supplement from a brand they've never encountered is not a proper funnel. you’re BURNING ads spend.

i've seen brands double their cold traffic conversion rate simply by inserting a single advertorial between the ad and the product page. nothing else changed.

same creative, keywords, and budget - just a different destination.

the correct version: match the landing page to the traffic temperature. cold TOF traffic to an advertorial. MOF problem-aware traffic to a comparison page or listicle. BOF branded traffic to the PDP.

## step 4: ignore the Shopping feed

the merchant center feed that shopify auto-generates is not a shopping strategy.

the Shopping title is Google's primary matching signal. if your title says "Brand Name Magnesium Capsules 500mg" instead of "Magnesium Glycinate Sleep Supplement 500mg 120 Capsules", you're invisible for every non-branded search that should be triggering your listing.

the correct version: pull the Shopping search terms report. filter for converting queries. rewrite your product titles so the first 60 characters contain those keywords, not the brand name.

## step 5: switch to target ROAS on day one

smart bidding on target ROAS requires data to work from. google's recommendation is 30-50 conversions per campaign per month before switching to a value-based bidding strategy.

below that threshold, the algorithm is guessing. the result is erratic performance, extended learning phases that reset every time a change is made, and CPAs that look unpredictable because they are.

the correct version: launch new campaigns on maximise clicks or maximise conversions with no CPA target. accumulate 30-50 conversions before introducing a target. the trigger is the conversion volume, not the calendar.

## step 6: run all campaigns to the same audience with no segmentation

your 45-year-old buyer with joint pain responds to completely different creative, copy, and landing page structure than the 28-year-old athlete who found you via a YouTube Shorts recommendation.

putting both of them in the same campaign, serving them the same ad, and sending them to the same page is the reason accounts with strong product-market fit struggle to scale.

the correct version: segment campaigns by ICP. one asset group per persona in PMax. one set of ad copy per intent tier in search. the message matches the person. the landing page matches the message.

## step 7: never test anything

the accounts that flatline are the accounts where nothing changes.

without tests, you have no learning. without learning, you have no improvement. the account performs at whatever level it started at and stays there.

the correct version: define one test per campaign type per month. document the hypothesis, the control, the variable, and the measurement criteria. run it. implement the winner. design the next test.

## step 8: skip the negative keyword architecture

negative keyword neglect compounds over time. the irrelevant queries that were cheap in month one become expensive habits by month six.

common categories of irrelevant spend that accumulate:

- competitor brand names you don't want to bid on
- informational queries with no purchase intent
- geographic irrelevance
- price-sensitive queries on premium products

build the negative list before launch. review the search terms report monthly. the account gets progressively cleaner. the budget goes further.
