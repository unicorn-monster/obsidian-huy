The worst thing you could ever hear a client say is:

> "What are we working on?"
> "What are results like right now?"
> "What's the agenda?"

That right there is proof that you haven't done a good enough job on communication and it's one of the core things we fight against at our agency.

During onboarding we do weekly calls, sometimes even two a week when we're moving fast…

And we make sure to give an update EVERY single day early on so there's never a question about what we're working on.

When you're a client literally all you can see is that Slack channel.

If there's no messages in the Slack channel coming in, the client will be thinking "is anything actually going on?"

Once onboarding is settled, we get through our key communication cadence:

> Every Monday: an automated stats report
> Every Tuesday: a manual report from the account manager covering what we did last week, what we're doing this week, and any key learnings
> Monthly: full report breakdowns
> Quarterly: business reviews and wider roadmap planning

This is what we've found works the best.
