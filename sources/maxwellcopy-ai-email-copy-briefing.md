"I fired my whole email team and replaced them with AI."

This is retarded and please don't do that.

We tried and our results suffered and clients churned.

Here's how we're now using it for our 100+ email clients:

1) Analysis

Dump numbers and results and see what AI finds.

Send in flow and campaign results, compare them to your benchmarks, flag anything broken, and rank your biggest opportunities by dollar impact.

It catches leaks you'd never find scrolling through Klaviyo yourself.

Do this at least monthly and just iterate on what it calls out.

2) Campaign calendars

Building a month of campaigns from a blank page is difficult and low-converting.

But give a properly trained AI (who has seen your campaign results from the past) your brand context and it'll map the whole month, the right send volume, a balance of educational, social proof, and sales angles, and it'll even catch the holidays you forgot.

Now the key word is TRAINED.

A raw ChatGPT calendar is too generic. So you need to build on actual email data and real framework.

3) Copy BRIEFING (the biggest one).

You can't just prompt "write me an email and make no mistakes."

The name of the game is context, context, context.

And NOT just using what AI gives you.

The knowledge, the brief, and the brand voice you feed it are what separate usable copy from AI slop.

We still use copywriters to oversee all the copy because it isn't fully there to generate and ship.

We built our entire system into a Claude skill that won't even write until it has the right context, then QAs its own output against the brief and checks itself for anything made up.

The output is miles past anything you'd get from a cold prompt.

So no, don't fire your team and hand the channel to AI.

Just use it for what it's good at, and you keep the rest.
