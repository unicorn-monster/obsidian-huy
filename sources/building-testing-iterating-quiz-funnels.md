Often it's the same questions that come up when testing & iterating on quiz funnels. After working with the team at clarflow.com I've been able to see behind the curtains of 100's of funnels. So here's some of the main mistakes I see made.

## Before Launching
There's 3 core elements that determine whether your quiz works, or not: **positioning**, **graduality**, **personalisation**. If you nail all 3 you're not building a quiz anymore, you're designing a money printer.

### Positioning
Positioning is 2 things: congruency of ads to quiz, and the core reason someone would want to complete this quiz. "Take the 2 minute survival quiz" = weak. "Take the 2 minute test to find out whether you'd survive WW3 and get your personalized survival profile" = specific and incentive to complete.

![[btiq-01.png]]

Positioning should give them a strong reason / offer just for completing. Something simple works: "your gut microbiome profile" for example. And it should also be a continuation of the ad — ads speaking about the gut → gut profile / gut health diagnosis; surviving WW3 → find out your survival chance %.

### Graduality
This is how you work your customers to get the yes, yes, yes effect. They agree with what you're saying, and they're also admitting & realising themselves they have a problem. "Do you wake up feeling tired?" → "How often does this happen?" → "Do you wish you had more energy throughout the day?" This gradually leads to realising they had a problem, and that this problem is a real issue impacting their life. Don't start off too strong — start with micro commitments ("how old are you?", "biggest day to day issues") — as they get more comfortable, start getting more personal. Favorite mental model: imagining a doctor's questioning — how he finds the problem and gradually goes deeper into the experience.

### Personalisation
If they fill out the whole quiz and get to a generic-ass page & offer, it undermines everything. Even the simplest line "based on your answers" helps. A trick with ClarFlow: pulling previous answers directly into the offer-page copy:
- Eyebrow: "Based on your results"
- Headline: "**<product>** is the perfect solution for **{gender}** between **{age}** that struggle with **{problem}**"
- Subhead: "100,000+ women like you achieved **{desired solution}** within 12 weeks"

With one simple copy section, you're speaking EXACTLY to this customer. This is zero-party data — marketing data directly from the customer's mouth, not guesses, not Reddit threads. This final offer page determines how many people checkout & buy, or click off disappointed.

## After Launching
Let's say you took everything above — or you're even running your quiz. First thing is identify where the major drop-off points are. If performance is bad and you haven't done everything above, it's fairly self-explanatory what to do next. But maybe your quiz is missing the mark a bit, or doing OK but you want it to do better — here's how to find weak points & split-test them.

First you want granular insights on the flow of the quiz — this lets you identify which questions have the highest drop-off rate, then tweak those. (This graph, as well as more in-depth analytics, is from Clarflow.)

![[btiq-03.png]]

As you'd imagine, the highest drop-off rate comes from the first question (initiation slide). So what matters here & what should you test? This ties back to the offer & promise — why should they complete the quiz? What will they gain? What sort of question works well here? Gain their attention immediately and get them to take action. Once they click on the first question, everything else becomes substantially easier. In short: offer & positioning to complete the quiz.

When you combine this A/B test with the question drop-offs, it becomes ridiculously easy to find weak points, test & improve on them.

## General KPIs to aim for
Disclaimer: the ONLY KPI that actually matters is profit. If it's profitable, scaling and allowing more spend incrementally, then who cares whether your drop-off rate is below the numbers here, or whether your first-step initiation is lower than you aim for. Maybe you can improve it & profit improves — but these metrics are for stacking small wins. In the case a quiz is a total flop, back to the drawing board.

- **Drop-off rate Q1:** aim for 50%-70% start rate.

![[btiq-06.png]]

- **Quiz completion rate:** aim for 20%-30% completion rate. (Caveat: I have seen quizzes sub-20% completion rate that are printing.)

![[btiq-07.png]]

- **Completion to purchase:** generally anywhere above 10% here (depending on completion rate).
