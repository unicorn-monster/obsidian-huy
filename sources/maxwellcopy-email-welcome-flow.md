It shocks me the amount of 9-figure brands that don't even have a properly set up email welcome flow.

It results in underperformance without them even knowing.

Almost always it's because the emails don't have any structure to them.

It's just a discount, a few random emails, and then nothing. No order to it all.

A good welcome flow gives each email ONE job and sends them in the right order.

But before you touch any of the emails, you've gotta get the setup right or none of the rest matters:

> Turn double opt-in off
> Fire your first email the second they sign up
> Keep your time delays 1-2 days apart
> Remind them of the discount in every single one

Get these wrong and the best emails in the world won't save you.

Now, let's break down the flow:

=====================

1) Email 1: The welcome

Job: Hand over the discount and get them back on the site.

The customer gave you their email for one reason and one reason only: the code.

So deliver it immediately, put the code and a button right at the top with a quick line on who you are. That's all!

The modern customer is a doom-scrolling zombie with a fried attention span so give them what they came for and get them clicking.

2) Emails 2-5: The filler emails

Job: Build trust and kill objections while they shop your competitors.

These are the emails that keep you top of mind the week or two that customers are deciding what to buy.

Send things like:

- Our story
- FAQs
- How the product works
- What's inside (ingredients, materials, etc)
- Social proof / Credibility, features
- How the brand is different from the rest
- Bestsellers

You won't use all of these so just pick the few that make sense for your brand, and remind them of the discount in each one.

3) Email 6: The last chance

Job: Pure urgency, and nothing else.

By now the trust is built, and they just need a shove to get past the finish line.

Some of the best-performing emails we've ever sent are literally "Last chance for 10% off, buy now" with a button.

Show them the code is about to expire, and FOMO does the rest.

4) Email 7: The text-based support email

Job: Win back the ones still sitting on the fence.

Where the last email was all urgency, this one is all human. Just have it come from a real person checking in.

Send a note from the founder saying "Saw you didn't use your code, so I extended it 24 hours, and reply if you've got any questions."

It reads like a friend reaching out instead of a pitch, and it's consistently one of the highest converters in the whole flow.

=====================

Once you set all of this up, it never really stops!

If you're already sending 3-4 campaigns a week, take an email that absolutely rips, add a line pointing to the welcome discount, and swap out your weakest email in the flow.

That way your flow keeps getting stronger without you writing a single new email.

Do this and you've now got a flow that turns cold traffic into buyers on autopilot.
