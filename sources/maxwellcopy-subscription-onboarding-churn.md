I've audited over 250+ subscription brands.

The #1 DRIVERS OF CHURN are the customer having too much product or they stop using the product.

The #1 LEVER TO DECREASE CHURN is having a proper onboarding email/sms sequence.

Your post-purchase flows need to be done properly to push daily consumption, set proper expectations, and educate on long term benefits

Here's how the best brands do it:

1. The first 7 days are everything. Most churn decisions are made before day 7 is up. Use days 1–7 to get the customer using the product daily — every email should push a consumption habit.

2. Hit the "why bother" objection head on. Most customers quietly wonder if they made the right call. Your emails need to reassure them: social proof from people who had the same doubts, founder stories that feel real, science that backs the product up.

3. Manage expectations for the long game. Most consumables take 30–60+ days to show results. If you don't set that frame upfront, they'll cancel before seeing anything. Tell them exactly when to expect what.

4. Stop burning onboarding on upsells. The second-biggest mistake I see is using those first 30 days to push upgrades, bundles, and referrals. Wrong. Build the habit first. Revenue follows retention.

5. The timeline cadence:

Go hard the first 7 days. Keep educating through day 30. Ease off after that.

Onboarding is the single biggest predictor of whether someone stays subscribed. Most brands burn those first 30 days pushing the next upsell, then wonder why retention is bad.

Get people using what they bought. Everything else follows.

---

If you run a subscription brand and your retention is slipping, it's often because of your improper onboarding.
