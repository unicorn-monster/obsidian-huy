title: Ugly ads win because research beats design
---

Ugly ads and landing pages often perform better, but not because they're ugly.

They perform better because the person who made them wasn't spending his time on designing the most beautiful ad or page, but on research so that he's writing copy and making content that resonates with his audience.

In the beginning you should spend most of your time understanding your customer.

How they think, what lingo they use to describe their problems and symptoms, what they've already tried that didn't work, what makes them skeptical, how they describe the dream outcome.

That's the only way to resonate with them, so don't waste your time on making things look "beautiful", it won't move the needle.

Good copy, an easy to read font (make it bigger than you think) on a contrasting background, and 1 accent color is more than enough to build creatives and pages that convert.

Your design should be purely optimized for ease of digestion of copy and content, and leading them to the next stage in your funnel (eg. where to click).

In later stages you can blend the two, great design that also converts. But until you do at least $10M/year, don't focus on design. Just keep it simple, and spend your time on things that actually drive conversion.
