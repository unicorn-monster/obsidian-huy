This is one of the major differences between a quiz and a listicle. Listicles & advertorials are not inherently fun, and customers get nothing (aside from closing a loop on a knowledge gap) — which is your goal with text-based pages: intrigue the visitor enough that they NEED to read all the information to close the loop.

The same thing applies to a quiz (they NEED to find out something)... BUT they always WANT to get there. They want to find out their skin profile. They want to figure out if their boyfriend is autistic. They want to know what their chance of survival during WW3 is. So how do you position a quiz to make someone want to complete it? Give them something.

Make them feel they're getting something: you're going to get a full diagnosis of why you're losing hair, or find out if you could survive the zombie apocalypse. If framed correctly, this is an inherently fun activity, and they'll likely get to the end just to see their personal results.

Take this from Gundry MD — their ads state "it depends on your gut type," and here you get to find out: what is your gut type. Think: IQ tests, personality tests. We all tried a random one online (and it was all a funnel).

## The Profile Trifecta
This is a very interesting element and I would bet we're going to see this become much more common in DTC. This will become recognisable — not only because of how well it works, but how many quiz funnels are using this. Originally popularised in the app space; DTC realised they could do the same thing.

**The Profile Page.** This is what all the content leads up to. Everything about "what would you do if zombies arrived at your door" (zombie survival product), "multiply 12 by 12" (IQ test), "how often you get shooting pains in your leg" (edema quiz) — these lead up to the profile, which (you guessed it) will always be severe (or low if it's like a testosterone product).

![[profile-trifecta-02.png]]

**The Future Pacing Page.** Now what you can do after this is offer a solution: "Based on your results, we believe you may need something to support your joint pain… See how to fix this →"

![[profile-trifecta-03.png]]

From there they go to the future pacing visual (diagrams & graphs work very well) — allow them to visualise a life without pain. Be specific on the time (AOV boost). What I like to do here is make the CTA something along the lines of "claim my offer" & add some text about "based on your answers, we can identify the perfect solution for you." Then add a loading screen about sourcing product, adding offer & confirm you're ready to try.

![[profile-trifecta-04.png]]

**The Offer Page.** This is where it all comes together.

![[profile-trifecta-05.png]]

Limited availability." — the callout names the specific quiz result and frames the discount as "unlocked" through completion, not just given.)

What also works super well is using a split step to send customers (based on their ideal desires) to an offer page with that desire or headline as the main headline. In this case you can see "hormonal foundation" — if you ran through it again and selected a different option, there'd be a different headline. This is an easy way to bump up CVR & AOV. Plus this final step with the confirmation loading screen rips.
