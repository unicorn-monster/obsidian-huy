Summary (paraphrased). Nutrops is a nootropic mushroom gummy brand owned by Gruns (same parent as Grüns Kids). Rather than skewing young, they built a dedicated congruent page for an older-grandmother segment.

![[nutrops-01.png]]

**The angle:** positioning is not "cognitive supplement" but "being the grandma who's really there / cognitively present with your grandkids" — framed as one of the few things a grandmother has left to hold onto.

![[nutrops-02.png]]

**Ads** speak directly to forgetfulness moments (brain fog, forgetting a party, forgetting a name known for years) — specific enough to feel personally observed rather than generic "boost memory" copy, paired with an aging-brain-slower angle alongside the more common aging-skin-slower angle.

![[nutrops-03.png]]
![[nutrops-04.png]]

**Landing page:** kept simple, message stays consistent from ad to page, built as a 5-Reasons-Why listicle. Instead of a doctor/coach authority figure, the storyteller is another grandmother. The headline leads with identity ("the grandma who's really there"), not the product category — meeting the reader emotionally before ever naming a supplement.

![[nutrops-05.png]]

**The messenger is chosen to resemble the market, not to out-rank it:** "Sherry," ~70, presented as an ordinary active person (reading to grandkids, hiking, doing puzzles) rather than a model/influencer — every photo signals "someone like you," building trust through resemblance rather than authority, which the author frames as the right lever for this specific audience (older women, or younger women shopping for their mother/grandmother).

![[nutrops-06.png]]

**Awareness-matched copy:** this audience isn't searching for "nootropics" — they're noticing afternoon brain fog and quietly worrying about staying sharp. The page avoids clinical/ingredient-forward language up front, instead talking about afternoon energy dips, keeping a routine, wanting to feel present.

![[nutrops-07.png]]
![[nutrops-08.png]]

**CTA integrated directly into the article** (no separate click-through needed) — first-order discount, 30-day money-back guarantee, free fast shipping all bundled right there.

![[nutrops-09.png]]

Author's overall take: strong example of a problem-aware-stage landing page for the supplement category, built around resemblance-based trust rather than authority-based trust.
