## Quiz Analysis (funnel stage → question → how it primes the buyer)

**Ad → quiz landing page.** Ads push users to the "Ask SpoiledBrain" quiz. The page claims most consumers use products they don't need and that SpoiledBrain uses machine-learning to find exactly what your body needs. → Instils uncertainty (maybe you're using the wrong products) + establishes authority ("millions of data points") + FOMO on wasting money.

**1. Health & symptom diagnostic.** "Do any of the below apply to you?" (Feel tense/overwhelmed, digestive issues, low concentration, low energy, don't sleep well, + "Nope"). → Encourages self-diagnosis, highlights pain points; selecting symptoms = micro-commitment, users admit issues and become receptive.

Fitness level + protein intake questions → collect lifestyle data + status-priming (people feel seen). Digestive + energy/mood questions → deepen self-diagnosis; more acknowledged issues = more invested. Prior supplement use → segments new vs existing users (upsell vs risk-free trial). Flavor preference (mango vs pineapple) → personalizes, increases investment/anticipation.

**2. Goals & demographic segmentation.** Desired improvements (smoother skin, thicker hair, stronger nails…) + age range. → Users articulate outcomes = emotional connection; age segments dosage/messaging (anti-aging vs performance). Skin type & wrinkles questions → make user acknowledge visible aging, so collagen seems necessary (appearance anxiety).

**Social-proof interlude.** Quiz pauses to show "5 of 6 users saw fewer wrinkles…" / "4 of 5 saw visibly thicker hair…". → Affirmation + social proof reduce scepticism; progress bar keeps momentum.

**3. Hair-focused diagnostic.** "What bothers you most about your skin?" + hair description + current hair condition + a **hair-loss severity slider** ("Nope" → "Help! It's serious"). → Slider engages physically, compels quantifying the pain; higher = stronger urgency + more aggressive offer.

**4. Marketing attribution.** "Where did you hear about SpoiledChild?" + "When?" → No effect on recommendation; pure attribution data, asked late when user is already invested (answers honestly).

**5. Lead capture & commitment.** Email (required) + phone (optional) for "personalized matches", plus a full-sized mystery gift for joining SMS. → Endowed-progress effect (bar nearly full → willing to share); mystery gift drives SMS opt-in = future channel.

**INSANE UPSELL** 🤯 — click add to cart → "unlock your best results" with the double-action pack. 2× products, **$110 if you keep**, but **$0 now.** Also on subscription. This is how you get creative with subscription offers. 60-year-old women aren't thinking about how hard it is to return this — they just see "$0 now for 2, $110 off the rip." ## Closing thoughts
Prime example: crazy images get the click, the quiz primes buyers, then BOOM — an offer you genuinely feel dumb to say no to. The 3 steps (often done in reverse):
1. Get a good offer (that won't bankrupt you) — theirs is a later-stage offer.
2. The funnel — max CVR and sales. Quizzes are great for this.
3. MORE ADS — funnel is solid, now good ads to get more clicks cheap.
