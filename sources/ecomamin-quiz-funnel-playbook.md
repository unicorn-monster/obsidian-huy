System for building ad→quiz→product-page funnels, with the core focus on how the TRAFFIC-GENERATION ad strategy must differ by platform (Google Search vs YouTube vs Meta) even though the quiz itself stays the same.

## Why quiz beats direct ad→product-page
Traditional funnel (LP→PDP→upsells→thank-you→email→retarget) converts cold 0.8-2.3%. Ad→Quiz→PDP converts 8-18% — 5-10x from the same traffic, because by the time someone reaches the product page they've spent 3-4 minutes answering questions (sunk cost), opened curiosity loops (need to see results), believe the product was formulated for them (perceived personalization even if it's 1 of 5 preset options), and have been reframed from "being sold to" to "being advised" (authority transfer).

## Platform Intent Matrix — same quiz, 3 different ad approaches
| Platform | User intent | Awareness | Best quiz angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google Search | Problem-aware, actively seeking | mid | Educational/diagnostic |
| YouTube | Problem-aware, researching (learning mode) | mid | Story-based/visual proof |
| Meta | Often problem-UNAWARE, scrolling | low | Curiosity/self-discovery |

**Google Search:** target question-based keywords ("what [product] is right for me", "why do i have [problem]"), quiz-intent keywords ("[product] quiz", "[product] finder"), and comparison/confusion keywords ("[A] vs [B]", "which [product] should i get") — these are problem-aware searchers, not brand searchers, which is exactly the quiz funnel's target. Ad copy template: Headline 1 = "Not Sure Which [Product] You Need?", Headline 2 = "Take Our 2-Minute Analysis", Description = "Answer 8 questions... quiz analyzes your needs and recommends the exact [product] for you." Two campaign types: quiz-intent keywords (tighter, $50-100/day, target ROAS 3x+) vs problem-aware keywords (broader/colder, $100-200/day, target ROAS 2.5x+).

**YouTube:** two creative formats — story-based hook (person tried everything, took quiz, got personalized formula, results) or educational hook (shocking stat → quiz as the fix). Campaign structure: in-market audiences (problem-aware content categories), custom-intent (competitor URLs/review searches), remarketing to quiz-visited/quiz-started/quiz-completed-no-purchase segments. Notable: 55+ age group converts HIGHEST on YouTube quiz funnels (14-20% vs 8-12% for 18-34) because they watch full ads, trust video like TV, have disposable income, and are problem-aware from aging.

**Meta:** audience is problem-UNAWARE — 3 hook frameworks: self-discovery ("find out your [metric] in 2 minutes"), problem agitation ("if you [specific symptom], you need to see this"), social proof ("12,000+ people discovered their perfect [product] with this quiz"). Campaign structure: cold (Advantage+ Shopping, all-quiz-focused creative), warm (retarget by quiz-drop-off-point: visited-no-start / started-no-finish / finished-no-buy, each with a different message), engaged-video-viewer remarketing by watch-percentage.

Ecosystem effect: Meta creates awareness → Google captures intent → YouTube builds trust ("they're everywhere, must be legit") → combined 3-5x better ROAS than any single platform run alone.

## Budget phasing (illustrative, $10k/mo → $50k/mo)
Testing phase: 40% Meta / 40% Google Search / 20% YouTube. Scaling phase: 30/50/20. Maturity phase: 25/60/15 — Google Search share grows over time as it's the highest-ROAS channel at scale once quiz-intent + problem-aware keyword pools are built out.

## Product page ("pre-sold traffic") template
By the product page, the visitor is "90% sold" — the page's only job is confirm the choice, remove final friction, make checkout easy. Concrete pattern:
- Headline pulls their EXACT quiz scores: "Formulated specifically for: → High Sleep Latency (78/100) → Frequent Night Waking (65/100)..."
- Ingredient breakdown mapped 1:1 to each scored dimension ("FOR YOUR HIGH SLEEP LATENCY (78/100): → L-Theanine... → Magnesium...") — makes the "custom formula" claim concrete instead of asserted.
- CTA button = ownership language ("Get My Custom Formula"), explicitly NOT "Add to Cart" / "Buy Now".
- Testimonials matched to similar quiz scores ("My sleep latency score was also 75+...").
- FAQ objection-handling references the personalization directly ("Generic melatonin treats ONE issue... your custom formula addresses YOUR specific profile: 78/65/82").
- Trust-signal placement table: SSL badge + money-back guarantee above fold near price (HIGH impact), review count above fold + social proof section (HIGH), verified reviews in social proof section (MEDIUM), FDA/compliance in FAQ/footer (LOW).

## 7-email quiz-abandoner sequence (skeleton)
Immediate (5 min): "your results are ready, resume where you left off." Day 1: "why most solutions fail" (explains that different sub-profiles need different ingredients — reframes past failed attempts as wrong-match, not user's fault). Day 3: "what your symptoms reveal" (symptom→root-cause mapping, positions brand as diagnostic authority). Day 5: social-proof case story ("Rachel had the same problem — here's her exact quiz result and what worked"). Day 7: scarcity/loss-frame ("your incomplete quiz data will be deleted tomorrow, you're already 60% done"). Day 10: "what separates people who succeed" (identity framing — they test, they don't guess). Day 14 (final): root-cause reveal + explicit "last email about this."

## Author's own framing of what actually matters
Explicit disclaimer-adjacent framing from the author: execution speed beats the playbook itself (most readers do nothing), quiz+ad+email need ongoing creative production (10-15 new ads/month, not a build-once asset), and "the brands exiting for 7-8 figures built their entire go-to-market around the quiz — it's not a side feature."
