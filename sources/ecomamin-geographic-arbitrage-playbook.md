Thesis: US Google Ads is the most saturated, most expensive market in the world (CPCs tripled in 4 years). Non-US markets with real disposable income and low competition offer dramatically better margin-per-click — the relevant metric isn't market size, it's margin per click.

## 3 markets flagged as underpriced right now
- **Australia** — GDP/capita comparable to US, real demand for the same health/wellness/home/lifestyle categories, but nowhere near as many DTC brands competing for attention.
- **The Balkans (Serbia, Croatia, Slovenia)** — growing middle class, high English proficiency, strong youth demographics, Western-lifestyle aspiration → direct demand transfer from US-proven products. CPCs often 60-70% lower than Western Europe for equivalent intent.
- **Saudi Arabia** — high GDP/capita, large young population, fast ecommerce adoption, aspirational buyer profile (supplements, fitness, premium personal care) — same categories that print in the US, far less auction competition.

## Case study: $57k → $1.21M/mo in Australia, 21.2x blended ROAS (9 months)
Brand's US account had plateaued (ROAS compressed 7x→4.5x over 18 months as competitors piled in). Tested Australia at $6k/mo alongside the still-running US account.

| Month | Spend | Revenue | ROAS | Key action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | $6k | $28k | 4.7x | Baseline validation |
| 3 | $8k | $52k | 6.5x | GMC feed optimization + negative refinement |
| 6 | $12k | $95k | 7.9x | Geo bid adjustments — Perth + Adelaide top converters |
| 9 | $22k | $210k | 9.5x | Full deployment: search + shopping + PMax w/ brand exclusion |
| Total | $57k | $1.21M | 21.2x | 9-month blended |

Being the first serious operator in a market compounds: quality scores build faster (less competition), feed-trust scores improve faster (cleaner conversion signal), the algorithm learns the customer profile faster (higher signal-to-noise).

## The geographic-arbitrage principle — city-level, not country-level
Blanket national campaigns let the algorithm allocate budget by volume, which systematically overspends on the biggest city even when it converts worse. Australia case: Sydney 2.1% CVR vs Adelaide 4.2% vs Perth 3.8-4.2% — yet Sydney got most of the spend by default. Fix: pull the location report → conversion rate by city → bid adjustments matching actual performance (Adelaide +60%, Perth +40%, same total budget). Called "one of the highest-ROI 2-hour tasks in Google Ads" because almost no brand does it.

## Targeting templates by market
**Australia:** Afterpay + Zippay at checkout (+15-20% CVR). Top cities: Perth, Adelaide, Brisbane. $X.99 pricing, GST-inclusive beats GST-exclusive. Budget split: branded search 10-15% ($0.20-0.60 CPC), high-intent shopping 40-50%, problem-aware search 25-30% (phrase+broad, tROAS), competitor conquest 10-15%.
**Saudi Arabia:** run Arabic and English campaigns SEPARATELY (not one bilingual campaign). Mobile-first even more than US (desktop bid −30 to −40%). Evening/weekend-heavy dayparting. Ramadan = demand surge, needs dayparting shift. Shopping feed needs Arabic-translated titles in a supplemental feed. Certain imagery/claims won't land culturally.
**The Balkans:** Serbia/Croatia/Slovenia are different languages — segment, don't lump. PayPal critical alongside card. English works for 18-34; native language significantly outperforms 35+. CPCs often $0.20-0.50 vs $2-4 for equivalent US/UK terms.

## Prompt library — qualify any market before spending (5 prompts)
1. **Market Qualification** — feed `[product category]` + `[country]`, ask for: top-5-keyword local search volume, major local competitors + scale, dominant payment/fulfillment expectations, cultural/regulatory considerations, retail spend/capita vs US baseline → output a viability score.
2. **Competitive Density Analysis** — how many brands appear for top-10 category keywords, local vs international brand dominance, estimated CPC range, dominant ad-copy angles → classify market saturated/emerging/untapped.
3. **Keyword Research by Market** — structure into bottom-funnel transactional / mid-funnel problem-aware / comparison / research terms, each with local volume + intent score + competition.
4. **Creative Angle Adaptation** — which US benefit-angles resonate locally vs are US-centric, local reference points/influencers, cultural values to amplify or avoid, local price-anchoring → output an adaptation brief.
5. **Geographic Bid Strategy** — which cities/regions show highest CVR, highest AOV, lowest competition for the category → output city-level bid-adjustment recommendations.

## Margin-based bidding — rebuild CPC ceiling from unit economics, not US habits
Formula: `(AOV × target conversion rate × gross margin) ÷ target ROAS = maximum CPC`. Example: 65% margin, £45 price, 4x min ROAS, 3% CVR → max CPC ≈ £0.85. If comparable local keywords cost £0.30 because competition is low, there's large headroom to bid aggressively and stay profitable. Run this calculation PER MARKET before setting bids — explains why low-competition markets often out-profit higher-volume ones.

## Local trust/payment signals by market
Australia: Afterpay/Zippay (+20-30% CVR claimed), GST-inclusive pricing, "Australian owned"/"ships from Sydney" trust copy even when fulfillment is 3PL. Saudi Arabia: Arabic copy on every pre-checkout touchpoint outperforms English-only even among English-proficient buyers (lower cognitive friction); local WhatsApp support adds trust for higher-AOV purchases. Balkans: display local currency (HRK/RSD) rather than EUR/USD even though buyers are comfortable with both; card + PayPal covers most transactions.

## Month-by-month scaling sequence per new market
Month 1 (Validate): hero product only, exact+phrase match only, $3-5k/mo, don't expand before 30+ conversions. Month 2 (Establish): broad match + tROAS on validated keywords, add shopping campaign, start building negative list. Month 3 (Scale): add secondary products, expand keyword themes, add PMax, apply city-level bid adjustments. Month 4+ (Full deployment): full account structure, Bing expansion (strong in AU/UK), YouTube retargeting, display remarketing.

## Why early entry compounds (and the window isn't permanent)
First-movers accumulate quality scores, feed-trust scores, algorithm training on local conversion patterns, and larger remarketing pools — a brand running competently in a market for 18 months has structurally lower effective CPCs than a same-day entrant. As more brands catch on, CPCs rise and margins compress — framed as a moat that's easier to build now than to displace later.
