# Native ads ≠ Meta ads

Many would consider this a native ad:

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HLb-LGUbAAAE3zH?format=jpg&name=large)

Ok. It doesn't have any text overlay. Great. **Still not a native ad.**

Meta ads can never be native ads.

Meta users behave completely different to native platforms.

And if you listen to any of my advice, but try to apply it to meta, please don't blame me if you lose money...

## But What Actually Are Native Ads?

If you scroll down all the way, on literally any big US newspaper/ magazine, you'll likely see some variation of a "read more" section.

It could look something like this:

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HLb8W8bXgAEYMLw?format=jpg&name=large)

**These are real native ads!**

You find them on the open web, meaning all kinds of websites in the internet (mostly newspapers/ magazines).

**What makes them so powerful is that they look like real journalist articles from a "read more" section.**

This means that the user is still in "learning mode" when reading your advertorial.

DO YOU EVEN KNOW WHAT THAT MEANS?

It means YOU are the authority when it comes to educating the reader about the problem they have.

AND: How your product is gonna fix that.

**THEY WANT YOU TO TELL THEM HOW TO SOLVE THEIR PROBLEMS.**

On Meta you have to fight for every bit of their attention between all those brainrot reels.

On Native they are already reading articles, interested in learning something new.

**The only thing you need to do is convert that curiosity into a desire to buy.** (write this down, it might be the most important sentence of my X account)

That's also why copywriting is SO important: You need to be able to plant a thought in your readers mind that grows with every line they read, until they can't resist buying what you offer.

## Scaling Is Not Hard On Native.

And here's the best part: Achieving big scale on native is much easier than meta.

Once your initial funnel is dialed in and the metrics are good, you can go from 300/d to 5k/d literally within a week.

Why? **CPM's remain stable as you scale.**

They actually favor big spenders (I'll go into that in another article).

I personally know brands that run only 5 Advertorials and spend more than 20k/d on native ads.

You don't need a million different angles to scale. Ad fatigue is much slower.

Honestly native ads are just fun. Its what meta used to be in 2016...

## Why Brands Can't Figure Out Native

So if it's this good, why isn't everyone running it?

I'm not going to pretend it's plug and play.

You need to learn what your audience is responding well to. And its completely different than on meta...

The first campaigns usually lose before they win (that's normal).

This initial learning curve is the real moat.

The brands already winning here tend to stay quiet about it, because the quiet is part of the edge.
