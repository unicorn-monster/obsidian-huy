title: Blaming strategy when execution is the real problem
---

One of the biggest copes I see is blaming the strategy when the execution was just bad.

It's also a sign of a low performer, because blaming a strategy someone else set is easier than admitting their own execution was shit.

The dangerous part is when the strategy actually gets changed, because it usually isn't the problem.

Before changing your strategy always ask yourself if it was actually executed well and be very fucking honest to yourself, it will save you a lot time, money and stress.
