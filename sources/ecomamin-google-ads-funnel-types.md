the typical google ads account runs exactly one funnel: ad → product page → checkout. it converts at 2%, cpa climbs every month, and nobody can find the leak.

the leak is structural. one funnel can only match one kind of buyer intent, and you're paying for at least five kinds in the same campaign. the accounts doing 8x roas in 2026 fix this by running 5-7 funnel types at once, each pointed at a specific layer of intent - the same product, shown on a different page to a buyer at a different stage of the decision.

here's every funnel type that actually works for ecom right now, when to use each one, and what kind of traffic it converts best.

## the advertorial funnel

what it is: a long-form educational article (1,500-2,500 words) that reads like magazine content but functions as a sales page. it walks a problem-aware reader through five stages: hook their attention with the exact symptom they searched, agitate the cost of ignoring the problem, explain the mechanism behind why it happens, introduce the solution category before naming any product, then position your product as the best version of that category.

when to use it: top-of-funnel symptom-based search queries where the buyer doesn't know the product category exists yet. also works well for mid-funnel category search when the buyer needs education about why your specific form, dose, or manufacturing process matters.

what traffic it converts: people searching problems ("why do i wake up at 3am"), people comparing solution types ("magnesium glycinate vs oxide"), people researching categories ("best natural sleep supplement"). converts cold traffic at 4-8% when written well, versus 1-2% on a product page for the same queries.

the key principle: the advertorial must genuinely educate. the reader should learn something valuable even if they don't buy. if it reads like a sales letter dressed up as an article, it won't work because buyers in 2026 can smell manipulation immediately.

## the comparison page funnel

what it is: a structured side-by-side comparison of your product against 1-4 competitors. includes a comparison table with specific criteria (price per serving, ingredient form, third-party testing, guarantee terms), an honest assessment section that acknowledges competitor strengths before explaining your advantages, and a clear recommendation with cta.

when to use it: competitor conquesting campaigns where you're bidding on competitor brand names, and mid-funnel comparison search queries like "brand X vs brand Y" or "best magnesium brand 2026."

what traffic it converts: people who already know the category and are deciding between brands. these are high-intent buyers mid-comparison. converts at 12-18% because the visitor arrives comparing and leaves with a clear answer.

the key principle: honesty is mandatory. acknowledge 2-3 things the competitor genuinely does well. the moment a buyer reads something honest about a competitor, they trust everything else on the page. a dishonest comparison page converts worse than a product page because it triggers the scepticism response.

## the listicle funnel

what it is: a "best of" ranking page structured as an editorial review. "the 5 best magnesium supplements for sleep (2026 tested and reviewed)." your product is positioned #1 with the most detail and strongest social proof. competitors are included with honest pros and cons.

when to use it: mid-funnel "best \[product\]" search queries and competitor conquesting. also works as organic seo content that compounds over time.

what traffic it converts: people browsing the category who want a curated recommendation. these buyers trust editorial-style rankings and will follow the recommendation if the review feels credible. converts at 7-12%.

the key principle: rate your product 4.8 not 5.0 because a perfect score looks fake. include real competitors. be honest about your cons. the listicle that converts best reads like an independent reviewer spent a weekend testing all six products - the moment it sounds like the brand crowned itself, the credibility is gone.

## the quiz funnel

what it is: a 5-7 question interactive assessment that segments the buyer by their specific situation and recommends a personalised product. landing page → quiz questions → personalised results page with product recommendation → product page or checkout.

when to use it: when you have multiple product variants or multiple use cases for the same product. works across TOF, MOF, and even retargeting.

what traffic it converts: people with choice paralysis who don't know which variant to buy. people searching symptoms who need help connecting their problem to the right solution. converts at 11-15% because the recommendation feels personalised.

the key principle: the quiz has to genuinely function as a diagnostic - that's the whole reason it out-converts a static page. questions should be genuinely relevant to the recommendation logic. and the results page must explain WHY this product matches their specific answers.

## the product page funnel (direct to PDP)

what it is: a standard ecom product detail page with hero image, headline, price, features, social proof, guarantee, and add-to-cart. optimised for conversion with benefit-led copy, trust badges, review integration, and subscription options.

when to use it: transactional bottom-funnel search queries where the buyer has already decided to purchase. "buy magnesium glycinate," "magnesium gummies order online," "magnesium supplement free shipping."

what traffic it converts: people ready to buy right now. these are the highest-intent queries in the account and they convert at 8-14% on a well-built PDP. sending this traffic to an advertorial adds friction and kills conversions because the buyer doesn't need education, they need a checkout button.

the key principle: the PDP for transactional traffic needs price visible above the fold, star ratings, clear add-to-cart, trust signals, subscription option, and bundle upsells. nothing educational above the fold - this buyer already chose the category, so the page's only job is to make ordering frictionless.

## the VSL funnel

what it is: a video sales letter page where the primary content is a 5-15 minute video walking through the problem, the solution, the product, and the offer. typically used with a founder story angle. page has minimal text, the video does the selling, and the cta appears below or beside the video.

when to use it: works best for high-aov products ($100+) where the purchase decision requires more trust and education than a PDP can deliver. also effective for health products where the founder's personal story adds credibility.

what traffic it converts: mid-funnel category search and retargeting audiences. converts at 5-9% depending on video quality and offer strength. works less well for TOF symptom traffic because the buyer needs text-based education before they're willing to watch a video.

the key principle: the first 30 seconds of the video determine everything. lead with the problem, not the product. and keep the production quality deliberately imperfect because polished VSLs trigger the "this is an ad" response while raw, founder-led videos build trust.

## the application funnel

what it is: a multi-step form where the buyer applies to purchase or book a consultation rather than buying directly. typically landing page → application form → qualification → call or personalised offer.

when to use it: high-ticket products ($500+), subscription programs, or premium service bundles where the buyer needs to be qualified before purchase. also works for products that require customisation.

what traffic it converts: mid-funnel comparison searchers and retargeting audiences who need human interaction before committing to a large purchase. converts at 3-6% to application submission, then 30-50% from application to purchase.

the key principle: the application creates perceived exclusivity and raises the perceived value of the product. "not everyone qualifies" is a powerful positioning mechanism that makes the buyer want it more. but the application has to do real work - qualify, customise, route to the right offer. the moment a buyer senses it's a velvet rope with nothing behind it, the exclusivity reads as a gimmick and the whole mechanism backfires.

## the homepage funnel

what it is: the brand's homepage, optimised as a conversion page rather than a navigation hub. hero section with primary offer, social proof bar, featured products, brand story section, and trust signals.

when to use it: branded search campaigns where the buyer searched your brand name specifically. also works as the destination for broad awareness campaigns where the buyer isn't looking for a specific product.

what traffic it converts: brand-aware buyers who want to explore before committing. converts at 4-8% for branded search traffic. should never be used for non-branded traffic because it's too generic to match specific search intent.

the key principle: the homepage funnel only works when the buyer already knows and trusts the brand. for any non-branded traffic, a specific landing page matched to the search intent will always outperform the homepage.

## matching funnels to search intent layers

the art of google ads for ecom is matching the right funnel to the right search intent. here's how the mapping works across the 3-layer search system:

TOF symptom queries → advertorial or quiz funnel. never a product page.

MOF category search → advertorial, comparison page, or PDP depending on how educated the buyer is.

MOF comparison search → comparison page or listicle. the buyer is mid-decision.

MOF transactional search → direct to PDP. the buyer is ready. don't add friction.

BOF branded search → homepage or hero PDP. the buyer knows you. close the sale.

conquesting → comparison page or listicle. the buyer knows the competitor. show them why you're better.

two accounts can run the same product on the same budget with the same creative and still land at 3x versus 8x. the only variable left to explain that spread is whether each click hits a page built for where the buyer actually is in the decision. fix the architecture and the traffic you're already paying for converts at a different number.
