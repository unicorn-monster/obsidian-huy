For the past few years, we've been testing a bunch of strategies to help ecom brands acquire more new customers.

Out of everything…

This has probably been the most reliable acquisition strategy we've used across our client base.

The comparison funnel.

![](https://rhetorical-garage-a1e.notion.site/image/attachment%3A19f17266-84a5-4d06-92a3-5d3484e24cac%3AGroup_217_(2).png?table=block&id=397a60cf-8ed8-8019-b247-fed096f87bd0&spaceId=d9fb0601-20b1-4779-a1c6-d68592cdd197&width=2000&userId=&cache=v2&imgBuildSrc=requestProxiedImageUrl)

It generally goes after the solution-aware segment of the market.

They have already decided they need this type of product. Now they're trying to figure out which brand to buy from.

This is where you step in.

Rather than trying to sell them, you help them compare their options.

You do that with a review comparing the top brands in the category, written like a neutral blog post:

Explain to the reader what to look for before buying and why each detail matters

Rank the top brands in the category against those criteria

This funnel is working really well right now because:

Many markets have skeptical buyers who resist direct pitches

People are more willing to read an article than land on another PDP or sales page

The review format lowers sales resistance because it feels like educational content.

The foundation of the whole funnel is the third-party comparison page.

It's a ranked list of products in your category that reads like an article/blog post.

We've scaled it into 6 figures/month in spend for plenty of clients, usually sitting around 2 to 3x ROAS.

It's worked so well that it's often the thing that finally gets a channel to scale for brands.

Take this account for example.

The brand had never been able to crack Google Search.

It works in almost every category we've tested.

We’ve getting great results in these markets:

And more placements means more opportunities to scale.

## The Anatomy of a High-Converting 3rd-Party Comparison Page

Now let’s quickly go through the standard structure of a 3rd-party comparison page.

Signals this is editorial content, not a direct ad, borrowing trust from a "reviewer" identity

![](https://rhetorical-garage-a1e.notion.site/image/attachment%3Aabc245f2-1003-432e-a1c0-bc64e8d3aa07%3Abb53fefb-5575-4810-b3a9-cd17bbe598b3.png?table=block&id=397a60cf-8ed8-80e0-b7cf-ddafacbc4e8b&spaceId=d9fb0601-20b1-4779-a1c6-d68592cdd197&width=1700&userId=&cache=v2&imgBuildSrc=requestProxiedImageUrl)

### Headline

States the topic plus a year/freshness marker ("Best \[Product Category\] of \[Year\]")

Subhead adds specificity/credibility: "We Tested and Reviewed the Top 3..."

![](https://rhetorical-garage-a1e.notion.site/image/attachment%3Ac7d5a3ae-a894-4062-b828-9813ff8a2c9b%3A8138acfa-ae6a-4b60-812d-ebe9f3e81fbb.png?table=block&id=397a60cf-8ed8-80b0-97b5-d8687e43f082&spaceId=d9fb0601-20b1-4779-a1c6-d68592cdd197&width=1020&userId=&cache=v2&imgBuildSrc=requestProxiedImageUrl)

### Personal Hook / Problem Acknowledgment

Establish stakes tied to the topic (why this problem matters)

Hero image showing the reviewer/expert in context

![](https://rhetorical-garage-a1e.notion.site/image/attachment%3A04f90109-7598-405a-bc79-5539c6f72a25%3Af9e37dfd-ce18-43b6-9fe0-1261528a1719.png?table=block&id=397a60cf-8ed8-808e-beae-db5dbb1c9eef&spaceId=d9fb0601-20b1-4779-a1c6-d68592cdd197&width=1020&userId=&cache=v2&imgBuildSrc=requestProxiedImageUrl)

#### Problem Agitation

Names the common failure mode of existing solutions ("not without a downside," "too much X can Y")

Sets up criteria for what a good solution looks like

![](https://rhetorical-garage-a1e.notion.site/image/attachment%3A1b18a643-f28a-441e-a483-073d1032bafb%3Aimage.png?table=block&id=397a60cf-8ed8-8005-9d14-df27aefce074&spaceId=d9fb0601-20b1-4779-a1c6-d68592cdd197&width=1310&userId=&cache=v2&imgBuildSrc=requestProxiedImageUrl)

#### Educational / Buyer's Guide Section

Bulleted list of features/criteria, each bolded term with explanation

Supporting images per feature (if relevant)

![](https://rhetorical-garage-a1e.notion.site/image/attachment%3A9aa6c35a-e7ea-41b8-868d-ae6fe2ba70a6%3Aimage.png?table=block&id=397a60cf-8ed8-803b-9ab6-d2534c43f59a&spaceId=d9fb0601-20b1-4779-a1c6-d68592cdd197&width=740&userId=&cache=v2&imgBuildSrc=requestProxiedImageUrl)

Methodology / "Our Testing Process" Section

Explains how the reviews were conducted (criteria, expertise, rigor)

Builds credibility for the rankings that follow. Frame it as a non-biased review.

![](https://rhetorical-garage-a1e.notion.site/image/attachment%3A44cbaac0-e685-4a66-b685-aa479ac1c1fa%3Aimage.png?table=block&id=397a60cf-8ed8-80f7-baf1-e8be529c0e09&spaceId=d9fb0601-20b1-4779-a1c6-d68592cdd197&width=930&userId=&cache=v2&imgBuildSrc=requestProxiedImageUrl)

Ranked Product Reviews (Top 3 Countdown)

Repeated block structure per product, ranked #1 to #3:

Rank badge/label (e.g., "#1 Choice")

Product logo/name

Short description paragraph

CTA button plus pricing (regular price and sale price/urgency)

Expanded description paragraph(s) with contextual/seasonal offer callout

Star rating

Image gallery (before/after or product-in-use)

Pros / Cons two-column list

![](https://rhetorical-garage-a1e.notion.site/image/attachment%3Adcb73ba2-4962-4a25-9f34-9d7d09a3102e%3Aimage.png?table=block&id=397a60cf-8ed8-803a-9037-c5fa615b03a2&spaceId=d9fb0601-20b1-4779-a1c6-d68592cdd197&width=830&userId=&cache=v2&imgBuildSrc=requestProxiedImageUrl)

Final Verdict / Summary

"Final Thoughts on..." recap section

Side-by-side comparison table across all products and features

Reiterates the winner and why, compares runner-ups briefly

Closing credibility statement and link to the best product

### A few notes when making this page

Write in a neutral tone throughout.

Every product gets real pros and cons listed, including the lower-ranked ones.

The cons should be honest and specific, helping the reader understand who each product fits and who it doesn't.

Back up your claims with:

Customer testimonials that reinforce the problem → solution journey

Before/after results, if applicable

Third-party validation (studies, expert endorsements)

Star ratings and review counts

Studies and research

Expert opinions

⚠️

Before building a comparison page from scratch, check if you already have one sitting unused. We see plenty of brands with comparison pages that converted well on Meta but was never pushed on Google.

To find those, go to Shopify Analytics → Sessions by Landing Page → filter to "custom" → extend the date range back 12 months.

## How to Run 3rd-Party Comparison Page on Google

### 1\. Comparison Keywords on Search

![](https://rhetorical-garage-a1e.notion.site/image/attachment%3Aa15e895b-0473-4006-a844-fdb69f2f9d86%3Aimage.png?table=block&id=396a60cf-8ed8-805c-93b7-e2bd35baac87&spaceId=d9fb0601-20b1-4779-a1c6-d68592cdd197&width=1310&userId=&cache=v2&imgBuildSrc=requestProxiedImageUrl)

Who you're reaching: People who already know what type of product they want. But they haven't decided which brand to buy from yet.

Target keyword types:

Best-of searches ("best \[product category\]," "top \[product\] 2026," "highest rated \[product\]")

Competitor searches ("\[competitor brand\] reviews," "\[competitor brand\] alternative")

Standard product category search ("collagen powder," "ergonomic desk")

The volume can be solid and the intent is strong.

It works especially well if you operate in a crowded category with recognizable brands.

⚠️

If you're using competitor names in ad copy or on the page itself, run the exact phrasing past ad policy first. Don’t use dynamic keyword insertion on competitor terms if that competitor owns the trademark. It can misfire and get the ad flagged.

### 2\. "Review" Image Ads on Discover

![](https://rhetorical-garage-a1e.notion.site/image/attachment%3A01231e3b-ea12-4e4f-a577-ec8348ceede4%3Aimage.png?table=block&id=396a60cf-8ed8-801e-bdad-d74332e32751&spaceId=d9fb0601-20b1-4779-a1c6-d68592cdd197&width=1600&userId=&cache=v2&imgBuildSrc=requestProxiedImageUrl)

In case you're not familiar with Discover, it's basically Google's content feed, where people scroll through articles and news.

It shows up when someone opens the Google app or lands on a new tab in Chrome mobile.

We've been getting great results with this placement. I'm seeing plenty of brands scaling this to $10k to $20k per day in spend.

Unlike Search, the users aren't actively looking for a product.

They're just going through content.

Your job is to catch their attention in the middle of that scroll.

Image: Gets people to pause the scroll and gets the first look

Headline: Builds curiosity around a ranking, comparison, or review

Description: Expands on the headline and creates more desire to click

A few angles you can run:

Best-of list ("Top 7 magnesium supplements in 2026.")

Expert recommendation ("Physical therapists are calling these 6 massagers a game-changer for mobility after 40.")

Consumer review ("We tested 10 popular skincare tools. Here's what actually works for women over 50.")

Image styles that perform:

Editorial product layouts: multiple products displayed together, clean flat lays, magazine-style photography

Lifestyle comparison images: product being used naturally, research or evaluation cues in the scene, multiple options visible

Side-by-side comparisons: two products shown together, clear visual hierarchy, a subtle hint that one option stands out

The description reinforces credibility and frames the content as a helpful recommendation.

Credibility: "We evaluated effectiveness, ease of use, and overall value across more than 500 options to create this list."

Research-based: "We put the category's most popular products side by side to see which ones are actually worth buying."

Curiosity expansion: "One option consistently outperformed products that cost twice as much."

Authority: "Recommendations based on expert opinions and thousands of customer reviews."

From what I see, very few brands are competing here because they don't even know Discover is a placement you can profit from.

#### 3\. "Editorial" Video Ads on YouTube

![](https://rhetorical-garage-a1e.notion.site/image/attachment%3A11e1ac2a-d6e2-46e9-8355-2f7b28b1a68f%3Aimage.png?table=block&id=396a60cf-8ed8-80df-905a-eface06c8c25&spaceId=d9fb0601-20b1-4779-a1c6-d68592cdd197&width=1500&userId=&cache=v2&imgBuildSrc=requestProxiedImageUrl)

The ad structure:

Open with the benefit: the outcome people want and the problem your product solves

Raise the stakes: explain why picking the right product or brand matters so much.

Educate: Show the audience what separates high-quality products from average ones (ingredients, formulas, sourcing, quality standards, etc.), and how those differences change the results people get.

Set the criteria: walk through what viewers should look for when choosing.

Deliver the payoff: Show how your product checks every box you just laid out.

You can do this with UGC, podcast-style, AI animation, etc.

Example: applying this to pet nail clippers

USP: a specific blade angle and grinding mechanism that avoids cutting into the dog's quick.

Benefit hook: the wrong nail clip can cause pain, bleeding, and long-term joint issues for the dog.

Stakes: a proper clipper saves money over time versus vet bills for fixing a cut quick, or addressing the behavioral issues that come from a dog that's learned to fear paw handling.

Education: cheap guillotine-style clippers crush the nail before cutting, which splits and damages the quick over time. Poorly designed clippers also cause dogs to develop a fear response, turning every trim into a two-person wrestling match.

Set the criteria: look for blade angle and grip mechanism, not just price or brand recognition.

Payoff: your clippers use that blade angle and grinding mechanism — so trims stay quick, painless, and stress-free for the dog.
