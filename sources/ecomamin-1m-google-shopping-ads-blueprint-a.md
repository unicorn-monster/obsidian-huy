Largely overlaps with [[ecomamin-shopping-masterclass]] and [[ecomamin-ai-shopping-ads-gemini-prompt]] (Part 6 of this doc IS the same 5-concept A-E creative framework as that standalone prompt doc) — notes below focus on what's genuinely additive.

## 4-pillar system (title/image/social-proof/pricing — mostly duplicate of existing wiki, kept for reference)
Lifestyle visuals sell outcomes not products (white-bg = "here's a product", lifestyle = "here's your life improved"). Social proof: real A/B data — **shopping ads WITH reviews shown convert 6.8% vs WITHOUT reviews 2.3% — a 3x difference from one trust signal alone.** Title formula `[primary keyword] [size] [material] [benefit] [feature] [positioning] [brand]`. Pricing: free-shipping-built-into-price beats price+shipping-shown even at identical total cost; anchor pricing (crossed-out MSRP + sale price + "you save $X") triggers anchoring + loss-aversion + urgency simultaneously.

## The 5-strategic-images-per-listing sequence (distinct from the 5-concept TEST framework)
Google Shopping allows up to 10 images per listing; most brands use 1-2. Author uploads **5 images per listing as a deliberate sequential psychological journey** (not 5 things to A/B test against each other — 5 things shown together in order): (1) compliance shot — pure white bg, gets you into the feed at all; (2) aspirational lifestyle — emotional connection/visualization; (3) feature deep-dive — rational justification for the price; (4) social-proof overlay — trust/bandwagon, answers "can I trust this brand"; (5) problem/solution split-screen — urgency, makes staying-with-the-problem feel worse than buying. Claimed mechanism: each image answers the next objection in the customer's internal dialogue as they scroll the listing, compounding toward purchase.

## Free-shipping-threshold formula
Set threshold **20-30% above current AOV** (e.g. AOV $60, avg shipping cost $8 → threshold $80 = 33% above AOV) — pushes customers to add items to reach free shipping, raising AOV while shipping cost as % of order falls.

## Full Google ecosystem funnel (TOF→MOF→BOF→retention) with budget split + measured lift
Not isolated Shopping — full funnel across Google properties: **YouTube (TOF, awareness, 20-30% of Google budget, broad interest targeting, video lifestyle/founder-story/testimonial content, metric = view rate)** → **Discovery ads (MOF, consideration, 15-20% budget, retarget YouTube viewers 25%+/channel visitors/site visitors-no-purchase with shopping-style images, metric = CTR)** → **Shopping ads (BOF, conversion, 40-50% budget, full 4-pillar system, high-intent category/problem/brand/comparison keywords, metric = ROAS)** → **Brand search (retention, 10-15% budget, defend brand name + misspellings, target 90%+ impression share on own brand terms)**. Claimed lift of full ecosystem vs Shopping-only: conversion rate 3.2%→5.7% (+78%), AOV $58→$71 (+22%), LTV $127→$203 (+60%), ROAS 2.8x→4.3x (+54%) — repeated exposure across touchpoints pre-warms the Shopping click.

## Optimization cadence (ops rhythm, novel structure)
Weekly (1-2h Monday): search-terms report → negatives + new keyword finds; impression-share analysis (budget-capped vs rank-capped diagnosis); 2 new image variations tested; competitor shopping-ad screenshot audit. Monthly (3-4h): title refresh for top-20% revenue products; creative refresh to prevent fatigue; review-collection push; pricing-psychology A/B (shipping threshold, anchor framing, bundles). Quarterly (full day): seasonal visual overhaul + photography planning; category expansion into adjacent high-ROAS/low-competition products; advanced feed attributes (custom labels, priority, condition); scale budget +20%/7 days on 60-day-proven winners with impression share <70%.
