System for intercepting a big competitor's brand-search traffic with a neutral-domain 1-vs-1 "[YourBrand] vs [BigCompetitor]" comparison page, disguised as independent third-party research — not brand-owned like a conquest listicle.

Reference examples cited throughout: getmademan.com competitor-comparison-table, evvy.com/blog/evvy-vs-seed, trysnow.com snow-vs-crest, helixsleep.com/pages/helix-vs-casper, sleep.me chilipad-vs-eight-sleep, shop.harrys.com comparison page, ekster.com ekster-vs-ridge, casper.com/pages/casper-vs-nectar, purple.com/mattress-reviews/competitors, vybey.co.uk/pages/vybey-vs-ag1.

## The economics thesis
Big brands spend $500k-2M/mo on awareness (podcasts, influencers, YouTube pre-roll) — they're paying to educate the market ("what is this, why do I need it, what should I pay"), which produces buyers who then search "[brand] alternative"/"[brand] vs"/"is [brand] worth it"/"[brand] review". A comparison page intercepts that already-educated search at $0.80-2.50/click instead of spending $40-80/customer educating cold traffic from zero. "The competitor spent $200k to create that customer, you spent $0.80 to steal them."

## Cold-traffic vs comparison-page unit economics (worked example)
Cold traffic to PDP: 10,000 clicks × $3 CPC = $30k spend, 1.8% CVR, 180 customers, $67 AOV → $12,060 revenue, **loses $17,940**. Same budget via comparison page: 10,000 clicks × $0.80 CPC = $8k spend, 60% read-through, 12% CVR, 720 customers → $48,240 revenue, **profits $40,240**. Broader benchmark: comparison-traffic converts 8-15% vs cold-traffic 1-3% (5-8x); CAC $25-45 vs $80-150 cold.

## 6 psychological triggers that drive 10-14% conversion
1. **Social proof transfer** — comparing yourself to an established brand implies you're legitimate/same-tier/confident enough to invite comparison; trust transfers from them to you.
2. **Loss aversion** — repeated framing of "you're paying $X, we're $Y, you're LOSING $Z/year" — humans react harder to money already being wasted than to a hypothetical future saving.
3. **Smart-shopper identity** — choosing the "researched" option lets the buyer feel intelligent/savvy/informed; the purchase becomes identity-affirmation ("I'm not dumb enough to overpay for marketing").
4. **Authority positioning** — "we analyzed/compared/tested" language positions the page as investigative research, not a sales pitch.
5. **Confirmation bias** — the page validates a suspicion buyers already have ("am I overpaying? is this just good marketing?") — confirming an existing belief converts harder than introducing a new one.
6. **Paradox-of-choice elimination** — instead of 47 options causing analysis paralysis, the page narrows the decision to a binary: competitor or us.

## Domain strategy — never use the brand domain
Brand-owned comparison URL (yourbrand.com/x-vs-y) reads as biased → ~40% trust drop, ~30% conversion drop. Buy a cheap ($12) neutral-sounding domain (e.g. supplementcomparisons.com, nutritioncomparison.io) that reads as an independent research site.

## 15-section page structure (highlights beyond what's already in [[presell-pages]]'s 9-block anatomy)
1-4: neutral investigative headline mentioning both brands + subhead promising objective analysis + byline with a named "independent researcher" persona + a 150-200-word neutral intro that validates market confusion and acknowledges the competitor's strength before promising an objective comparison.
5: **hero visual contrast as a subconscious anchor** — competitor shown as cold stock/clinical photography, your product shown as warm lifestyle/human-touch photography, BEFORE any copy is read. Floating "doubt anchor" callout bubbles on your product only ("3x more active ingredients", "50% lower price") — framed as information, functioning as doubt-injection.
6: **price comparison goes first** (not last) — muted competitor price box vs bold-colored your-price box vs a huge "annual savings" number, plus a "if you've used [competitor] for 2 years, you've overpaid $X" sunk-cost calculator to trigger loss aversion on money already spent, not money that could be saved.
7: **ingredient/spec comparison as "the kill shot"** — line-by-line red-X/green-check spec table with multiplier framing (2.7x more protein, 8.3x more probiotics) rather than raw numbers.
8: ingredient/feature deep-dive with visual density (photos of individual components) — "even if they don't know what half these mean, visual density = perceived value."
9: **problem-exposure section** — pick ONE real competitor flaw, cite an actual health/industry authority (e.g. American Heart Association sodium limit) with real numbers, explain the specific downstream risk, then show your product's number — genuine concern injection, not just a stat.
10: effectiveness comparison framed as competitor's vague marketing claims ("increased energy") vs your specific claims with a cited study/sample-size/timeframe.
11: "what you actually get" value-stack — bundle in free bonuses (guide, book, bottle) priced out individually so your $49 reads as $114 of value vs their $97-for-product-only.
12: **switcher testimonials that must explicitly name the competitor**, include a timeframe (proves sustained use, not novelty), mention the specific $ saved, and read like an unpolished real person (age/city, casual language) — testimonials that don't name the competitor don't do this job.
13: expert validation (real credentialed quote or real press logo only — do not fabricate).
14: summary comparison table — the brain-dead-simple final decision aid with checkmarks/stars.
15: soft CTA — not aggressive ("might be worth considering"), leads with the guarantee (removes remaining risk), restates the $ saved, cites total switcher count, gives logistics (ship time).

## Product page after the comparison page (they're already 85-90% sold)
Keep it simple: hero restates the vs-positioning in the headline itself ("[Brand]: the [Competitor] alternative that's 3x more comprehensive for 50% less"), price shown as crossed-out-competitor-price → your price, benefit-framed CTA ("start saving now" not "buy now"), trust badges immediately visible, bullet benefits each carrying the vs-competitor multiplier, photo-review social proof snapshot, simple 3-step usage, a condensed comparison-reminder table, FAQ that answers "how is this different" and "why are you cheaper" directly, final CTA reframing the decision as a binary option A/B choice.

## Ad campaign setup (Google Search / YouTube / Meta)
**Google Search — 2 campaigns:** (1) branded-competitor-search: exact/phrase on `[competitor]`, `[competitor] review`, `[competitor] alternative`, `is [competitor] worth it`, `[competitor] vs` — ad copy headline structure "[Competitor] vs [YourBrand]" / "we compared ingredients & price" / "see which delivers better value", display URL showing the comparison domain. Expected CPC $0.80-2.50, CTR 4-8%, CVR 10-14%. (2) problem-based-search: category terms ("best [category]", "[category] comparison", "cheapest [category] alternative").
**YouTube:** 15-30s UGC-style "I was paying $X for [competitor], found [YourBrand], same benefits half the price, saved $Y in Z months" script ending on a quick comparison-chart flash, CTA to the comparison page; targeting includes remarketing to visitors of the competitor's own website.
**Meta:** UGC video, same switcher-script structure, first-comment link (not in-post link) to the comparison page; lookalike audience built off the competitor's customer base.

## Search-volume qualification bar
Minimum 500 combined monthly searches across `[competitor] alternative/vs/review/worth it` before building a page for that competitor. Optimal 2,000-10,000/mo. Above 10k = likely too competitive for a new/small brand to win.

## Tracking benchmarks
Comparison-page engagement: 80%+ scroll to price section, 70%+ to ingredient comparison, 50%+ to bottom, 3+ min avg time on page. Funnel conversion: comparison-page→product-page 35-50%, product-page→purchase 20-30%, overall comparison-click→purchase 10-14%. Economics: CPC $0.80-2.50, CAC $25-45, ROAS 6x+ target.

## Unit economics to $1M/yr (worked model)
At $49 AOV, $14 COGS ($35 margin), $1.20 CPC, 11% CVR: needs 20,408 customers, 185,527 clicks, $222,632 ad spend → $714,280 gross profit, ~$491,648 net after ads. Monthly: ~$83k revenue / 1,701 customers / $18.5k spend / ~$41k profit.

## Expansion path once one comparison is profitable
Add more named-competitor comparison pages (5x the keyword surface on the same product) → build a "we compared the top 7 [category]" roundup page targeting non-branded "best X" volume, with your brand as the declared winner → vertical-integrate into adjacent product categories with the same playbook → spin up a YouTube channel dedicated to "unbiased" category comparisons to build an evergreen owned-content moat feeding the pages.

## Defensive moat once established
First-mover on the exact competitor-branded search terms is hard to displace once ranking/ad-position is owned. Credibility compounds (backlinks, reviews, domain authority) the longer the page runs. You accumulate a data-moat on which comparisons/objections/price-points convert, which a copycat starts without. You lock in category-ownership as "the [Competitor] alternative" in the buyer's mind.

## Mistakes to avoid
Being too aggressive/attacking (reads as a hit piece, kills the "objective research" frame — acknowledge competitor strengths first). Using your own brand domain (40% trust drop). Fabricating studies/testimonials/citations (one fake citation destroys all credibility). Ignoring/denying real competitor strengths. Weak guarantee (<60 days, restocking fees). Complicated checkout after they're already sold (no forced account creation, no pre-purchase upsells).

## Timeline + window
Realistic ramp: month 1-2 setup/testing ($8-15k/mo) → month 3-4 optimization ($25-40k) → month 5-6 expansion to more competitors ($50-70k) → month 7-9 systematic scale ($70-90k) → month 10-12 ($90-120k/mo, ~$1M annual run rate). Author's claimed window: comparison-page conquesting is not yet saturated, but expects big brands to start defending with their own comparison pages and CPCs to rise over the next 18-24 months — "still viable after that, but not insane."

## Core thesis
"Marketing beats product 9/10 times" — two identical products, one positioned as "[Competitor] alternative for half price" and one as "revolutionary new formula," the alternative-framed one does 10x the revenue. Positioning > product, always.
