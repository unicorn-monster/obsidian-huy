For those who are unfamiliar with advertorials, an advertorial is a form of pre-sell page.

A pre-sell page is a page you send cold traffic to before sending them to your product page.

The goal of the pre-sell page is to educate the visitor on their problem, the solution, & your product enough & agitate their pain enough for them to make a purchase.

It’s how you sell to cold traffic (unaware or problem aware), which in turn is how you scale since only targeting warm traffic (solution aware, product aware, most aware) means you’ll have too few people to sell to & there’s far more competition.

Pre-sell pages are absolutely essential in ecom.

**There are essentially 2 types of pre-sell pages you can run:**

Advertorials or listicles.

A listicle is a "X reasons why people love this product" style page, so something like "7 reasons 120,000 women are using \[product name\]."

It's super skimmable & each point is just a pain point or benefit tied to your mechanism, or a bit of proof.

I broke down how to write listicles in this article if you wanna have a look: [https://x.com/CarlWeische/status/2063577589126471955](https://x.com/CarlWeische/status/2063577589126471955)

An advertorial, which is the one we're covering today, is a page that looks like a normal article & goes deep on ONE specific angle & one specific pain point, usually told like a story.

**Which one you use depends on the ad people came from…**

Use listicles when your ad has a more generic angle, or when it's mainly there to introduce the product or the brand, or when you want a more versatile pre-sell page you can use for a larger variety of ads.

Use an advertorial when your ad is built around a specific angle & a specific pain point, because then the whole page can build on that & really push people over the edge.

**Writing good advertorials comes down to one idea:**

Selling is just creating separation between where someone is now & where they want to be, then positioning your product as the vehicle that gets them across.

So before you write anything, you need to get crystal clear on:

Where they, your customer avatar, are now (their pains, their problems, & what they currently believe)

Where they want to be (their goals & their desires)

& then the entire page is just you bridging that gap, over & over again.

We’ll soon cover exactly how to structure advertorials & what should be in them, but first, you need to understand this:

## The pain threshold

This is an idea from Tony Robbins & it completely changed how I go about marketing.

Pain is the strongest motivator humans have, but pain only motivates action when it reaches the point where it’s more painful than taking the action.

This is the pain threshold.

Think about how many people are seriously overweight & still won't start a diet. They obviously feel pain, they just haven't hit ENOUGH of it to cross the line that forces them to act.

Your prospects are exactly the same way.

You need to hit their pains & desires really really hard, again & again until you push them over that threshold, because only then do they get the motivation to buy.

So what you do is pick the 3-5 biggest pains & the 3-5 biggest desires your customer has, & then you hammer those same ones across the whole page in different ways.

Don’t be scared of repeating yourself with this.

Before we move onto how to write advertorials, I want to make this clear:

This is ONE way of writing advertorials. There are infinite ways to do so. There are zero rules. If I were to cover every single little nuance this article would be 5000-10000 words long.

Now that you understand the concepts behind writing advertorials, here’s exactly how to do so:

## 1\. The hook (the very top of the page, above the fold)

This is something you should really experiment with. Try pain-driven hooks, desire-driven hooks, try first-person POV hooks, A/B test like mad.

This is the single most important part of the whole page since if people don’t keep reading then nothing else matters.

## 2\. Educate them on WHY they have the problem & WHY they’re stuck

This is what let’s you:

**differentiate against competition:** by naming the REAL problem as something the prospect wasn’t aware of & something different than what your competitors are telling them is the problem. for example: if you’re selling a fitness supplement & your competitors are saying “the reason you can’t build muscle is because of your testosterone”, you might say it’s because of myostatin levels (if that’s what your product solves of course)

**sell to cold traffic:** since these people are not aware of their problem. they have a pain but don’t understand why they have it. & if they don’t understand the problem, they won’t understand how your solution will help them

So you walk them through WHAT the problem is, WHY they have the problem (the root cause), & WHY everything they've tried hasn't worked for them.

Now their past failures aren't their fault anymore, there was a hidden cause nobody ever told them about.

(a hidden cause your product solves)

Then you spell out the ripple effect, meaning what happens to them if they DON'T fix this.

Show how the problem bleeds into other parts of their life & gets worse.

& if possible try to tie this to the core human desires (reproduction, status, belonging, avoiding pain/safety), so imply how the problem will cause them to not get a partner for example (not the best example but you get the point).

This is what drives their pain towards that threshold.

By the end of this part they should be thinking "ok so THAT'S why nothing worked, & I really can't keep ignoring this anymore."

## 3\. Your unique mechanism & the origin story

Now you bring in your solution, & preferably you do it through the story of how it came to be.

What I mean by this is: don't just say "here's our product."

Tell them how it was built, who you consulted, what you researched, what was broken about everything that already existed, & how long it took you to finally get it right.

This just makes your solution feel more legit.

A product that took 2 years & a team of experts to perfect is WAY more credible than one that just shows up out of nowhere & goes “buy me”

Then you lay out the features of your product & tie every single feature to a benefit the customer wants (you can do this in a listicle style if you want).

## 4\. The proof story

Here you insert a long first-person story from one of your customers where they basically just talk about the transformation they went through & the emotions they felt.

& make sure to write it as a skeptic-to-believer arc with the customer starting off doubting if it’ll work, trying it anyway, & getting completely shocked by the result & how fast they got the result.

You write it this way for 2 reasons:

The skepticism makes it more believable. If the narrator of the story instantly loves the product the reader will call BS on the whole page.

& the "I saw results way faster than I expected" part hits their desire for a quick win, which almost every single buyer secretly wants.

Basically you let the story do all the bragging your brand isn't allowed to do itself.

BUT MAKE SURE THIS STORY ACTUALLY FEELS LIKE A REAL CUSTOMER STORY.

I see so many brands writing stories like this that obviously are just some sales person pretending to be a customer. You need this to feel real or you’ll lose all trust.

## 5\. Add a risk reversal & CTA

Now you just add some sort of guarantee to make the offer a no-brainer.

The whole point is to leave them with literally nothing to lose, so the only thing left for them to do is make the decision.

Then you have your first CTA, & for the button, pls don't write "buy now."

Write something like "check availability." It's softer & way less pushy, & it also slips in a bit of scarcity.

A good chunk of people are already sold here & they buy right away.

But you're still not done, because plenty of them will scroll past this first CTA, so you have the rest of the page to convert as many of those as possible.

The reason someone wouldn’t convert here is either because their pain threshold hasn’t been reached, or because they still don’t trust that you can deliver the result you promise.

So…

## 6\. Hit the pain threshold one more time

So for everyone who didn't buy at that first ask, you come back & slam the pain points again.

Just something simple like: "how much longer are you gonna struggle with X that's costing you Y?"

Also take a few lines to frame every other option they're considering (your competitors’ solutions) as things keeping them stuck.

You're essentially making every other path feel painful af so that yours becomes the only way out.

## 7\. Kill objections with social proof, then have another CTA

Write down the top 5 objections that are stopping people from buying, & then you pick or write one testimonial that directly crushes each one of them.

So if an objection a lot of people have is "I'm worried it won't work for \[specific situation\]," you find a testimonial from someone with that exact situation who got a great result.

Every review on the page should be handling a specific objection, never just sitting there saying "great product :)"

Then you drop another CTA right after it, because the people who needed that extra bit of proof are ready to buy now, & you don't want to make them scroll all the way back up to find the button.

But you’re still not done…

## 8\. The close

Some people that “should” convert will still not have bought by now.

So you lay out 2 options for them:

Option 1 is they leave this page & keep doing the exact same thing that's kept them stuck this whole time.

Option 2 is they do what everyone else who got the desirable result did, & try it completely risk-free today.

A really nice touch here is a line like "the fact you've read this far means part of you is ready for change," which is kinda an identity reframe.

Then you finish on some scarcity & urgency if you have it.

& then you have your final CTA.

That's all.
