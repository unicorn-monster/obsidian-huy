title: Good judgement plus AI scales, bad judgement burns spend

This guy is very smart.

He's choosing the hard path: learning to write manually and developing judgement of what good copy looks like.

Most people choose the easy path: run a deep research prompt, put it into claude with a format to mimic, and ask it to write a good ad, but that only gets you 40% of the way (and you'll burn a lot of money testing it).

To actually get ai to write a good performing ad you need to steer it and give detailed feedback.

You'll only be able to do that if you deeply understand your audience and what resonates with them, human psychology, and which viral formats work and how to adapt them.

That judgement is only built by doing it over and over again.

Bad judgement + ai = scaling waste
Good judgement + ai = scaling wins
