"I have many different products. What the hell do I even send emails about?"

I get asked this on almost every call with SKU-heavy brands.

Many think the answer is to segment everything, slice the list a hundred different ways, and send a perfectly custom email to each little group...

But that's a ton of work for barely any extra return.

When a brand has hundreds of products, I actually go broader, not narrower.

You should keep sending general campaigns to your engaged list, and feature a ton of products in every email instead of one or two.

Because for a brand with a big catalog, the way you get sales is by being top of mind.

Frequency beats relevance every time.

Would you rather send four campaigns a week to a million-person list with a more general style of emails... or would you want to send to small lists four times of a hundred thousand people but with a little bit more relevant emails?

The wide list outperforms every time.

Klaviyo's product feeds also will pull in recommended products dynamically and automatically hide anything below a minimum stock level, so you're never featuring something that's already sold out.

For flows, it's the same core setup as any brand as well...

Just split based on the item someone viewed or added to cart. The feed will pull in other products in that category.
