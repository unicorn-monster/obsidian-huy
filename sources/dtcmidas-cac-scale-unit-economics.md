title: CAC cope — great execution beats high-CAC ceiling

"You can only scale if you can afford $100+ cacs" is cope.

Great margins, high ltv, and being able to afford high cacs are all super important and make it way easier.

But that doesn't mean you can only scale if you can afford the highest cac. In fact, the majority won't be able to scale at $100+ cacs anyway because they simply don't have the cash to sustain it.

Luckily your cac isn't set in stone.

By dialing in your creatives and funnels you can drop your cacs significantly (even in super competitive markets).

I'm in a super competitive niche myself with a lot of players. I don't compete on price, actually my product is one of the most expensive in the market.

But because a lot of focus, time and resources from the very beginning went into getting better at marketing, that muscle was trained and my cacs are still lower than most of my competition. Even tho I could afford and sustain higher cacs than them.

That's the secret:

Great marketing execution + great unit economics is how you outscale all your competitors.

Because remember what Larry Ellison said:

"Winning is not enough, all others must lose"
