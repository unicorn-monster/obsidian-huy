title: Quiz questions that emotionally amplify the problem
---

Most quizzes let people off the hook too early.

They ask "what's your goal?" then jump straight to "here's the fix!"

They're not emotionally charged... so why would they buy anything?

Good quizzes do the opposite.

Every question makes the problem feel bigger, not smaller.

Weak: "How's your energy lately?"

Strong: "How many days this week did you drag yourself through on empty?"

Weak: "Want better sleep?"

Strong: "How long have you been waking up tired no matter how early you went to bed?"

Same topic. But each answer makes them feel it more.

By the last question, the problem feels huge.

That's the exact moment you show your offer — right when it finally feels urgent.

You're not selling a product.

You're catching them the second they can't ignore the problem anymore.

Simply by waiting to catch them at the end, when they're about to emotionally climax, and then explaining how you're about to fix them...

Is exactly what we did for this Skincare brand & scaled their quiz revenue by 81% in 40 days
