## Two layers of "3rd party" (the distinction)
1. **FB Page layer** (sender identity — the page the ad delivers FROM): authority / creator / niche-community / magazine page. Already captured: [[dtcmidas-3rd-party-pages-strategy]], [[personas]]. DTC Midas's canonical post is about THIS layer.
2. **Domain layer** (the landing/presell website itself): the listicle lives on a site whose **brand = an independent review publication** (`reviewscout.org`, `top5-choices.com`). The halo = "an objective third party tested these and ranked ours #1" — stronger than "brand says ours is best."
Strongest = **stack both**: magazine FB page → click → review-site domain. No step in the journey reveals brand ownership.

## reviewscout.org teardown (observed live)
**Site chrome** (publication persona): logo "Review Scout" + top nav `Latest Articles` / `Buying Guides`. Reads as a standalone review magazine, not a store.

**Editorial skeleton** (verbatim from the page's table-of-contents anchors, in order):
1. Guide To [category]
2. How [category] Work
3. [category] Benefits
4. What To Avoid
5. **What To Look For**  ← criteria-funnel
6. **The Best [category]**  ← ranking #1–#5

**Two decisive tricks:**
1. **Criteria-funnel** (the "What To Look For" section): the article defines buying criteria that the #1 product *conveniently* meets, so the reader self-qualifies BEFORE seeing the ranking. Verbatim:
   > "#1 Ashwagandha … We recommend looking for Ashwagandha KSM-66®, the ashwagandha root extract with the most extensive set of research … We also recommend looking for a formula that has Ashwagandha at least 250 mg per serving."
   = you don't argue "we're best," you **set the measuring stick** so you win.
2. **Borrowed authority**: superscript citation numbers (e.g. ²⁵⁻²⁷, ¹⁷) scattered through the copy pointing to "studies" → neutral, scientific register, not sales. Same third-person editorial voice the wiki assigns to the Institution archetype ([[personas]]) — but at the website level.

**Ranking**: #1–#5 list of options (here adaptogens), #1 = the funnel target.

## Compliance note
This is one of the highest-risk presell formats: FTC targets fake review sites + undisclosed endorsements; Google flags deceptive review pages. The "defensible" version is the domain-level analog of DTC Midas's "don't fake a doctor — partner a real one": **clear affiliate disclosure + criteria/citations that are actually defensible (no fabricated studies)**.
