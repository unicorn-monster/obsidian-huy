We have an apparel brand client doing $7.3M a month.

$1.9M of that comes from email alone.

And as I went through their entire email program, I noticed something...

The majority of it is just plain text emails!

Text-based emails are no secret, but many just run the same style over and over again...

Instead, here are three formats you should rotate through:

1. Short form, SMS-style.

Under 30 words. A couple sentences and a link.

Example: "Hey, we just restocked [product], last time it sold out in 48 hours, grab yours: [link]."

Great for restocks, flash sales, and anything where the brevity is the urgency.

2. Medium form.

Use a short personal intro, a tight body where you tell a story or make your case, and one clear CTA at the end.

Best for founder messages, personal recs, and education that feels intimate.

3. Long form.

Classic direct response. You're telling a story, building tension, writing each line to pull them into the next.

Hardest to write well, but when it lands it absolutely rips.

Use it for deeper storytelling and high-AOV products where you have to build the case.

Over time we keep slowly increasing our share of emails that are text based...
