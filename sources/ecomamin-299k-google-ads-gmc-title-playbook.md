Case study + 4-step process: took a feed-only PMax campaign from 9.63x to 18.97x ROAS (97% lift) on the SAME $32k/month spend, changing only product titles in Google Merchant Center — no new campaigns, no bid changes, no landing page changes.

## Why titles carry outsized weight
Google uses the title as the primary signal for which searches trigger a Shopping ad. A vague title ("Magnesium Supplement - 120 Capsules") gets matched to garbage-adjacent queries ("free magnesium samples", "magnesium for horses") because Google has no other context. A rich, specific title ("Magnesium Glycinate for Sleep and Anxiety - High Absorption 400mg - 120 Vegan Capsules") gets matched to high-intent buyers who already know what they're searching for.

## 4-step process
1. **Download search terms** — export last 90 days from the PMax campaign, filter out branded, minimum 5 impressions, sort by conversion rate descending. These converting non-branded terms are what belongs in titles.
2. **Categorize converting terms by intent** — benefit-focused ("magnesium for sleep"), type-focused ("magnesium glycinate"), quality-focused ("high absorption"), problem-focused ("can't fall asleep"). Rank by conversion rate; the highest-converters go earliest in the title because Google weights the **first 3 words** most heavily and that's what survives truncation on mobile.
3. **Rebuild titles on the formula** `[primary keyword for benefit] - [differentiator] - [specs]`. Example: "Magnesium Supplement - 120 Capsules" → "Magnesium Glycinate for Sleep and Anxiety - High Absorption 400mg - 120 Capsules Vegan." **Critical rule most people miss: the first 3 words must be non-branded.** Leading with the brand name gets the title categorized as branded by Google, and PMax will cannibalize the 15-25x-ROAS brand-search traffic into the Shopping line — reported blended ROAS looks like ~4x instead, tanking the account's apparent performance even though nothing about brand-search itself changed.
4. **Deploy via supplemental feed, not the primary feed** — build a Google Sheet with product ID + new title, upload to Merchant Center as a supplemental feed that overwrites titles without touching the source data. This makes testing and reverting trivial. Approval takes 24-48h; give the algorithm 2-4 weeks to re-learn matching patterns before judging results.

## Case numbers
Before: $32k/mo spend, $308k revenue, 9.63x ROAS, 4.13% CVR, 2,847 conversions. After (same spend): $607k revenue, 18.97x ROAS, 9.31% CVR, 6,521 conversions. Impressions actually dropped ~5% (specific titles stopped irrelevant matches), clicks rose modestly (better CTR), but conversion rate more than doubled because clickers were now actually looking for what's being sold — i.e. the lift is a pre-qualification effect, not a volume effect.

## Category-specific title-lead formulas
Supplements: ingredient type + benefit ("Magnesium Glycinate for Sleep", not "Magnesium Supplement"). Apparel: style + fit ("High Waist Yoga Pants for Tall Women", not "Yoga Pants"). Home goods: include dimensions ("Blackout Curtains 52x84 Thermal Insulated", not "Blackout Curtains"). Beauty: skin type + concern ("Vitamin C Serum for Oily Skin Dark Spots", not "Vitamin C Serum"). Pet: pet type + size ("Orthopedic Dog Bed for Large Dogs Arthritis", not "Dog Bed").
