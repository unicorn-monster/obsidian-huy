Walkfit is a 51-step quiz pulling in 8.1 million visitors per month. So what can we learn & not learn from them…

Here's the first MAJOR take-away: **if you have a walking offer… do not go rip them.** 51 steps is the result of hundreds of split tests and not something you should go do out the gate. With that being said, here are 3 major take-aways.

(Reference: https://walk-fit.io/onboarding)

## Onboarding Mentality
Seeing a quiz as an onboarding is such a powerful framework. See yourself as onboarding customers to your product. This is why app guys do so well → it is an onboarding. Now imagine onboarding them to our supplement, our knee brace, or even a fat-loss shirt.

![[walkfit-01.png]]

## Info Slides Sell & Shift Belief
Questions are layups for info. You get a series of yes, yes, yes — and now that they've admitted problems and made specific things relevant to the conversation, your job is to take these beliefs and shift them towards your offer. E.g.: "How much sleep do you get per night?" "Do you wake up rested?" Boom, info slide:

![[walkfit-02.png]]

(Screenshot verified: interstitial titled "Sleep is key for weight loss" — photo of a man stretching happily in bed. Body copy: "Restful nights support a healthy metabolism, regulate appetite, curb cravings, and boost energy. By supporting hormonal health and better digestion, **walking** can help promote **better sleep**." Small citation line below: `*Information sourced from "ScienceDaily"` — a micro-authority citation on a single interstitial slide, not just the result page. Note the bridge logic: the headline claim is about SLEEP [unrelated to walking], then the copy pivots to tie it back to the core mechanism [walking] — expanding perceived benefit surface into an adjacent problem space the user wasn't originally asked about.)

This helps shift belief.

## Give Real Value
Giving people real value in a quiz is all-around the best thing you can do. It helps you position the quiz ("Take the quiz to get XYZ"). It improves retention — they're waiting for their answers/outcome. It also creates trust: "We're going to email you your free personalised plan based on your answers."
