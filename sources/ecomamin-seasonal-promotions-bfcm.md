## why seasonal windows are so valuable and most brands waste them

during black friday, christmas, and other major promotional windows, two things happen simultaneously that create an extraordinary opportunity:

**buyer intent skyrockets.** the percentage of people searching product keywords who are ready to buy immediately spikes dramatically. "best magnesium supplement" in october is mostly research. "best magnesium supplement" in november is often gift-buying with credit card in hand. the same click buys at a dramatically higher rate.

**budget floors rise.** advertisers flood into auctions, CPCs increase, and the cost-per-click environment becomes more expensive. but because conversion rates are also higher, the economics can still be favorable for brands whose infrastructure is ready.

the brands that 10x during seasonal windows capture the intent spike before the CPCs fully inflate. the brands that don't are paying peak CPCs with the same infrastructure they run in october and wondering why ROAS compressed.

## 6-8 weeks before: the remarketing pool build

this is the most important step and the one most brands skip entirely.

the best customers to convert during a promotional window are the ones who already know your brand. they visited your site, maybe added to cart, maybe subscribed to your email, but haven't purchased yet. the remarketing audience.

six to eight weeks before your primary seasonal window, increase the budget on your TOF and demand gen campaigns by 30-50% with the explicit goal of building the remarketing pool, not direct ROAS.

this is counterintuitive. you're spending more at a time when you're not expecting immediate returns. but the math works dramatically in your favor. a buyer who saw your brand in october and searched for "best \[product\]" during black friday is 5-8x more likely to convert than a completely cold visitor who found you on the promotional day itself.

for a supplement client, we increased TOF spend from $8k/month to $12k/month in october, two months before the november promotional window. that increased remarketing pool growth by 40%. during november BFCM, the remarketing campaigns ran at 12-18x ROAS while the cold traffic campaigns ran at 4-5x. **the remarketing pool buildup was worth an estimated $85k in additional promotional revenue.**

the formula: every dollar you spend building the pool in the 6-8 week window earns $8-12 back during the promotional window itself through remarketing conversion.

## 4 weeks before: dedicated seasonal landing pages

generic product pages do not capture seasonal urgency. "buy our supplement" with a discount code in the banner is not a seasonal promotion. it's a product page with a sticker on it.

dedicated seasonal landing pages have four elements that generic pages don't:

**urgency infrastructure.** countdown timer to sale end. "X days until prices return to normal." this is not manipulation; it's accurately communicating that the promotional pricing is temporary. buyers who are on the fence convert when there's a genuine deadline.

**seasonal social proof.** aggregate your reviews from previous black friday or holiday periods specifically. "over 12,000 holiday gifts ordered last year" or "our most popular gift product." seasonal social proof is different from general social proof because it speaks to the buyer's specific context: they're shopping for gifts or for themselves during a period when everyone else is also shopping.

**bundle-forward presentation.** seasonal windows are when aov should increase, not decrease. a "holiday collection" bundle at $120 converts at a comparable rate to a single product at $45 during BFCM because the buyer's spending mindset is already activated. build bundles specifically for the seasonal window and feature them prominently.

**explicit gift-framing.** for christmas specifically: gift-ready packaging, delivery guarantee, gift notes option. these reduce purchase friction for the massive proportion of buyers who are buying for someone else rather than themselves. the jewelry brand's "arrives gift-ready in 24 hours" messaging drove disproportionate christmas conversion.

build these pages in october. let them index and load cleanly. don't build landing pages three days before the window opens.

## 2 weeks before: the target ROAS adjustment

this is the most technically specific part of the system and the one that determines whether you capture maximum impression share during the peak window.

two weeks before your primary promotional date, lower your target ROAS on all campaigns by 15-25%.

this sounds backwards. you're accepting lower efficiency on purpose. but here's the logic: your actual ROAS during the promotional window will be higher than normal because conversion rates are higher. if you keep your normal ROAS targets, google's bidding algorithm will be too conservative and you'll lose impression share to competitors who are willing to bid more aggressively.

by pre-adjusting targets downward, you're telling the algorithm to spend more aggressively to win auctions. when those auctions convert at holiday rates, your effective ROAS comes in above your target anyway.

the jewelry brand: normal target ROAS 5x. two weeks before christmas, we lowered to 3.5x. actual december ROAS: 7.2x. the relaxed target allowed the algorithm to win more auctions, more impressions went to buyers in gift-purchasing mode, and the conversion rate spike made the economics work beautifully.

reset target ROAS back to normal on January 2nd.

## the BFCM-specific timeline

**week 1 of november:** launch pre-black-friday email capture campaign with early access offer ("join the list for 24-hour early access to our biggest sale"). this builds a buyer list that you can hit on thanksgiving rather than black friday, ahead of the main auction surge.

**thanksgiving (US):** launch the sale to email list. 24 hours before public launch. these are your warmest buyers. this is also when remarketing campaigns from the pool you built in october should increase budget 2x. you're catching decision-makers before friday's CPC spike.

**black friday:** launch to everyone. increase shopping and search budgets 2-3x. the promotional landing pages are live. impression share targets are set to maximize. run hourly budget pacing checks rather than daily.

**saturday-sunday:** maintain budgets. resist the urge to cut spend on saturday when Friday's numbers look great. the weekend converts differently (more mobile, more impulse) but still converts.

**cyber monday:** typically has different buyer profile (more considered, larger baskets, more subscriptions). adjust toward subscription offers and higher-aov bundles specifically on monday.

**december 1-18:** transition messaging from "sale is over" to "gifts in time for christmas." delivery deadline urgency replaces discount urgency. the message changes; the infrastructure stays the same.

**december 19-22:** free shipping cutoff urgency. "last day for standard delivery before christmas" is one of the highest-converting urgency messages in ecom. countdown timer to shipping cutoff on all landing pages.

## the year-round seasonal calendar

bfcm and christmas get the most attention but they're not the only windows. here's every major seasonal opportunity in ecom google ads and the prep lead time for each:

**january (new year's resolutions):** supplements, fitness equipment, wellness products, and productivity tools all spike in the first 2 weeks of january. prep: mid-november remarketing build, new year's specific landing pages live december 15th.

**february (valentine's day):** jewelry, skincare, chocolates, experiences. prep: start remarketing build january 1st. "gift for her/him" landing pages live january 25th. urgency: "last day for delivery before february 14th" is extremely high-converting.

**may (mother's day):** one of the highest-gift-spending windows of the year. jewelry, skincare, wellness, home goods. prep: start april 1st. gift-bundled product configurations featured prominently.

**october-november (pre-bfcm):** this is the most valuable prep window. anyone who visited your site between october 1 and november 20 is an extremely warm remarketing target for bfcm. this entire period should be treated as remarketing pool building.

**december 1-22:** the full christmas window, not just the week before. buyers start searching "christmas gifts" in late november. the brands live with christmas gift messaging on december 1st capture 3-4 weeks of intent vs the brands that launch christmas campaigns on december 15th.

**december 26 - january 2:** post-christmas. buyers who received cash or gift cards, returns and exchanges driving product page traffic, "treat yourself" psychology is at its peak. most brands go dark. the ones that run aggressive campaigns in this window often see the best ROAS of the year because competition drops dramatically while buyer intent stays high.

build the year-round seasonal calendar in january. assign prep timelines to each window. build the infrastructure once. reuse and iterate.
