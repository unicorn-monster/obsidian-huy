## The Strategy, Step-by-Step

#### Step 1: Use Demand Gen, Not Legacy Video

YouTube Ads now run best inside Demand Gen campaigns — not the legacy “Video” campaign type.

Demand Gen lets us:

Custom thumbnails

In-feed and short form placements that actually convert

Stronger device control

Native CTA overlays

More stable conversion optimization

Set campaign subtype to:

Demand Gen (Video Focused)

Goal: Conversions

Bidding: Maximize Conversions (or tCPA once stable)

#### Step 2: Clean Placement Strategy

Weak placements destroy ROAS. Your job is to eliminate the bad inventory upfront.

Here’s how we fix that:

Exclude the following:

TURN OFF OPTIMIZED TARGETING (waste of budget unless you’re already spending over $5-$10k/day)

Mobile App Placements (via device exclusions AND placement filters)

YouTube Kids Channels (use managed placement exclusions)

Discovery Feed, Gmail, and Display Partners (in campaign settings)

Low-quality foreign geos (watch CPMs + conversion density)

Keep only:

YouTube Videos (in-stream, in-feed, and shorts)

We build a custom placement exclusion list of 2,000+ junk channels and update it monthly.

#### Step 3: Smart Audience Targeting

We don’t trust Google’s “optimization.” We engineer the audience ourselves.

Target using:

In-Market Audiences (narrow, purchase-driven categories)

“Meal Prep Kits”, “Home Workout Equipment”, etc.

Competitor URLs (insert into Custom Segment builder)

Sends ads to users who've visited similar product sites

Avoid:

Auto audience expansion

Affinity audiences (too broad for TOF acquisition)

Each campaign gets 1 audience group max for clean signal learning.

#### Step 4: Creative Strategy That Feeds the Algorithm

Forget 15-second polished brand spots.

YouTube rewards ads that:

Hold attention beyond 30s

Drive high view-through rates

Our top-performing format:

Hook (0–5s): Call out the common mistake

Problem Intro (5–15s): Show how your category fails

Product Reveal (15–40s): Demo + explain

CTA (60–90s): Call to action with offer or urgency

Total length: 60–90 seconds

Goal: 100% view time → triggers lower CPMs + better conversions

We rotate creatives every 7–10 days based on:

Drop in VTR (25-100%)

Rising CPM

Drop in CTR or Conv. Rate

#### Step 5: Optimization Benchmarks & Scaling Rules

Once live, here’s how we evaluate performance:

| Metric | Benchmark |
| --- | --- |
| CPM | $15–$35 |
| 100% VTR | 10%+ |
| CTR | 0.6%+ |
| Cost per Click | <$2.5 |
| CAC (Cold) | Within 1.2–1.5x blended AOV |

If CPMs spike:

Check for fatigue or poor hook

Audit placements

Refresh thumbnail or overlay CTA

If CAC spikes:

Re-segment audiences

Replace creative

Add first-party data exclusions to refine delivery

#### Step 6: Retargeting Layer (Optional, But Structured)

We run separate Demand Gen campaigns for retargeting.

Target:

YouTube viewers (past 30 days)

Site visitors (via GA4 or Enhanced Conversions)

Add-to-carts without purchase

Creative:

30–45s direct offer pitch

Less education, more urgency

This increases frequency without muddying the cold data or skewing CAC tracking.

## New Bonus: Presell Landing Pages

Presell pages can double the scale of a strong YouTube system. They act as warm-up funnels for cold prospects before showing the true offer. When built correctly, they smooth conversion friction, lower CAC, and stabilize scaling past the usual limits.

#### When to use presells

When the product requires education

When the AOV is above 50 dollars

When native-style storytelling improves clarity

When cold CAC is stuck but on-site conversion rate is acceptable

#### How to integrate presells into YouTube campaigns

All cold traffic goes from the Demand Gen campaign to the presell page.

The presell sends users to your actual product page.

Retargeting runs on people who hit the presell but don’t buy.

#### Best practices

Use a strong problem-led headline

Include long-form copy with educational tone

Add true product demo images

Use preview-style CTAs

Keep load speed fast

Add multiple buttons through the page

#### Why presells expand scale

Lower cognitive load on cold users

Creates “buying momentum” before the real offer

Gives Google a wider conversion surface

Stabilizes volatile CAC when scaling spend

Allows YouTube traffic to behave like paid advertorial traffic

At Vysta, we often see a 35-100 percent lift in scale when presells are used correctly.

## New Bonus: Budgeting, Ad Group Structure, and Targeting Sequence

Here are the structural rules that make optimization easier and unlock scaling:

#### Budget

Minimum 100 dollars per day

200 dollars per day preferred for steady data flow

Avoid launching below these thresholds

#### Ad Group Structure

One ad group per campaign

Five creatives in each ad group

Use the same thumbnail format across creatives for consistency

#### Targeting Sequence

Start with:

Broad targeting (narrow gender optional)

If this does not stabilize by day 7 to 10:

Add Lookalike Broad

Add Lookalike Narrow

Test In-Market

Test Custom Keyword Segments

This sequence ensures the algorithm starts with maximum freedom, then you refine from strongest signals to more controlled intent layers.
