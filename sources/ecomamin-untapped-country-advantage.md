THE UNTAPPED COUNTRY ADVANTAGE — how to escape US competition & print cash in smaller markets. The exact strategy used to turn $57k into $1.2M.

## WHY LISTEN

Scaled own pet store to $955k in 1 year with google ads only. Scaled dozens of client brands ($10M+ in client results). Scaled a jewelry brand to $1.1M/mo with 2 friends (50%+ from google).

## THE MISTAKE EVERYONE MAKES

Everyone obsesses over the US: CPCs $1.20+/click, 1-2% conversion, 10,000+ competitors, burn $200k just to hit 2x ROAS. Same product sold in Australia instead: $57k spend → $1.2M revenue → 21x ROAS, basically zero competition. "Stopped fighting in oceans, started dominating ponds."

## WHY "SMALL" COUNTRIES PRINT MONEY

US: 330M people, $1.20/click, 10k+ competitors, 1.2% CVR, ~2x ROAS. Australia: 26M people, $0.30/click, 50 competitors, 4.8% CVR, 21x ROAS — 4x cheaper clicks, 200x less competition, 4x higher conversion, 10x better returns. Other markets: UK 12x avg ROAS, Canada 15x, Norway 16x, New Zealand 18x. Combined "small" markets = 200M+ wealthy people, higher GDP/capita than US, ~10% of US competition levels.

## THE 5-STEP COUNTRY SELECTION SYSTEM

**Step 1 — identify target countries:** high GDP/capita ($40k+ USD — google "[country] GDP per capita 2024"), English-speaking (or your language, no translation/cultural barrier), low ecom penetration, good logistics. Candidates: Australia, Norway, Switzerland, Canada, UK, New Zealand, Singapore.

**Step 2 — validate demand:** Google Keyword Planner, select target country, search main product keywords, look for 1,000+ monthly searches. Example: US "dog collar" 450k searches vs Australia 33k — 10x less volume but 200x less competition, you'll actually win those searches. Bonus check: search product on google.com.au — weak competitors/bad images/generic descriptions/no sponsored ads = you found gold.

**Step 3 — calculate opportunity score:** `(search volume / competition) × GDP per capita`. US: (450k/10k)×76k = 3.4M. Australia: (33k/50)×64k = 42.2M — Australia wins by 12x. This is why "smaller" markets print money.

**Step 4 — test with $1,000:** Week 1-2 setup, Google Ads account in target country, basic Shopping campaign, $20-30/day for 2 weeks. Watch: CPC should be 50-75% lower than US, CTR 2-3%+, conversion 2-4%+, ROAS 4x+ minimum. Hit these → found your market. Don't → test next country. Total investment $1,000 to find a goldmine.

**Step 5 — scale with confidence:** Month 1-2 $50/day Shopping+Brand, collect data, optimize feed. Month 3-4 scale $100-150/day, add Exact Match Search, negative keywords. Month 5-6 scale $200-300/day, launch PMax, expand catalog. Month 7-12 maintain 8-15x ROAS, then move to next untapped country. By month 12: $1M+ across 2-3 small countries with less stress than the US.

## REAL RESULTS

Client 1 (pet products, UK): $43k spend → $516k revenue, 12x ROAS. Client 2 (health supplements, Canada): $67k → $1M, 15x ROAS. Client 3 (home decor, Australia): $52k → $885k, 17x ROAS. Author's own brand (pet accessories, UK): bootstrapped $0 → £955k ($1.2M USD) year 1, ROAS started 8x scaled to 15x+.

## BIGGEST MISTAKES

1. Thinking you need the US first — prove product in an easier market first. 2. Spreading too thin — dominate ONE market completely before the next. 3. Not localizing — "maths" not "math" (AU), "mobile" not "cell phone" (UK), "colour" not "color" (CA) — small tweaks = big conversion lifts. 4. Using US-based agencies — they apply US strategies that don't transfer; hire local or learn it yourself. 5. Giving up after one test — if Australia doesn't work, try Canada, then UK; one of these will work.

## NEXT STEPS

Pick top-3 countries (GDP $40k+, English/your language, product fit) → validate demand via Keyword Planner (1k+ monthly searches) → check competition (google product keywords in-country, <100 strong competitors = good) → test with $1k for 2 weeks (CPC/CTR/CVR/ROAS) → scale the winner.
