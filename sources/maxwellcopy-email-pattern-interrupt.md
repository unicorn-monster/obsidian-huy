HOW TO STAND OUT IN AN INBOX WHERE EVERY EMAIL LOOKS THE SAME

Step 1: Choose one idea
Pick a single message and cut everything that isn't it
Rule: No stacked sections, or too many products at once. One email, one point.

Step 2: Mimic something familiar from their phone
An incoming call, an AirDrop, a notes app screenshot
Rule: It should stop feeling like an email and start feeling like a notification they actually check.

Step 3: Match the subject line to the visual
"Incoming call" opens to a slide-to-answer screen. "Incoming AirDrop" opens to accept or decline
Rule: Stand out twice. Subject line gets the open, and the actual visual of the email holds their attention.

Step 4: Point to one action
Just a hero section and a single button
Rule: No wall of copy. Just "here's what's happening, and click here."

Step 5: Use them sparingly
Mix them in, don't make every send one
Rule: The whole power is the novelty. Every email a pattern interrupt and it stops interrupting anything.

Most brands send the same branded layout every single time.

We drop in something that looks like unique and no BS, and it becomes one of the best performers of the month.
