title: Too expensive objection means value too low
---

The "it's too expensive" objection is a LIE.

Whether it's someone cancelling their subscription in your ecom brand or you are an agency on a sales call, "it's too expensive" isn't what it actually means.

It just means they didn't see or get the value they thought they signed up for.

You could call a $50,000 Toyota Camry expensive. But if that same $50,000 got you a Lamborghini, suddenly it's a steal, and most people would figure out how to find the money for it.

When you hear "it's too expensive," that's not really feedback on your price… it's feedback on the value.

> You didn't make the outcome feel worth the price.
> You didn't show them the ROI they'd actually get.
> You didn't dig into what they're really after.

I've stopped letting brands (and myself) hide behind "it's too expensive," because it covers up the real question underneath.

Was it the price they had a problem with? or was the value too low in their head?
