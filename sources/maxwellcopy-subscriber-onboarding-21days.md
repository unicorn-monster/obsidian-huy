"This is the first subscription I actually didn't cancel."

^ a customer told one of our brands this, and it came down to one thing:

The onboarding when they subscribe.

The hardest part about onboarding a subscriber is getting them to not churn. That's why the 30 day mark is brutal for so many brands.

Here's how i think you should onboard a new subscriber, across two phases in the first 21 days:

Phase 1, before the product arrives...

They've bought but don't have it yet, so they're still a little skeptical, but excited. Your job is to make them feel good about the decision by:

- Confirming they made the right call
- Killing any skepticism with testimonials, social proof, and founder credibility
- Pre-framing the timeline so they commit to 90 days+ before expecting results

Send daily to every other day emails here for 4-10 days.

Phase 2, once it's in their hands...

Now the job flips. You want them using it, building the habit, and feeling like they belong by:

- Showing them how to actually use it
- Making it a habit by showing them how to pair it with something they already do (morning coffee, brushing teeth, etc)
- Reminding them they're now part of the community behind the brand

Again, daily to every other day emails here for 4-10 days.

And layering SMS in for the high-impact moments, the delivery day education, a day 3 check-in, and a day 7 nudge to recommit before the first rebill hits.

This is the #1 driver of positive customer experiences with subscription... make sure you do it right.
