I got the OG of email marketing himself, @ecomchasedimond, on the pod.

Here are 10 lessons I learned from a guy who's done over $250M in ecom email marketing:

1/ Welcome flows should be 4 to 6 emails, not 12. Past that they are pointless.

2/ Stop opening with "welcome to the family." Nobody's family with a brand they found 30 seconds ago. Lead with your core points.

3/ Don't completely rewrite a weak welcome flow. Take your best non-discount campaigns, add the welcome code, slot it in.

4/ AI's best use in retention isn't just writing emails. It's mining your reviews and support tickets for the exact words customers use.

5/ Split abandonment flows by purchase history or cart value. A small cart needs a reminder. A big cart might need a discount, so protect your margin.

6/ Time replenishment emails to when people actually rebuy, not day 30 because it's round. Most brands are guessing.

7/ Send review requests too early and they're useless. Wait until the product's been received and used.

8/ The highest lever in a welcome flow is the offer. Copy tweaks won't double conversions. Testing percent-off vs dollar-off vs free gift can.

9/ Open rates are over-inflated now that Apple inflates them. Revenue per recipient tells you if a send actually worked.

10/ The agencies that survive AI won't be the cheapest. They'll be so deep in the business they're a fractional retention team.
