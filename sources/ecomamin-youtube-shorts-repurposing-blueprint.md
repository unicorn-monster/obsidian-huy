System for repurposing proven Meta-ad winners into YouTube Shorts ads — the "cheap blue-ocean channel" pitch, but with real platform-mechanics detail on why a naive 9:16 crop underperforms and what actually needs to change.

## Why the opportunity is claimed to be real (author's framing, not independently verified)
Shorts CPMs ~$8-20 vs Meta ~$40-80. Audience skews older/higher-income than TikTok — 25-45-year-olds with real purchasing power, already in a "content consumption" mindset (vs Meta's "social connection" mindset) which the author argues makes them more receptive to a video ad functioning as content rather than interruption. YouTube's 15+ year history of training users to click links from video is cited as why the view-to-purchase path is short. Competitive bar is currently low — most advertisers just run their existing Meta creative unadapted. Side-effect: every Shorts impression also feeds regular YouTube (subscribers, organic distribution, pixel/audience data), so direct-conversion numbers understate total value.

## Why a naive crop-to-9:16 repurpose underperforms — the real platform differences
**Attention context:** Meta = passive scroll, ad is an interruption → rewards pattern-interrupt/curiosity-gap hooks that feel organic. Shorts = actively opened video app, viewer already leaning in → more forgiving of a slower build IF the first instant signals something worth staying for; in "discovery mode" not "social mode."
**Sound:** Meta 60-85% sound-off (captions carry the message, audio is accessory). Shorts is overwhelmingly sound-ON (YouTube trained as an audio-visual platform for 15+ years) — audio is the primary channel, not a fallback. Practical implication: Meta creatives where the voiceover is basically redundant to on-screen captions are the HARDEST to repurpose (need a rebuilt audio layer from scratch); creatives where a compelling voiceover carries the story are the EASIEST.
**Hook window:** Meta ≈1.5-2s on cold feed. Shorts ≈0.5s before a swipe — the first frame alone has to create a visual question, show something unexpected, or open mid-sentence, while still reading as relevant to something the viewer cares about.
**Algorithm logic:** Meta optimizes for engagement-probability targeting (gets pricier as competition increases). Shorts distribution is heavily driven by **watch-completion rate** — a hook that gets the swipe-stop but a body that doesn't hold attention throughs gets throttled distribution, unlike Meta where a strong hook can carry a longer, more front-loaded-value ad regardless of the back half. Comment engagement is also a distribution signal Shorts weighs more heavily than Meta does.

## Which Meta creatives to prioritize repurposing
Baseline filter: only repurpose creatives with ≥4x ROAS sustained ≥30 days (30-day requirement specifically to exclude lucky-short-run creatives from a bad sample). Format-compatibility check: length (15-45s ideal; a 90s VSL needs a real re-edit, not just a trim, so lower priority), visual-story structure (text-card-heavy 4:5 content gets cramped in 9:16; naturally visual content translates better), hook structure (a hook that's already fairly direct/outcome-focused is easy to tighten to 0.5s; a slow-build hook usually isn't salvageable). Compatibility hierarchy, most→least repurposable: UGC talking-to-camera testimonials (already native-feeling, voiceover baked in) → before/after transformation structure (visually transferable in any format, "before" frame IS the hook) → problem-solution narratives where the problem is visually demonstrable (works for "back pain at a desk," doesn't work for "feeling unfulfilled") → talking-head founder/expert content (needs hook rework since these usually open with context-setting, which is death on Shorts) → product-demo content for visually-compelling product functions. Don't bother repurposing: pure brand-story/emotional-build ads (format demands every second pay off commercially), heavy-text-overlay ads (edge text falls into the 9:16 dead zone), music-driven ads with no strong voiceover/visual hook (the 0.5s hook has to be entirely visual and music-mood-building doesn't front-load that).

## The hook rewrite (the single highest-leverage step)
Method: take the Meta hook, extract the core tension/outcome it was building toward, express THAT in under 5 words, immediately, with zero setup. Examples given: Meta "if you're tired of being tired no matter how much sleep you get..." → Shorts "8 hours of sleep. Still exhausted. This is why." Meta "we've all been told the key to clear skin is washing your face twice a day..." → Shorts "Washing your face is making your acne worse." Meta "as someone who struggled with their weight for years before finding something that actually worked..." → Shorts "Lost 31 pounds. Tried nothing new. Changed one thing." The sequencing flips: Meta builds TOWARD the hook moment; Shorts starts AT the hook moment and lets the rest of the video supply the context Meta would've front-loaded.

**4 hook categories that consistently work:** specific-outcome hook (concrete quantified result — "this routine cut my skincare routine from 45 minutes to 8" — specificity signals truth, vague benefit language doesn't). Pattern-interrupt visual hook (unusual angle/surprising juxtaposition/before-after split/something that seems to contradict a belief — a deliberate choice of first frame, not whatever the Meta cut happened to open on). In-progress hook (starts mid-demonstration without explaining what's happening — exploits the brain's drive to complete a partial pattern). Controversial/contrarian statement hook ("melatonin makes insomnia worse" — pulls in BOTH agreers seeking validation and disagreers wanting to argue).

## Production process
**Reframe ≠ crop.** Use AI-assisted reframe tools (CapCut smart reframe, Premiere auto-reframe, Resolve reframe) to subject-track for talking-head content; for product/lifestyle shots with no moving subject, manually decide the visual anchor at each moment and check nothing critical falls in the 9:16 dead zone. Treat the new top/bottom real estate as usable space (bottom 15-20% is natural CTA/text-callout territory) rather than an artifact to ignore.
**Audio:** if a usable voiceover exists, isolate and QA it standalone — does it read naturally without the visual captions doing half the work? If not, record one: write the script, read it aloud repeatedly until it stops sounding like ad copy (author: "the first read almost always sounds like copy, the fifth usually sounds like a person"). Music should be more present/active than a typical Meta ad (sound-on environment) and should do real emotional work (tension→resolution arc for problem-solution content, build arc for transformation content) rather than a flat loop. Light sound-design cues (a "pop" on product reveal, a "swoosh" on transitions, a "ding" on a key on-screen claim) are called out as underused polish.
**Captions:** still needed even on a sound-on platform, but doing a different job — not substituting for audio (Meta's role) but reinforcing it via selective-emphasis captions on the 3-4 most important phrases in larger text, rather than full-sentence captions tracking the whole voiceover.

## Campaign structure
**Testing budget:** $15-20/creative/day (below this, insufficient volume for meaningful completion/CTR/CVR signal in a reasonable window) — launch 3-5 repurposed creatives simultaneously at equal budget for 7-10 days to find which DIRECTIONS are viable (not yet picking a single winner), then double budget on the 1-2 promising ones for another 7-10 days to validate at higher spend, then scale strong performers to $50-100/day.
**Targeting by phase:** early testing = specific-interest targeting matched to category (e.g. "sleep health"/"wellness"/"anxiety relief" for a magnesium supplement) to isolate creative-intrinsic performance from targeting noise. Once validated: layer custom/remarketing audiences from Shorts viewers by watch-%, lookalikes off those, and combine with the Meta pixel for cross-platform remarketing sequences — the specific claimed mechanism is Shorts does the awareness touch cheaply, then a Meta retargeting ad closes the sale at a fraction of cold-Meta CAC, lowering blended CAC across both channels. For scaling: custom-intent audiences built from relevant YouTube SEARCH terms (people actively searching "magnesium for sleep" on YouTube are compared to Google-search-quality intent, still cheap because Shorts targeting sophistication lags Search).
**Bidding:** start Maximize Conversions (needs flexibility to find converters early) → after 30-50 conversions in a campaign, switch to Target ROAS set at ~80% of the actual ROAS achieved during the Maximize-Conversions phase, tightening gradually as the algorithm's model strengthens. Dayparting: Shorts consumption peaks evenings/weekends unlike Search's flatter intent curve — test 24/7 delivery first, only daypart if the data shows a clear differential worth the reach tradeoff.

## Advanced repurposing techniques
**Multi-angle extraction:** one strong 30s UGC testimonial can yield 3-4 independent Shorts without new footage — a problem-hook cut (just the problem description, ending on an open loop), a result-reveal cut (starts at the outcome, works backward), a specific-moment cut (isolates one uniquely compelling detail, e.g. "I tried seven other things first..."), and a direct-product cut (strips the testimonial frame entirely for a narrated product demo, which can outperform for audiences who prefer straightforward info over social-proof framing).
**Series/sequencing:** a 3-part series (problem → product/mechanism → result/social-proof) each ending on an open loop is claimed to get algorithmic benefit because Shorts rewards repeat-channel-watch behavior; multi-part watchers show 3x the engagement of single-short viewers at low incremental production cost since parts can be extracted from one longer source asset.
**Comment seeding:** comment volume is a distribution signal — respond to early comments conversationally to keep the thread active, consider a pinned comment with key product info or an explicit question, and end videos with a question specifically designed to solicit a comment ("has anyone else had this experience?").
**UGC amplification loop:** ask customers who leave strong reviews to record a short-form video using the SAME hook framework/structure taught above (give them the format, not just "make a testimonial") — claimed output of 10-15 new Shorts-optimized creative assets/month from the customer base at minimal cost, combining authentic social proof with Shorts-specific hook/pacing optimization.

## Economics by brand scale (author's stated ranges — treat as directional claims)
$500k-1M/yr Meta brand (~$50k/mo spend, 4x ROAS): $5-8k/mo Shorts test budget → realistic $200-350k/yr incremental revenue if maintained 12 months. $2-5M/yr Meta brand (~$75k/mo spend): $15-25k/mo Shorts budget → $400-800k/yr incremental (cited example: $42k/mo Meta + $8k/mo Shorts → +$52k revenue, ~31% lift). $5M+/yr brand: $30-60k/mo dedicated Shorts budget, target 20-30% incremental lift ($1-1.5M+/yr) — treated as a real channel with dedicated creative/attribution, not a side experiment; brand-building/remarketing-audience effects at this scale are argued to exceed what direct conversion numbers alone show.

## Stated benchmark ranges
Shorts ROAS typically 20-40% better than Meta after a 60-90 day learning period (primarily a CPM effect, not a conversion-rate effect — matched-audience CVR is argued to be similar cross-platform). Completion rate 35-55% on well-optimized Shorts. CTR 0.8-1.5% (slightly lower than Meta's 1-2%) but click→purchase conversion tends higher due to stronger intent signal among clickers.

## Window / timing claim
CPMs reportedly rose ~15-25% over the prior 12 months as more advertisers discover the channel; author frames current entrants as building a durable creative/algorithm/audience head start analogous to brands that built deep Meta expertise 2017-2020, with an explicit but soft framing that later entrants "pay more and compete harder for the same result," not that the opportunity disappears.

## Implementation roadmap (weeks, not months)
Week 1: filter Meta library for ≥4x-ROAS/30-day-sustained winners, pick top 10, assess format-compatibility, narrow to 5, write hook rewrites. Week 2: production (reframe, audio, captions, end-screen) for all 5, ~30-45min/creative once practiced. Week 3: launch 5 ad groups at $15-20/day, set up conversion tracking properly, no changes for 7 days (let the algorithm learn). Week 4: assess completion/CTR/CVR, double budget on top 1-2, start comment-seeding, begin multi-angle extraction from the best performer. Week 5+: continuous 2-week creative-test cadence, scale winners, build the UGC loop, begin series content once there's enough audience data.
