how to become a marketing engineer the role is changing, people call it marketing engineer, gtm engineer, growth engineer, same idea. instead of relying on tools that are prompted one chat message at a time, we are moving to building custom agents and orchestration I made this shift over the last year, here's the path: 1. build, don't just use right now you open a chat and ask for one output. the move is building the thing that produces that output on its own, again and again 2. build your company brain first this is the moat, a centralized place for everything you and your company know: > transcripts and call notes > previous campaigns and what they returned > client learnings and strategy docs > brand rules, positioning, ICP, offers > internal workflows and examples of what good looks like every agent reads this before it works. aim an agent at good inputs and weeks of work turn into hours. point it at a mess and it just makes slop, faster 3. learn agent profiles a profile is a dedicated agent with its own config, skills, memory and model. build open ones that need you in the loop for research and one-offs, and closed ones that own an outcome end to end and run on their own. promote the open ones into closed loops over time 4. find your agents by watching your own work dont jump to building a new agent. prototype in your main agent first, do the work yourself, run the same shape of task a few times, notice what you keep repeating and what context it needs. that pattern becomes your next specialist 5. design the loop before you run it a poorly designed loop burns tokens and hands you slop fast. design the steps, the checks, the budget and the done state, then run it. a loose loop wanders, a designed loop runs the same every time 6. orchestrate agents, not tools one agent per vertical, SEO, PR, paid, CRO, each with its own skills and scoped sub-agents. an orchestrator routes the work and a human champion keeps the brain clean. now its a team running your marketing, with you directing it 7. close the eval loop slop is a systems problem, judge the output against what good looks like, feed the misses back into the brain, and the system gets better you really don't need a CS degree for any of this, you need a builders mindset
