What makes a good offer?

The offer is not only the core conversion mechanims...

It affects how scalable your backend is too

Essentially the end all or be all of any traffic you run

So here's how to make a good one 👇

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HI3hkCGa0AA_Q1q?format=jpg&name=large)

---

What an offer actually is:

Everything the customer receives

(product, price, bonuses, guarantees, claims, terms.)

The full perceived value package.

Not just the bundle on your product page. It's the whole thing.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HI3hk7nbYAAAAdR?format=png&name=large)

---

3 core points that make a good offer:

Packaging: how you communicate what you do. Not what you do. Specific problem, specific person, specific way. Nail this and cold ads work.

Economics: packaging lets you charge more.

Higher price = better margins = better team = less churn =

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HI3hl1PbIAA8pfo?format=jpg&name=large)

---

The 4 levers inside any offer:

1)Dream outcome

What they actually want. The more visceral, the better.

2) Perceived likelihood

Do they believe it'll work? Social proof, UGC, reviews, guarantees. All of it.

3) Time delay

How fast do they get the result? Faster = more valuable.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HI3hmykakAALMrT?format=jpg&name=large)

---

Most people compete on price by accident.

No differentiation > turns to a commodity = race to the bottom.

The fix isn't better ads or a better team.

It's the offer. Fix that first. Everything else gets easier.
