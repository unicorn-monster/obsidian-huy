My ecommerce hot take is that most email/sms segmentation is bullshit.

Many just do targeted segmentation to feel sophisticated and because they hear other dumb-dumbs talk about it online... when in reality it doesn't make nearly as much money.

Reach >> relevance

I was just auditing a 9-figure brand doing $10M/mo, and the numbers made no sense.

- They only send to their wide-engaged list 3x per week
- Their wide engaged list is about 2 million people
- They get 50-100% more revenue on campaigns they send to this list

The other 4 days per week they send to a list half the size, sometimes smaller, and make a fraction of the revenue.

It makes zero sense as to why they are trying this segmented approach to be more "personalized".

My guess is whoever runs that account is over-segmenting to feel sophisticated.

The data doesn't care how clever the setup looks!

Sometimes the simple, "retarded" shit is the one that makes them most money.

As long as your list growth is beating your unsubscribes 3:1, keep sending wide and hit your big engaged list often.

Only after you hit your benchmark of daily sends should you layer in additional segmented / personalized campaigns.
