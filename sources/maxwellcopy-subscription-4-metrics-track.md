title: 4 subscription metrics to track for retention
---

Track these 4 metrics if you want to actually keep your customers (FOR SUBSCRIPTION BRANDS ONLY):

1/ First order subscription take rate.

- If you're a daily use product like AG1, you want to be at 70-80%.
- If you're more of a not-everyday product like a focus drink, 50% ish is perfect.
- First-time order subscribers have double the 12-month LTV compared to one-time purchasers.
- This is the metric you want to optimize for.
- Create subscription-first offers, optimize your PDP page, whatever you can to increase this.
- However, you MUST track CAC and contribution margin LTV to make sure it pays back… it will usually be more expensive first purchase to get someone to subscribe

2/ Order 2, 4, and 6 retention.

- Don't look at subscriber retention... Look at order retention.
- How many orders customers actually place orders and money actually gets into your pocket.
- Order 2 is your first rebill, so that's a key point. We like this no less than 60-70%.
- Order 4, we want 50%+.
- Order 6, 40% would be great but 30% is more typical.
- Look at where your biggest drop-offs are in these retention cohorts. That tells you exactly where to focus.

3/ Billing reminder cancellations.

- Look at your billing reminder email and A/B test the hell out of reducing cancellations.
- You can do this by customizing the email inside of Klaviyo rather than within your subscription platform.
- Core KPI varies brand to brand so it's hard to give a metric.
- Just make sure it's not transactional. Reaffirm the benefits of your product.
- And do NOT put the cancel your subscription button high up in the email.

4/ Cancellation flow recovery.

- We want to be at 15-20%+.
- When people cancel, don't just let them cancel.
- Use your subscription platform to see why they cancelled and get them back with timely messages.
- BONUS: use a founder message or video when people try to cancel to convince them to stay. This converts way better than any automated winback.
