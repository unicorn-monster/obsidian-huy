The most valuable google shopping ads masterclass you'll ever read fits in a tweet.

Your shopping title is a keyword field. Google uses it as the primary signal to decide which searches trigger your listing. Most brands waste it on the brand name.

Here's what to do instead:

Titles: the first 60 characters need to contain the highest-converting non-branded query for that product, not the brand name. "magnesium glycinate sleep supplement 500mg" beats "brand name magnesium 500mg" every time for discovery reach. Benefit language in the title also beats pure SEO strings on CTR.

Images: screenshot the shopping results for your biggest keywords. Whatever the majority is doing, do the opposite. If everyone has white background shots, run lifestyle. The listing that stands out gets clicked. The one that blends in doesn't.

Social proof: connect google customer reviews to merchant center. 4.7 stars from 847 reviews on the listing closes buyers before they've read a word of your ad copy. Minimum threshold is 50 reviews at 3.5 stars or above.

Free shipping badge: two identical listings, one with a shipping cost and one with free shipping. The free shipping one wins the click at a rate that should embarrass every brand not using it.

Bid structure: bestsellers in their own campaign with aggressive bids. New products in a testing campaign with controlled spend. Low-margin products with lower bids or excluded entirely. The algorithm should be executing your profit logic, not its own.

Search terms report: review it monthly. Add negatives. Mine it for title keywords. The brands printing with shopping do this without fail.
