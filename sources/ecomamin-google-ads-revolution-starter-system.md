## THE GOOGLE ADS STARTER SYSTEM

here's exactly how to launch your first profitable campaigns:

### STEP 1: SET UP YOUR FOUNDATION (WEEK 1)

WHAT YOU NEED:

1. Google Ads Account (free to create) — go to ads.google.com, click "Start Now", follow the prompts
2. Google Merchant Center Account (free) — go to merchants.google.com, connect your website, upload your product feed
3. Conversion Tracking (critical) — install Google tag on your site, track purchases as conversions, verify it's working

TOOLS YOU'LL NEED: Shopify/WooCommerce store (you already have this), Google Tag Manager (free, makes tracking easier), 30 minutes to set everything up

DON'T SKIP THIS: broken tracking = blind campaign management = wasted money

### STEP 2: FIX YOUR PRODUCT FEED (WEEK 1)

WHY THIS MATTERS: your product feed is how Google understands what you sell. bad feed = bad results (even with perfect campaigns)

PRODUCT TITLE FORMULA: [Main Keyword] - [What It Does] - [Key Feature] - [Your Brand]

EXAMPLES:

BAD: "Premium Dog Collar - Blue - XL"

GOOD: "Dog Collar - Adjustable Nylon - Reflective Safety Strip - [YourBrand]"

WHY IT WORKS: starts with what people search for ("dog collar"), includes benefit ("adjustable"), adds unique feature ("reflective"), brand at end (not start)

PRODUCT DESCRIPTION: First 150 characters are critical.

BAD: "Made from high-quality materials, this collar is perfect for your pet"

GOOD: "Stop your dog pulling on walks with our no-choke training collar. Vet-recommended, 60-day guarantee"

WHY IT WORKS: solves a specific problem, includes authority ("vet-recommended"), removes risk ("60-day guarantee")

PRODUCT IMAGES: minimum 800x800 pixels (higher is better), white background OR lifestyle shots (test both), show product clearly, no text overlays on main image

TIME INVESTMENT: 2-3 hours to optimize your top 10 products

IMPACT: 30-50% improvement in click rates

### STEP 3: LAUNCH YOUR FIRST 2 CAMPAIGNS (WEEK 2)

forget the 47 campaign types gurus talk about. you need TWO campaigns to start:

**CAMPAIGN 1: BRANDED SEARCH**

WHAT IT IS: capture people searching for YOUR brand name

WHY IT MATTERS: these are your hottest buyers - don't let competitors steal them

HOW TO SET IT UP: click "New Campaign" in Google Ads → select "Sales" as goal → choose "Search" campaign type → set budget: $10-20/day → location: your target country → language: english → add keywords: [your brand name], [your brand name] + product, buy [your brand name]

USE EXACT MATCH: put brackets around keywords: [your brand name]

EXPECTED RESULTS: 8-15x ROAS, 5-10% CTR, 3-5% conversion rate

BUDGET: $10-20/day = $300-600/month

**CAMPAIGN 2: STANDARD SHOPPING**

WHAT IT IS: show your products when people search for what you sell

WHY IT MATTERS: this is your main revenue driver (50-60% of sales)

HOW TO SET IT UP: click "New Campaign" → select "Sales" as goal → choose "Shopping" campaign type → select your Merchant Center account → campaign subtype: "Standard Shopping" → set budget: $30-50/day → location: your target country → bidding: start with "Manual CPC" → set bids: $0.30-0.50 per click

CRITICAL SETTINGS: campaign priority: keep as low for now, enable "Local inventory ads": NO (unless you have physical store), exclude brand terms: YES (those go in Campaign 1)

EXPECTED RESULTS: 3-5x ROAS, 1-2% CTR, 2-4% conversion rate

BUDGET: $30-50/day = $900-1,500/month

### TOTAL STARTING BUDGET

Month 1: $1,200-2,100. Expected Return: $4,800-10,500 (4-5x ROAS). Net Profit: $3,600-8,400. Time Investment: 5-7 hours setup, then 30 mins/day monitoring

### STEP 4: THE 2-WEEK MONITORING PROCESS

DON'T PANIC AND CHANGE EVERYTHING — this is the #1 beginner mistake. here's what to do instead:

DAYS 1-7: JUST WATCH — check campaigns daily, note which products get clicks, note which keywords get impressions, DON'T change anything yet

WHAT TO LOOK FOR: are ads actually showing? (check impression share), are people clicking? (CTR should be 1%+), are they buying? (even 1-2 sales is a good sign)

DAYS 8-14: OPTIMIZE CAREFULLY — if something is clearly broken, fix it:

IF NO IMPRESSIONS: increase bids by 30%, check if budget is limiting spend, verify product feed is approved

IF CLICKS BUT NO SALES: check if tracking is working, review landing pages, make sure prices are competitive

IF GETTING SALES: don't touch anything, let it run for full 14 days, collect more data

THE 2-WEEK RULE: never make major changes without 2 weeks of consistent data. Google's algorithm needs time to learn. every change resets the learning phase. patience = profit

### STEP 5: SCALE WHAT WORKS (WEEK 3-4)

after 2 weeks, you'll see patterns:

GOOD SIGNS: 3x+ ROAS, 2%+ conversion rate, consistent daily sales

WEEK 3: increase budget by 20% on profitable campaigns, add negative keywords (searches that didn't convert), identify your top 5 best-selling products

WEEK 4: increase budget by another 20%, create exact match search campaigns for top keywords, start planning PMAX campaign (but don't launch yet)

SCALING RULES:

1. never increase budget more than 20% per week — bigger jumps reset the algorithm, patience compounds
2. only scale campaigns with 3x+ ROAS above your tROAS — if it's not profitable, fix it first, don't throw more money at losers
3. check campaigns 2-3x per week (not daily) — daily checking leads to panic changes, weekly reviews lead to better decisions

MONTH 2 TARGET: $2,500-4,000/month spend, 4-6x ROAS, $10k-24k revenue, 15-30 mins daily management

## THE BEGINNER'S MISTAKE CHECKLIST

avoid these and you'll be ahead of 90% of people:

### MISTAKE #1: LAUNCHING 10 CAMPAIGNS AT ONCE

WHY IT FAILS: budget spread too thin, can't tell what's working, algorithm doesn't get enough data

DO THIS INSTEAD: start with 2 campaigns, get them profitable, then add more

### MISTAKE #2: SETTING BUDGET TOO LOW

WHY IT FAILS: need minimum $40-70/day combined, lower than that = not enough clicks, not enough clicks = no data

DO THIS INSTEAD: save up $1,500 minimum, commit to 30-day test, don't go below $40/day total

### MISTAKE #3: CHANGING EVERYTHING AFTER 3 DAYS

WHY IT FAILS: Google needs 7-14 days to learn, every change resets learning phase, panic = wasted money

DO THIS INSTEAD: set it up correctly once, let it run 14 days minimum, make ONE change at a time

### MISTAKE #4: IGNORING PRODUCT FEED QUALITY

WHY IT FAILS: bad titles = wrong searches, bad images = no clicks, bad descriptions = no conversions

DO THIS INSTEAD: spend 2-3 hours on feed optimization, follow the title formula, use high-quality images

### MISTAKE #5: NOT TRACKING CONVERSIONS

WHY IT FAILS: can't tell what's profitable, flying blind with your money, algorithm can't optimize

DO THIS INSTEAD: verify tracking BEFORE spending money, do a test purchase, confirm it shows in Google Ads

### MISTAKE #6: COPYING U.S. STRATEGIES TO OTHER COUNTRIES

WHY IT FAILS: different competition levels, different search volumes, different consumer behavior

DO THIS INSTEAD: test in smaller countries first (UK, Canada, Australia), adjust based on local data, scale what works in THAT market

### MISTAKE #7: GIVING UP AFTER 2 WEEKS

WHY IT FAILS: 2 weeks isn't enough data, algorithm is still learning, you quit right before it works

DO THIS INSTEAD: commit to 60 days minimum, adjust based on data, give it a real chance

## COMMON BEGINNER QUESTIONS

**Q: Can I start with just $500?** A: technically yes, but you'll struggle. $500 = ~12 days at $40/day. not enough time to collect meaningful data. save up $1,500 minimum for a real test

**Q: How long until I'm profitable?** A: depends on your product and setup. good products: 14-30 days. average products: 30-60 days. bad products: never (fix product first)

**Q: Do I need a big product catalog?** A: no. even 5-10 products is enough. better to have 10 great products than 100 mediocre ones

**Q: What if I don't get any sales in week 1?** A: normal. Google needs data to optimize. if you get ZERO clicks, increase bids. if you get clicks but no sales, check: tracking (is it working?), pricing (are you competitive?), landing page (is it converting?)

**Q: Can I run Google Ads for services (not products)?** A: yes, but this guide is for ecom. service businesses use different strategies. focus on local service ads + search campaigns

**Q: What ROAS should I expect?** A: month 1: 2-3x (learning phase). month 2-3: 3-5x (optimization phase). month 4+: 4-8x (scaling phase). anything above 3x is profitable for most products
