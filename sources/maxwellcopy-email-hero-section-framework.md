title: Email hero section 6-step build framework

The hero section is the most important real estate in your entire email.

That's why 75% of your design effort should live in this single section…

Here's how to build one that actually earns the click:

1/ Start with what's above the fold

Above the fold is everything they see before scrolling. The goal is that they don't have to scroll to get what they need.

You need a clear headline, a line or two of supporting copy, an obvious button, and a strong graphic.

2/ Make the image do the selling

The image drives more performance than anything else in the hero, so don't just drop in a product shot. Match it to what actually sells the product.

3/ Keep the background behind your text clean

Your headline should be EFFORTLESS to read. Busy stuff behind the text creates friction, and you should never put faces or people behind your copy.

Use AI image extenders to add clean space up top if you need it, or add contrast behind the text so it reads instantly.

4/ Shape the text itself

Don't leave one or two stray words dangling on the last line of a block. Shape your copy like a reverse triangle that funnels the eye down to the button.

It takes some messing with the wording and line breaks, but it's worth it.

5/ Make the text POP.

Mix up your text sizes, fonts, and colors to give the hero some life and pull attention. A flat, uniform hero just gets skimmed past.

6/ Mix up your heroes

Running the same headline, body, and button layout every single email is how your sends start feeling like a template. And the second an email feels templated, people stop opening.

So rotate through different formats. Things like

> Leading with the image and putting the headline under it
> Stacking a testimonial above the headline.
> Opening with a bold infographic.
> Trying a full-bleed image with the text laid over it instead of the usual white background.

Get the hero right and you're most of the way to an email that actually converts.
