5-step AI-research system (using Claude Cowork's web-browsing/vision) for finding ecom product opportunities BEFORE they saturate, instead of copying an already-crowded winning brand.

## Why this over copying a winner
Copying a visibly-winning brand means entering a market with 200 competitors already fighting on price. The system instead looks for the intersection of: a rising macro trend (tailwind) + an underserved micro-segment within it (less competition) + a real unsolved problem (demand exists) + a provable winning-angle pattern from adjacent categories (messaging that's proven to work) + something actually buildable/sourceable.

## 5-step framework with prompt structure per step
1. **Identify rising industries** — prompt researches industry-growth articles, VC funding trends, Reddit/social trend chatter, regulatory changes creating new demand, demographic shifts (aging population, Gen Z habits, WFH). Target filter: 20%+ annual growth but still under $5B market size (early enough to enter, big enough to matter). Example outputs cited: metabolic health tracking ($800M, 45%/yr, driven by GLP-1 awareness spillover), indoor air quality ($2.1B, 28%/yr, post-COVID + wildfire smoke), pet telehealth ($340M, 67%/yr, vet shortage), adaptogens for women ($950M, 38%/yr), elderly tech ($1.4B, 31%/yr, tech-savvy aging boomers).
2. **Find pain points within the rising category** — mine Reddit (top posts last 3mo in relevant subs), Amazon 1-2★ reviews on category leaders, forum "problems with X" threads, YouTube review-video comments. Output: ranked pain points in the customer's own language, why current solutions fail, price sensitivity. Example (metabolic tracking): "$400/mo CGM needs a doctor," "I don't know what to do with the data," "insurance won't cover unless diabetic," "want to track without wearing a device 24/7."
3. **Find underserved segments within the category** — scrape top-10 brand sites in the space to see WHO they target (demo, language, testimonials shown), then identify who's absent (demographic not addressed, price tier not served, geography ignored), plus intersectional angles (`[rising category] + [specific demographic]`, e.g. "adaptogens for MEN" when everyone targets women) and lifestyle segments current solutions don't fit (busy parents, shift workers, renters). Example (indoor air quality): renters (can't install whole-home systems → need portable plug-and-play), pet owners specifically (generic purifiers don't target dander), budget-conscious ($400+ quality tier has no $89 essentials-only option), mold-specific sufferers (detection/remediation is expensive, no cheap testing-kit option), small commercial spaces (stuck between home-grade-too-weak and commercial-too-expensive).
4. **Extract specific product ideas** — research adjacent-segment products that could adapt, mine "I wish someone made X" forum language, check funded Kickstarter/Indiegogo campaigns in related categories for feature-validation, scan what's already normalized in Japan/Korea/Europe but hasn't crossed over. Output per idea: description, differentiator vs current solutions, estimated COGS, realistic retail price, and PROOF of demand (forum mention counts, search volume, backer counts) — not just a hunch.
5. **Reverse-engineer winning angles from adjacent successful brands** — scrape 3-5 successful brand homepages/product pages/advertorial content/reviews for: main headline framing, unique-mechanism claim, social proof type, guarantee structure, objection-handling pattern, and the specific customer language that shows up unprompted in positive reviews. Output: angle formulas + patterns common across winners + gaps (angles NOBODY in the space is using yet).

## Downstream steps once a product idea exists
**Competitive gap analysis** — map direct + adjacent competitors' pricing/features/positioning to find a real (not imagined) gap: a price tier nobody serves, a feature nobody has, a positioning claim nobody makes.
**Funnel-type selection** — match funnel format to how much education/trust/consideration the specific customer+price point requires (direct-to-PDP for high-intent-low-consideration, advertorial for education-needed, quiz for multi-SKU-choice-paralysis, comparison page for aware-and-choosing-between-brands, VSL for high-ticket/story-driven, free+shipping for trial/continuity).
**Ad-angle generation** — 10 angles combining a hook category (problem/benefit/story/contrarian/question) + a credibility element (social-proof count/authority/science-citation/founder-story) + an urgency/offer mechanic (discount/scarcity/bonus/guarantee), validated against what's actually running in the Meta Ad Library / Google Ads preview for the category.
