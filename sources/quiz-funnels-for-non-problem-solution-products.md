Welcome Baby's Quiz Funnel is a great way to show how you can take an average product idea — something without maybe a huge pain point, compared to back pain, neck pain, etc — yet create it as almost part of your product. Like I have mentioned before, how apps do onboarding funnels.

![[welcomebaby-01.png]]

This creates your personalised baby book. Let's break down how they've created this as not only a sales mechanism, but part of their product & experience too.

First step is qualifying who this is for: yourself, or a gift. Intentions and phrases are different — it's almost 2 funnels in 1.

![[welcomebaby-02.png]]

They have made the quiz about the customer — not the product. Welcome Baby's first question isn't about the book. It's: "Has your baby arrived yet, or are you still expecting?" That's not data collection — that's emotional activation. You just made a new parent think about their baby. Game over.

![[welcomebaby-03.png]]

They're not pushing as much emotion — simply gathering data on who it's for, which ultimately ties right back into maximising their product experience. They also do a great job remembering who is buying the product — what is going to make the person going through the quiz have the "aha" moment.

![[welcomebaby-04.png]]

Great social proof & loading screen to make this feel more interactive.

![[welcomebaby-05.png]]

Baby's gender → arrived yet? → first child? → family structure → what excites you most? Each answer pulls them deeper into their own story. By question 5, they're not evaluating a product — they're imagining their family's book. It then dives into future pacing — you're starting to get yourself excited about the product.

![[welcomebaby-06.png]]

And you're arguing with yourself on why you should buy this product. No one's telling you "oh you can track their milestones" or "keep their memories" — you're telling yourself. Back to building the product.

![[welcomebaby-07.png]]

Questions 9 and 10 — events, cute details. Gender reveals. First haircut. Handprints. You're mentally flipping through a book that doesn't exist yet.

![[welcomebaby-08.png]]

Loader #2 fires. Progress bar. "1.7 million families." You're not early — you're late. Break #2 shifts the frame entirely: expert authority, brain development, games designed by professionals. The book just went from sentimental keepsake to parenting tool. New value layer unlocked.

Questions 11–15 go deep: baby's personality, activities, development focus — you're giving them information only a parent would know. This isn't a quiz, it's a confession. The more you share, the more tailored the result feels. The more tailored it feels, the harder it is to leave. The final personalisation layer for the product is a nice touch.
