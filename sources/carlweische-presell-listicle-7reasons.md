Before we get into it, I just wanna explain why a pre-sell page is the most important page you can have as a brand owner…

If you want to scale seriously, you need to target cold, less aware traffic since your warm, more aware audience is really small & caps out really fast.

& in order to scale on cold traffic, you can’t just send people to a product page without properly educating them first on their problem & your solution.

Aka: you need a pre-sell page you send cold traffic to before sending them to your offer page.

It’s by far one of the biggest levers you can pull for scaling.

The one we used to take this brand to $1.36M was a listicle with 7 reasons for why people buy their product.

Here’s exactly how we structured it so you can do the same:

**The headline**

The template we used is "7 reasons over \[X amount\] of \[target audience\] choose \[your product\]."

(you can also add a \[to achieve desirable result\] at the end)

So something like: "7 reasons over 50,000 gardeners now use elevated planters to grow more with less weeding."

So you tell the reader what they’re about to read & show social proof.

Under the headline you have this:

**A comparison table**

Right under the headline, put a simple table where you compare your product against your competitors.

So you have your product and its beneficial features one side, the competition and everything they're missing (what they’re doing wrong) on the other.

This sets the frame for the entire rest of the page by showing your product win a side-by-side comparison against other products.

You’re essentially educating the reader very quickly on why your competitors’ products aren’t viable options.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HKDuoj1XsAACUnm?format=jpg&name=large)

Just add your product name to that top box.

Then under that you have the 7 reasons with their own sections, in this order:

**1\. Money (or whatever resource they're burning by not having your product)**

Start with a common problem they have right now where they're wasting that resource, introduce your product's mechanism as the thing that fixes it, then close on the upside of having more of that resource back.

So if the resource is money:

Don't just say "it saves you money"

Walk them through exactly WHY they’re wasting money now, HOW your product saves them money, & what this means for them.

Also, & this goes for all 7 sections, have a related/supporting image for each point.

**2\. Ease of use**

Here you just walk them through a stupidly simple version of how the product works.

Something like "insert it, turn it on, line up the laser, that's it."

The point of this is to kill the "this feels complicated" objection.

You want them thinking anyone could use your product in seconds & get the result easily without any skills needed.

**3\. Versatility**

Just quickly show the versatility/compatibility of your product.

Show that it works in a bunch of different situations, or alongside stuff they already own/do.

Just write out the range of desirable things it can do.

**4\. Identity + quality**

**Identity:** (something like "built for serious \[whatever\]") makes the reader see the product as something that reinforces the identity they want to have/the person they wanna be.

**Quality:** (something like "made from aircraft grade aluminium, flawless performance even in the toughest environments" essentially just show that your product is of good quality) justifies the price & negates any quality-related worries (which is a massive one for a lot of products).

**5\. Speed**

Here you just wanna show how fast they’ll get the result using your product (preferably faster than ever before).

& make it as concrete as humanly possible...

Don't say "get \[desired outcome\] faster than ever before"

Say something like "with this product, you can get \[desired outcome\] in under \[timeframe\]."

This just makes it more believable & helps the reader actually picture the outcome clearly.

**6\. Social proof**

Just drop some social proof. Could be your costumer count, some reviews, some clear visual results.

& if you have a lot of social proof, make that clear.

Not really any downside to showing a lot.

**7\. Guarantee**

Just state your guarantee as another main point.

(if you have a good guarantee of course. if you don’t have one then you should add one. it helps a ton with AOV & CVR since it makes the purchase feel safer, & most people won’t even use the guarantee so nothing to lose really)

CTA + some other stuff

Finish with a bridge headline, something like "the smarter way to \[your core promise\]," then state your unique mechanism one more time so it's the last thing in their head, then drop a CTA that sends them to your actual offer page.
