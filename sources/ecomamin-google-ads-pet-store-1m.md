## the product hunt (finding dog harnesses nobody else was selling)

i was 19, broke, living at home, watching everyone around me lose money on dropshipping

they were all selling the same aliexpress garbage everyone else was selling

wireless earbuds, phone cases, led strips

i knew i needed something different

so i started looking at markets everyone ignored

not the us (too competitive)

not massive categories (too saturated)

i looked at the uk pet market specifically

here's my thought process: pet owners spend money like crazy, uk has high gdp per capita similar to us, way less competition than american market, google ads works better in english-speaking markets

i started researching what uk pet owners were actually searching for

not what was trending on facebook, what they were typing into google

that's when i found it: "no pull dog harness"

thousands of searches per month, barely any good ads showing up

the products on the market were either cheap garbage or £80+ premium brands

nothing in the middle

i found a supplier making quality harnesses for £8-12 landed cost

i could sell them for £35-45 and still be cheaper than the premium brands

profit margin: 65-70% after product cost and shipping

this was it

then i found the second product: orthopedic dog beds

same story: high search volume, shit competition, great margins

people searching "best bed for old dogs" and "dog bed for arthritis"

these weren't impulse buys, these were problem-solution purchases

perfect for google ads

## the ad angles that actually worked (problem-aware beats everything)

most people run google ads with generic angles

"premium dog harness - shop now"

"comfortable dog bed - free shipping"

boring, converts at 1-2%

i went problem-aware instead

here's the difference:

generic angle: "no pull dog harness - durable and comfortable"

my angle: "does your dog pull on walks? this harness stops pulling in 3 days or full refund"

generic angle: "orthopedic dog bed for large dogs"

my angle: "vet-recommended bed for dogs with arthritis and hip dysplasia - 60 night trial"

see how mine speak directly to the problem?

when someone searches "my dog pulls on leash," they don't want a harness

they want to stop getting their arm ripped off every morning

when someone searches "best bed for senior dog," they don't want a bed

they want their dog to stop limping and be in less pain

i built every campaign around this

the harness campaigns had angles like:

- "stop leash pulling without choking your dog"
- "the harness dog trainers actually recommend"
- "why prong collars hurt more than help (and what works instead)"

the bed campaigns had angles like:

- "the bed that helps arthritic dogs get up easier"
- "recommended by vets for senior dogs with joint pain"
- "memory foam that actually supports your dog's hips"

conversion rates: 8-12% depending on the search term

everyone else was getting 2-3%

## the campaign structure that scaled to $83k/month

i didn't start with some complex 47-campaign structure

i started simple and added complexity as i scaled

month 1-2: testing phase

campaign 1: branded search (my brand name + product terms)

- budget: £20/day
- roas: 15x+ (people specifically searching for us)

campaign 2: high-intent shopping (product-specific terms)

- budget: £50/day
- search terms: "no pull harness," "anti pull dog harness," "harness to stop pulling"
- roas: 4-6x

campaign 3: problem-aware search (pain point terms)

- budget: £30/day
- search terms: "my dog pulls on leash," "how to stop dog pulling," "dog pulling training"
- roas: 3-5x

total daily budget: £100/day monthly spend: £3k revenue: £12-15k roas: 4-5x

this ran for 60 days while i figured out what worked

month 3-6: scaling phase

once i had data, i started scaling

i added:

- google shopping campaigns (standard shopping for control)
- competitor comparison campaigns (bidding on competitor brand names)
- youtube ads (retargeting people who visited but didn't buy)
- display remarketing (showing ads to site visitors on other sites)

the shopping campaigns were the real unlock

i optimized the google merchant center feed with the exact titles people were searching:

- "no pull dog harness for large dogs that pull - stops pulling without choking"
- "orthopedic memory foam dog bed for senior dogs with arthritis - vet recommended"

conversion rate on shopping: 6-8%

way higher than search because the images showed the product solving the problem

month 6-12: optimization phase

by month 6 i was spending £200/day profitably

this is where most people fuck up

they just keep increasing budget and watch roas decline

i didn't do that

instead i got smarter about where the money went

i added:

- negative keyword lists (blocking "diy," "homemade," "cheap," "free shipping" searches)
- geographic bid adjustments (london converted 40% better than manchester, so i bid 40% more)
- time-of-day bid adjustments (evenings converted 2x better than mornings)
- device bid adjustments (mobile converted worse, so i bid 30% less on mobile)

these tiny optimizations added up to 15-20% roas improvement

same budget, way better results

by month 12:

- daily budget: £500/day
- monthly spend: £15k
- revenue: £83k/month
- roas: 5.5x
- profit: £35k/month after product costs, ads, and overhead

i was 19 making more than my dad

## the beginning of the end (the vat law nobody saw coming)

everything was printing

£35k/month profit, growing 15% month-over-month

then in month 13, the uk changed their vat laws for imported goods

previously: goods under £15 were vat-exempt

new law: ALL imported goods subject to 20% vat, even if under £15

my products were imported from china

my landed cost went from £10 to £12 overnight (20% vat added)

my margins went from 70% to 58%

still profitable, but the economics changed

here's where it really hurt:

the vat also applied at checkout for customers

so my £45 product became £54 at checkout when customers added vat

conversion rate dropped from 8% to 4.5%

people were seeing the price increase at the last step and bouncing

i tried:

- absorbing the vat (killed margins to 40%)
- increasing prices to £60 pre-vat (conversion rate dropped to 2%)
- finding uk suppliers (no one made the quality i needed at the price point)

nothing worked

over 3 months revenue dropped from £83k to £41k

i was still profitable but the growth trajectory was dead

i had two options:

1. keep running it at lower margins and slower growth
2. shut it down and start something new

i chose option 2

sold the inventory at cost, shut down the ads, moved on

total time: 15 months

total revenue: ~£850k

total profit: ~£280k

at 19 years old

## what i learned (the principles that still work today)

looking back 6 years later, here's what i'd do differently and what still works:

what still works:

**1\. pick less competitive markets**

everyone fights for the us

there's money in uk, australia, canada, nordic countries

less competition = better economics

**2\. problem-aware beats product-focused**

"stop your dog pulling" beats "no pull harness" every time

sell the solution not the product

**3\. google ads rewards good products**

facebook lets you scale garbage with great creative

google doesn't

your product has to actually work or refunds kill you

**4\. margins matter more than revenue**

i made more profit in 12 months than most people make in 3 years

because i had 65-70% margins

if you're running 30% margins you need 2x the revenue for same profit

**5\. test simple, scale complex**

start with 3 campaigns and £100/day

don't build some 40-campaign structure on day 1

add complexity as you learn what works

what i'd do differently:

**1\. plan for tax law changes**

i got blindsided by the vat change

should have had uk suppliers lined up as backup

should have stress-tested the economics at different margin levels

**2\. build the brand earlier**

i was purely performance marketing

no social media, no email list, no brand equity

when the vat hit i had no moat

if i'd built a brand i could have raised prices and kept customers

**3\. diversify revenue earlier**

i was 100% google ads

should have added email, organic social, influencer partnerships by month 6

one channel dependency is always risky

**4\. hire help sooner**

i did everything myself until month 10

should have hired a va at month 4 to handle customer service

freed me up to focus on scaling ads and finding new products
