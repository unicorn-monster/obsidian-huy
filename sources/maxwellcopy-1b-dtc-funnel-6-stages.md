**STAGE 1: THE OFFER**

Nothing works unless you have a banger offer (specifically for subscription).

A banger offer that converts cold traffic.

Gone are the days of just doing 10% off.

These brands are all subscription brands and they stack the offer so hard that NOT subscribing feels stupid.

AG1:

- $79/mo
- 20% OFF + $43 in gifts + Free shipping
- If you want a one-time purchase, you don’t get any gifts and pay $99/mo + $9 shipping

It’s a value stacked offer and it’s only available for subscription.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjfWsLb0AA849y?format=jpg&name=large)

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjfhskb0AEHlAZ?format=jpg&name=large)

IM8 Health...

- $78/mo
- 30% OFF + $72 in free gifts + 90 day transformation program
- One time purchase option is barely visible and is $112 ($34 more expensive)

Another no-brainer here.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjfnZOaEAAOMVi?format=jpg&name=large)

Grüns...

- $40.80
- Up to 52% OFF + Free shipping
- One time purchase is $54.40 + shipping

Simpler, but larger discount.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjfqmraYAAFtf7?format=jpg&name=large)

Notice how these brands don't just throw up an extra 10% for first purchase or subscription…

They give you a massive discount, stack free gifts, and make one-time purchase feel like the sucker's choice.

The value of a customer whose first order is a subscription is around 50-100% the 6-month LTV of someone whose first order is a one-time purchase.

And about 2x at 12 months.

That's why they go so hard on these offers.

You should put serious time into testing your acquisition offers until you find the one that converts cold traffic the best.

**STAGE 2: ADS**

The first thing you'll notice when you look at these ad libraries is the volume.

AG1: 530 active ads. Grüns: 910 active ads. IM8: 1,300 active ads.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjftjZaIAACXzW?format=jpg&name=large)

The IM8 founder said in a recent interview they're pushing towards 2,500 ads live at once.

With Meta's Andromeda update, the algorithm really values having a ton of diverse creatives live at all times.

You need an engine of new content being produced every single week and you need to be killing your losers on a weekly basis. Newness drives down CPMs and increases conversions.

Here's what the ad mix actually looks like...

**1/ Static Ads (about 50% of total):**

You only need two types of static ads. That's it.

Educational infographics showing ingredients, benefits, and what's inside the product.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjfvg5bQAAxzBu?format=jpg&name=large)

And direct offer ads highlighting that acquisition offer and the welcome kit.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjgHEMa8AAUABI?format=jpg&name=large)

A lot of these statics are being generated by AI which lets them pump out massive volume efficiently.

**2/ Video Ads (about 50% of total):**

Huge variety... UGC, educational, instructional.

But the big thing these brands are doing that most people aren't is collaboration and white label ads.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjgWfkb0AAlZ4B?format=jpg&name=large)

They partner with influencers and run ads directly from the influencer's account with the brand tagged as a collaborator.

This targets the influencer's followers and looks completely native... it's not some random brand in your feed, it's someone you already follow showing you something they use.

If you look into their, 50% of their video ads use this format. It's clearly working.

The formula...

→ Pump out new creatives weekly, kill losers weekly

→ Even split static to video

→ Static = educational infographics + direct offer ads

→ Video = diverse UGC and educational + heavy white label and collab ads with influencers

→ Use AI for static volume

**STAGE 3: CUSTOM LANDING PAGES**

This is where it gets really interesting and where most brands completely drop the ball.

Most brands just drive ad traffic to a basic PDP page. Same product page everybody sees regardless of what ad they clicked on.

These brands don't do that...

They build custom landing pages for every specific pain point or benefit that the ad targets.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjgaYvbcAAk1RU?format=jpg&name=large)

This IM8 ad talks about gut issues.

Instead of sending people to the general product page, they send them to a custom listicle specifically about gut health and how the product helps with that problem.

The only people clicking that ad are people with gut issues.

You shouldn’t send TOF folks send them to a generic page anymore.

You need to show how your product specifically solves their direct pain point.

These brands typically have 10-20 different landing pages and are rolling out new ones consistently.

Examples:

- IM8 Gut Health: [https://get.im8health.com/pages/gut-health](https://get.im8health.com/pages/gut-health)
- IM8 Scientific Angle: [https://get.im8health.com/pages/science](https://get.im8health.com/pages/science)
- IM8 Energy: [https://get.im8health.com/pages/feel-again](https://get.im8health.com/pages/feel-again)
- IM8 Convenience: [https://get.im8health.com/pages/why-im8](https://get.im8health.com/pages/why-im8)
- IM8 For Competitive Athletes: [https://get.im8health.com/pages/competitive](https://get.im8health.com/pages/competitive)

With AI being able to produce custom landing pages quickly, brands are going crazy with volume here.

Many are even building custom advertorials for specific influencers they work with…

And every single one of these landing pages directs towards that same acquisition offer.

The offer is consistent everywhere…

**STAGE 4: POP-UP FORMS**

People don't just click a landing page and instantly buy.

It takes time and education.

And ads are expensive as hell. If somebody doesn't buy on their first visit, the brand has to pay a ton more on retargeting to get them back to the site.

Or... you can get their email address and send them messaging for fractions of a cent.

Email is the most used communication channel on the planet, 90% of adults check it multiple times a day, and you get to be in people's faces for literally pennies.

That's why every single one of these brands runs pop-up forms aggressively.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjgiEGboAAaJdB?format=jpg&name=large)

Here's the format that's working in 2026…

You engage the customer first by asking them a question, then you ask for the email.

This works because of commitment bias.

The customer sees "52% off, all I have to do is answer this question."

They answer it, and now they're way more likely to follow through and give you the email because they already started the process.

You also get zero-party data because they told you exactly what they're trying to get out of your product.

Also, these brands don't give an extra discount on the pop-up.

They just highlight the existing acquisition offer!

The pop-up says "get 52% off" but that 52% is already available on the website to anyone.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjgkxia0AAvrJK?format=jpg&name=large)

People think they have to enter their email to get the deal.

They don't.

But the brand gets the email without giving up any extra margin.

- **Pop-up A:** "Get 10% off your first order" (extra discount, kills your margins)
- **Pop-up B:** "Get 52% off your first order" (same offer as the site, zero extra cost, and it sounds more enticing than 10% off so you get better opt-ins)

Pop-up B wins every time. And it didn't cost you a single extra dollar.

**STAGE 5: EMAIL & SMS FLOWS**

Once you have the email, this is where the real work begins.

These brands go aggressive.

Way more aggressive than most people think is acceptable…

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjgn4IaAAAtPAR?format=jpg&name=large)

Take a look at Mars Men.

They sent me five emails on the first day.

Then three the next day.

Then three more.

Here's why this frequency works...

When somebody clicks on an ad and enters your email list, Meta sees that and feeds that customer a ton of competitor ads selling similar products.

Your competitors are now in that person's face.

The brand needs to convert that customer as fast as possible before they go try someone else.

When a customer clicks on your ad and joins your list, that is the warmest they are ever going to be.

Every single day after that they only get colder.

So these brands go aggressive early because the cost of waiting is losing that customer to a competitor

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjgqiQbMAA8Tos?format=jpg&name=large)

Look at the email designs... strong infographics, tons of education, and the offer is at the top of every single email in the hero section

The customer never has to scroll to find the discount.

No blocks of copy anywhere (besides text based emails).

Everything is scannable infographics with social proof woven throughout.

And then the secret sauce... text-based emails from the founder.

These crush. They feel personal, they stand out from all the designed emails in your inbox, and they look like a friend just sent you a message.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjkQC5bEAA9SOX?format=jpg&name=large)

Daily SMS as well with infographics, education, and that welcome offer highlighted in every message.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHjhAkwbcAAlZFp?format=jpg&name=large)

All of this costs pennies compared to what it would cost to retarget these people with ads.

That's the secret sauce and that's what makes these brands so much more profitable.

**STAGE 6: POST-PURCHASE & RETENTION**

Since these are subscription brands, once they get the conversion, they've won.

Now the product does the work.

They don't keep hammering subscribers with sales emails. That would annoy them and give them a reason to cancel.

Instead they shift entirely to education.

Strong educational content with the first purchase.

Then educational emails and SMS reminding you to use the product every day, showing you the benefits, and reaffirming that you made a good decision.

If you subscribe to any of these brands, you'll notice they actually start emailing you less once you're a customer.

Because every email to a subscriber is a reminder that the subscription exists... and a potential reason to think "maybe I should cancel."

The focus shifts from selling to retaining. Keep them using the product. Keep them feeling good about the decision. Let the product do the heavy lifting.

**THE FULL FUNNEL:**

1. Create a banger offer and make it only available for subscription. Stack value with discounts and a welcome kit.
2. Pump crazy ad volume on Meta
3. Send ad traffic towards specific pain point landing pages that align with the pain point of the ad the person clicked on.
4. Fire an optimized quiz pop-up form 2-4 seconds upon page load simply highlighting your acquisition offer asking for someone’s email
5. Send daily email and sms to get people to convert
6. When people subscribe, front load educational content then dial back your communication and let your product retain them

When every stage of this funnel is connected properly, here's what changes...

- Your ads get cheaper because more people convert on the first visit.
- Your list grows faster because your pop-up is capturing emails without extra discounting.
- Your email flows convert subscribers within days instead of weeks.
- Your retention stays high because you're educating instead of annoying.
