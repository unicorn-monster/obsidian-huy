Breakdown of 5 real Google Shopping listings that combined for ~$1M revenue in 30 days, zero creative production — pure feed + structure + bidding discipline.

## Why Shopping beats Meta on speed
Meta timeline to $100k/mo: ~6 months (2mo creative testing → 2mo scaling → 2mo fighting creative fatigue). Shopping timeline to $100k/mo: ~2 months (days 1-2 feed optimization → days 3-7 campaign launch → weeks 2-4 profitable scaling). Meta = creating demand (interrupt, build desire from scratch, need perfect creative/targeting/timing). Shopping = capturing demand (people already searching, click, buy — no creative needed, no algorithm games).

## The 5 winning listings (pattern extraction)
1. **Polar Dive USA MAX Edition** — $949, ~$285k/30d. Brand-first truncated title, premium price filters tire-kickers, free shipping expected at this tier, captures "cold plunge"/"ice bath" searches. ~10 sales/day.
2. **VeeCleanse Boric Acid Suppositories** — $24.99, ~$187k/30d. Exact-name SEO title, 4.9★/4k+ reviews (sensitive category needs heavy trust), delivery-deadline shown for urgency, clean product photo. ~250 sales/day.
3. **Personalized Dog Harness** — $49.95, ~$224k/30d. Title leads with pain-point ("No Pull") + personalization + warranty signal, 4.7★/5k+ reviews, free shipping, bold contrast visual. ~150 sales/day.
4. **The Plunge Original Cold Plunge Tub** — $4,990, ~$199k/30d. Category-leader brand framing ("The"), ultra-premium price filters non-serious buyers, 4.8★/435 reviews validates the big spend, free shipping critical at this price, minimalist premium visual. 1-2 sales/day.
5. **Furbo 360° Dog Camera** — $34 (from $210, 84% off), ~$153k/30d. Aggressive discount + SALE badge, 4.6★/769 reviews, product-in-use photo (app shown). ~150 sales/day.

**Universal pattern across all 5:** optimized front-loaded title · 4.6+★ and 400+ reviews minimum, displayed prominently · free shipping non-negotiable · clean professional product visuals · high-intent/exact-match search terms.

## The feed formula
**Title:** `[Brand] + [Product Type] + [Key Benefit/Feature] + [Unique Value Prop]`, front-load most important keyword, brand included, natural language, <150 chars — no keyword-stuffing, no ALL CAPS, no price/promo text in title.
**Description:** first 160 chars = highest-impact info + primary keyword (shows in search result), full body 500-1000 chars, benefit-focused with specific details, materials, use-cases, warranty.
**Category:** use the MOST SPECIFIC Google Product Category taxonomy node available — specificity = better placement.
**Images:** min 800×800 (1200×1200 recommended), clean white/neutral background, product fills 75-90% of frame, no watermark/text/promo badges, additional angle images + lifestyle context where relevant.
**Pricing:** competitive within 10-15% of market unless justified by quality; charm-pricing ($49.95) for budget tier, round numbers for premium tier; sale-pricing shows original+savings clearly (Furbo: $210→$34 = 84% off drove volume).
**Shipping:** free shipping converts 2-3x better than paid — build cost into price if margin allows, else use a free-shipping threshold.
**Product highlights (2-4 bullets):** shown directly in shopping result, boosts CTR (e.g. "Lifetime Warranty", "Made in USA", "Free Returns", "Ships Same Day").

## Campaign structure (performance-tier model, complements priority-laddering)
4-way split instead of one dumping-ground campaign: **Best Sellers** (top 10-20% by revenue, highest budget, aggressive bidding, priority placement) · **New Products** (recent launches, testing budget, moderate bidding, gather data) · **Seasonal/Trending** (time-sensitive, aggressive during peak) · **Liquidation** (clearance/overstock, lower bidding, volume focus). Suggested starting split: 60% best-sellers / 20% new / 15% seasonal / 5% liquidation. Without structure: best sellers compete with dogs for budget, no spend control, losers eat budget while winners starve.

## Bidding progression
New campaigns start Manual CPC (learn, gather data, full control) → after 30 days of data switch to Target ROAS (let Google optimize/scale). Bid by tier: best-sellers = highest bids/top position/accept higher CPC for volume; new = moderate/patient; liquidation = lower bids, volume over position.

## Negative keywords (baseline list)
Generic exclusions for most products: free, cheap, diy, used, repair, how to, tutorial, review (sometimes). Product-specific example (premium dog harness): "cheap dog harness", "free dog harness", "diy dog harness", "used dog harness", "dog harness pattern", "how to make dog harness". Review search-terms report weekly, add negatives for anything irrelevant.

## First-7-days diagnostic
Low impressions → raise bids / expand keywords / fix feed / check categorization. Low CTR (<1%) → improve images / optimize titles / add highlights / check pricing vs competitors. Low conversion rate → check landing page / pricing / descriptions / reviews / checkout friction. Low ROAS → review product selection / bidding / feed quality / keyword targeting. Benchmarks: CTR 1-3%, conversion 2%+, ROAS 3x+ minimum (4x+ ideal).

## Scaling rule
Scale when: ROAS >3x, ≥50 conversions, consistent 7+ days, positive contribution margin. Cadence: week 1 +20% budget → week 2 +20% if ROAS holds → week 3+ +25-30% while profitable. **Never increase more than 30% in one adjustment** — sudden jumps reset Google's learning phase; gradual increases maintain performance. "More budget → more data → better optimization → better ROAS → more budget" flywheel; after ~90 days feed/bidding/negatives/scaling system fully dialed.

## Advanced tactics
Virtual product bundles in feed (higher AOV, less direct competition) · dynamic remarketing to viewed/cart/no-purchase · Customer Match upload of past-customer/subscriber/abandoned-cart emails for higher-bid targeted campaigns · Local Inventory Ads if physical locations exist · Merchant Promotions badges ("15% off first order", buy-2-get-1) shown directly in shopping results to lift CTR.

## Why 91% of brands miss this (failure modes)
Generic/low-quality feed (titles, images, categorization) → low impressions/poor placement. No campaign structure → best-sellers underfunded, losers eat budget. Set-and-forget → no negatives, no bid adjustment, declining performance. Wrong bidding order → starting on automated bidding with zero data → Google wastes budget "learning" → poor initial results → people give up before it works. No negative keywords → budget wasted on non-buyer searches.
