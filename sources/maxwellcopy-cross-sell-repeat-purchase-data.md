"Cross-sell everything!"

This is the most misunderstood and overrated advice I see in ecom.

Here's what actually gets people to buy again

Let's say someone buys a t-shirt…

Most brands will do everything in their power to push them to beach bags, then pants, then whatever else they sell.

But the data says to do the exact opposite!

We pulled the repeat purchase data on this in WellTV (our own retention analytics platform), and over half of repeat orders are the same product again, just in a different style.

Someone who buys a t-shirt is literally TELLING you they're a t-shirt buyer.

So why make it harder than it needs to be?

The question isn't "what do I cross-sell them next?"

It's "what did they already tell me they want more of?"

Simple beats clever every time.
