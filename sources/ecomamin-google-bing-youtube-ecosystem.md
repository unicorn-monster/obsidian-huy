## why most multi-channel setups fail

most brands running multiple channels are not running an ecosystem. they're running parallel campaigns on separate platforms with separate creative strategies, separate measurement frameworks, and no structural connection between them.

meta generates awareness. google captures some of it. bing is an afterthought or isn't running at all. the channels don't know about each other. the audience built on one platform doesn't inform the targeting on another.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HMJwcqUa8AAxlcN?format=jpg&name=large)

this is why most multi-channel reporting shows each channel "performing" while the blended CPA stays stubbornly high.

the ecosystem approach is different. each channel has a specific role. the audiences generated on one channel become the signals feeding the next. the buyer's journey across channels is anticipated and designed, not left to coincidence.

## the three channels and their roles

**google search and shopping: the intent capture layer**

google search and shopping are the highest-intent channels in the ecosystem.

non-branded search captures buyers who know their problem or their solution category but haven't chosen a brand. branded search captures buyers who already know the brand. shopping captures buyers in commercial intent mode comparing options in a grid.

**youtube: the awareness and remarketing engine**

youtube shorts reaches cold audiences at scale with low CPMs. a well-structured shorts campaign for an ecom brand can build a 100,000-person view audience in 60-90 days for a budget that most brands spend on a single week of meta retargeting.

youtube is also the remarketing engine for google search. buyers who clicked through an advertorial but didn't convert get retargeted on youtube with a longer-form product story. the impression cost is low. the audience is highly qualified.

VSLs work particularly well for high-consideration products. a 5-8 minute youtube video that walks through the full mechanism converts non-buying search visitors at meaningful rates.

**bing / microsoft ads: the ignored premium audience**

the microsoft ads network reaches a specific demographic: older, higher income, less exposed to the wave of blackhat ecom advertising that has trained younger demographics to be sceptical.

the specific niches where bing performs disproportionately well:

- supplements targeting 40+ demographics
- high-ticket home products and furniture
- health devices and monitoring equipment
- travel accessories and luggage
- gardening and outdoor living

bing also captures a meaningful share of google-intent buyers who use bing as their default search engine. every windows PC ships with microsoft edge and bing as default. a significant percentage of buyers never changed the default.

## how the three channels feed each other

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HMJwf7GbkAAW1xf?format=jpg&name=large)

**youtube builds the google audience:**

youtube shorts campaigns generate video view audiences. these audiences are added as audience signals to the google search and shopping campaigns. the buyer who saw a youtube shorts ad and then searches a category keyword on google is arriving at the google campaigns with prior brand context.

**google search feeds the youtube remarketing:**

non-converting search visitors get added to youtube remarketing audiences. the youtube retargeting campaign shows these buyers a longer-format product story. the impression cost is low. the audience is highly qualified.

**bing extends the google ecosystem without cannibalisation:**

the buyers on bing are not the same buyers seeing the google campaigns. different search engine, different demographic, different discovery path. building the same campaign architecture on bing captures a parallel demand pool that google campaigns never reach.

## the $1.4M case study

the account that produced $1.4M in conversion value from $203k in spend over 6 months was built on this exact ecosystem structure.

build sequence:

- month one: google foundation. branded search, Shopping with rebuilt feed titles, non-branded search with advertorials matched to each ICP. six separate ad groups per search campaign, one per intent cluster.
- month two: youtube shorts launched. three short-form video angles tested in week one. two winners identified by week three. remarketing pools started building.
- month two also: bing campaigns launched with the same structure as the google search campaigns but with adjusted bidding and copy. early CPA data on bing was 20% lower than equivalent google non-branded.
- month three: youtube view audiences added as signals to google MOF search campaigns. early data showed the view-through audience converting at 1.8x the rate of cold search traffic.
- months four through six: scaling within the established structure. no campaign rebuilds. no strategy changes.

final result: $203k spend, $1.4M conversion value. the account also ranked organically for the primary product category keywords as a byproduct of the campaign signals.

## what makes this an ecosystem rather than a strategy

a strategy produces linear results: spend X, get Y back. predictable, limited ceiling.

an ecosystem produces compounding results: the youtube audiences improve the google conversion rates, which justifies more spend on youtube, which builds larger audiences, which further improves google efficiency.

the ceiling on this system is significantly higher than any single channel. and the efficiency improves over time without proportional increases in spend.
