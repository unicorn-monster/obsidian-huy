Native Ads Will Make You Rich in 2026

Here Are 5 Native Ad Creative Prompts I Use:

Prompt 1 — Candid mirror photo (weight/midsection):
"Candid, plainly lit smartphone mirror photo of a woman in her late 30s standing sideways in an ordinary bathroom, wearing a fitted plain gray t-shirt and simple leggings, one hand resting flat on her lower stomach, looking down at her midsection with a neutral, slightly tired expression. Flat, even daylight from a small frosted window gives the scene a dull, honest, unflattering quality. The bathroom is unremarkable and real: beige tiles, a bath mat slightly askew, a laundry basket in the corner, a few bottles on the sink edge. The phone is faintly visible in her hand in the reflection. Slightly crooked framing, mild smartphone blur, neutral muted colors, a little sensor noise. Looks exactly like a real before-photo someone took on their own phone to track how they feel. No logos, no text, no flattering angles, no studio lighting."

Prompt 2 — Documentary man with bills (financial stress):
"Documentary, photojournalism-style smartphone photo of a man around 63 sitting at a cluttered kitchen table late in the evening, leaning over a messy stack of paper bills, bank statements, and an old desk calculator, pen in hand, brow furrowed, one hand pressed to the side of his head. A single warm overhead bulb lights the scene unevenly, leaving the corners of the room in shadow and casting a tired amber tone over everything. The table is full of real detail: a cold half-finished cup of coffee, reading glasses, an open envelope, a notepad with handwriting. He wears a plain flannel shirt, slightly hunched, visibly worried. Slightly soft focus, mild grain, warm dim white balance, natural unposed framing taken from across the table. Looks like a candid photo that belongs in a news article about retirement savings or rising costs. No logos, no text, no bank branding, no polished studio lighting."
