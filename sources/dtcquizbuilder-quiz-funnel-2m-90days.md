## The Problem: "No Foreplay" Funnels

Most DTC brands do this:

**Ad → Product Page → Checkout**

Cold traffic. No warmup. No relationship building. Just "buy now."

Result? 1-2% conversion rates and unprofitable campaigns.

The fix isn't better ads or a prettier product page.

It's what happens BETWEEN the ad and the offer.

We’ve be able to get this to convert at 4%+ on cold traffic at scale…

## Quiz Funnels as Competitive Arbitrage

Quizzes aren't new. But they're criminally underused in DTC.

While everyone's fighting over ad creative and product page optimization, the funnel itself is wide open for arbitrage.

Here's what a quiz does that a product page can't:

1. **Micro-commitments** — Each question answered is a small "yes" that compounds
2. **Belief seeding** — You shift their mindset BEFORE showing the price
3. **Objection pre-handling** — Their concerns get addressed through the questions themselves
4. **Data collection** — You know exactly what they care about (for retargeting)

By the time someone completes a 12-question quiz, they're not cold traffic anymore.

They've invested. They've told you their problems. They WANT a solution.

## The "Drop-Off Diagnosis" Method

Here's how I took our quiz from 19% completion to 25%.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/G-X7CeQWcAAjtxI?format=jpg&name=large)

Every question has a drop-off rate. The key is finding which question is killing your funnel.

We tracked completion rates per question:

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/G-X6-UwWsAASffu?format=png&name=large)

Question 4 was bleeding prospects.

The original question: "What solutions have you tried before?"

Options were too long. Too many choices. Decision fatigue.

**The fix:** Simplified to 4 options max. Added "None of the above."

Drop-off went from 22% → 9%.

One question fix. 6% more completions. Thousands more buyers.

Simply just find the question with the highest abandonment & split test it until it comes down to a normal rate.

## The "Low-Friction Entry" Sequence

I used to think the first question needed to be powerful.

Aspirational. Emotionally charged. "What do you want to achieve?"

Wrong.

Look at every high-converting quiz funnel — Bioma, Nooro, Lunavia, SkinnyFit — they ALL start the same way:

**Question 1:** Age or gender.

That's it. The most boring question imaginable.

**Why this works:**

1. **Zero cognitive load** — "How old are you?" requires no thinking. They just click.
2. **Starts the yes-chain** — One click in, they're committed. Quitting now feels like wasted effort.
3. **Sets up personalization** — The implied promise: "We need this to tailor your results." Now every question feels customized.

The aspirational stuff — "What do you want to achieve?" — comes at Q3 or Q4.

By then they're already invested. The emotional questions hit harder because they've committed to the process.

**The sequence that works:**

- 1: Age range
- 2: Gender
- 3: Goals/desires (NOW you go aspirational)
- 4: Pain points/struggles
- 5+: Deeper diagnosis

Don't try to hook them with your first question.

Get them clicking first. Hook them once they're in.

## The "Captive Attention" Loading Screen

Most brands waste the loading screen.

Spinning wheel. "Calculating your results..."

Dead time.

Here's what we do instead:

While the "analysis" loads, we show:

- A massive before/after testimonial ("Lost 47 lbs in 3 months!")
- Social proof stats ("Join 50,000+ customers who transformed their health")
- Product preview (so it feels familiar when they see the offer)

The prospect CAN'T click away. They're waiting for their results.

This is 10-15 seconds of captive attention. Use it.

**Your move:** Replace your loading screen with your strongest testimonial. Make those seconds work.

## The "Segment & Retarget" Data Play

Here's where most people leave money on the table.

Everyone who takes your quiz — even if they don't buy — gave you data:

- What they want to achieve
- What they've struggled with
- What solutions they've tried
- Their symptoms and pain points

You now know MORE about them than any pixel ever could.

**For non-buyers, we run retargeting campaigns segmented by quiz answers:**

- Answered "tried diets before, weight came back" → Ad about breaking the yo-yo cycle
- Answered "low energy" as main struggle → Ad focused on energy, not weight loss
- Answered "tried supplements before" → Ad addressing why this one is different

## What You Can Steal Today

**1\. Build your first quiz in 30 minutes**

I’ve dropped a few posts earlier on about how to get started with this

- Q1: Aspirational goal (multi-select)
- Q2: Main struggle (single-select)
- Q3: Past solutions tried (single-select)
- Q4: Symptoms/pain points (multi-select)
- Q5-6: Demographics (age, gender)
- Results page → Offer

**2\. Use this drop-off benchmark**

- Q1 drop-off: 30-40% is normal (biggest filter)
- Remaining questions: Under 15% each
- Overall completion: Aim for 25%+

If any single question has 10%+ drop-off, that's your fix.

**3\. Write questions with Claude**

Prompt: "I sell \[product\] to \[audience\]. Write 8 quiz questions that uncover their pain points and build desire for my solution. Each question should have 4-5 options. Include one 'breather' slide with social proof after question 4 & an urgency slider after question 7."

You'll get a solid first draft in 30 seconds.
