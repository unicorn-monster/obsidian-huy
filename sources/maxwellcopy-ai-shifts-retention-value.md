Every year of my work life summarized…

2020:
First taste of online business. Discovered reselling sports cards and was buying and selling pieces of cardboard all day, every day. Some of these were worth $3k.
What I learned: You could actually make real money on the internet transacting with random people.

2021:
Scaled up the card business and started my first personal brand around sports cards on Twitter, Instagram, and YouTube.
Found how out powerful a niche personal brand is.
Had to stop with sports cards as I went to college and couldn't have $10k+ of cards in my dorm room people could steal.
Started to get into crypto and NFTs
What I learned: The power of a niche personal brand.

2022:
Made a $100k+ then lost $100k+ from crypto & NFTs.
I was working all day every day doing research, being active in Discord groups, buying and selling, etc. I had a lot of ups and downs with it. I realized it wasn't sustainable and it was draining…
So I moved on and tried to build something real which ended up being copywriting.
Landed my first client within a couple weeks of outreach and was doing $10k a month within a few months with zero experience.
Got a few email marketing specific roles which I carried through the end of the year… relentlessly learning and implementing.
What I learned: There is no get rich quick scheme. A few months of ACTUAL brute force effort on a real business can get you results fast.
And most importantly… That year taught me you don't need to be scared of "business owners." Most of them have no clue what they're doing.
If you can prove you're smart, persistent, and show value up front, people will pay you no matter your experience or the fact that you look like a pimple-faced frat boy (because that is what I was).

2023:
Transition from a freelancer to building a real agency / company.
Started building a brand around Well Copy instead of Max the freelancer.
Made the biggest investment of my life ($12k) into a coaching program and it changed my life.
Grinded all day every day getting clients, struggling through building systems, hiring, etc. Ended the year with a real business and a few employees.
All while still… being in college and even studying abroad for 5 months in Prague.
What I learned: Literally everything. All the foundations of business. Sales, hiring, marketing, team management, automations, funnels, and everything in between. This year was the most I've learned / changed ever.

2024:
This is the year my content started to rip…
Went from signing 2 clients per month to 6-10 per month just from the videos I was posting on the internet for free.
To back the growth, we went from 4 employees to 30+ by the end of the year.
The hardest part was having to take a step back from fulfillment and let my team execute rather than me micromanage.
What I learned: Post every day your tactical knowledge and eventually the right people will come to you. Content is king.
I also learned how to delegate, how to hire people who actually knew more than me, and allocate my time on the stuff that mattered.

2025:
Same model sticking to just email & sms, but this year just pushed harder on everything that was already working.
Started to spend even more time on content and start working with agencies to help me pump more stuff out.
Big shift as well was hiring a sales rep to take calls off my plate cause I was spending 6 hours a day on sales.
Spent lots of time training. I kept audits to myself. This was a major unlock and biggest win for the year.
Also… I finished college and graduated in May!
Sales kept steady from my content so to keep up with demand we grew from 30 to 70+ people in the company. In this year we had to make real structure within the team and start getting real leaders…
Head of Copy, Head of Design, Head of Klaviyo Ops, Senior Strategists, etc etc etc.
We started to become an actual big company and one of the biggest in the space.
What I learned: There's no one playbook to follow and don't follow anyone's advice.
At some point you're growing faster than the people you'd take advice from, so you trust your gut and double down on what's working and what's unique to you. Build your company based on what feels right to you.

2026:
This year has been all about expanding our depth.
AI made pure execution way less valuable, so we've been focused on making what we do worth more than that.
Real retention and subscription expertise on top of full funnels.
Truly becoming industry leaders in terms of expertise and knowledge… providing a data and service that no one else can provide.
Expanded from just email & sms in Klaviyo to true retention management in Shopify and subscription platforms
What I learned (so far):
Once you get to this size, take shots and big swings. The company can grow and manage without me and the growth will plateau (as it already has started to)… so at this level to really see 2x+ growth, we have to take swings.
Some of them miss and some of them stick… But the ones that stick have exponential returns.
