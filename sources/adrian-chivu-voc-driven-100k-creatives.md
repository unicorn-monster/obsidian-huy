3 ads spending $180k. And the idea behind all three came from the same place most creative strategists ignore. My own customers. How many ads have you seen on your feed this week that say: "Premium quality" "Designed for the modern woman" "Elevate your everyday look" Nobody reads that nonsense. Nobody feels anything looking at that copy. You know who writes better copy than any marketer? The woman who bought the necklace and wrote a 4-sentence review about it at 11 pm because she was so moved she had to tell someone. She used her own words. Her own language. Her emotions spoke about why this product mattered to her. And that is the copy that scales past $100K in spend and still converts cold traffic. Not because it sounds good. Because it sounds like her. That is messaging resonance. That is the "is this me?" moment that fires before logic even turns on. Here's what I need you to do right now to start building $100k+ creatives: 1. Read every review, every ad comment, every DM. 2. Find the words that repeat and keep track of them. 3. Find the phrases customers used to describe how the product made them feel. 4. Find the specific moments they wore it, and the specific people they bought it for. Take those exact words and build the hooks, the body copy, and the identity statements directly from them. Don't try to be clever with your words, and no "elevated basics for the modern woman" nonsense. Your customers have already written your best ads. Go read them.
