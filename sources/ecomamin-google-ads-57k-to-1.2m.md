## strategy 1: fix the google merchant center (the 70% solution)

before touching campaigns, i audited their GMC feed

it was a disaster

product title: "pain relief device - laser therapy"

generic, boring, doesn't match what people search

i rebuilt it with this structure:

leading keyword + what it's for + angle + brand (if known)

new title: "pain relief LLLT cold laser therapy device for pain management - low-level laser therapy machine for home use by \[brand\]"

see the difference?

it targets actual search terms:

- "pain relief device"
- "laser therapy for pain"
- "LLLT device"
- "cold laser therapy"
- "home pain management"

one title, multiple search term matches

next: descriptions

their description: "made from high quality materials, portable and easy to use"

features nobody cares about

my description: "fixes your back pain in 14 days or full refund. clinically proven LLLT technology used by physiotherapists. 20-minute treatments at home replace expensive clinic visits. 60-day money-back guarantee."

outcomes, risk reversal, social proof

finally: images

they had white background product shots

i added lifestyle images:

- someone using it on their back
- comparison of inflammation before/after
- size comparison (showing it fits in a bag)

result from just GMC optimization: 30% more clicks, 15% better conversion rate

didn't change campaigns at all, just fixed the feed

## strategy 3: negative keywords (the profit protector)

every week i downloaded search terms report

fed it into claude with this prompt:

"analyze these search terms, flag any that are informational, price shopping, wrong audience, or unlikely to convert for a $400 pain relief device"

then added those as negatives

example negatives added:

- "free" (people wanting free solutions)
- "diy" (people wanting to build their own)
- "reviews reddit" (researchers not buyers)
- "chiropractor" (looking for professional treatment)
- "insurance coverage" (wrong buying mechanism)
- "cheap" "affordable" "budget" (price shoppers for a $400 product)

result: saved $12k/month in wasted spend

that's $144k/year redirected to profitable keywords

this ALONE paid for my entire service 3x over

## strategy 5: margin-based bidding (protecting profitability at scale)

not all products have same margins

their catalog:

- basic device: $400 retail, $120 cogs = 70% margin
- premium device: $650 retail, $180 cogs = 72% margin
- accessory bundle: $180 retail, $40 cogs = 78% margin

i used supplemental feed in GMC to tag each product with margin tier

then built separate campaigns:

high-margin campaign (accessories, bundles):

- aggressive bidding
- target 3x roas minimum
- scale hard

medium-margin campaign (premium device):

- moderate bidding
- target 4x roas
- scale steady

low-margin campaign (basic device):

- conservative bidding
- target 6x roas minimum
- only scale if hitting targets

most agencies bid the same for everything

result: unprofitable products eat budget that should go to profitable ones

with margin-based bidding: added $200k/month revenue at SAME ad spend

just by allocating budget to products with best unit economics

## the strategies that didn't work (what i tested and killed)

not everything worked, here's what failed:

**video ads on youtube:**

tested $3k budget on youtube action campaigns

roas: 1.2x

killed it after 30 days

why it failed: wrong format for high-ticket pain relief device, people need to read details and reviews, video wasn't compelling enough

**broad match keywords:**

tested letting google auto-expand keywords

roas dropped from 6x to 2.8x

killed it after 2 weeks

why it failed: google matched us to "free pain relief" and "home remedies" - wrong intent

**pmax without brand exclusion:**

initially ran pmax including brand terms

it cannibalized our 18x roas brand campaign

roas on pmax: 4x, roas on brand dropped to 12x

fixed by excluding brand from pmax

**automated bidding from day 1:**

tried target roas bidding on new campaigns with no data

spent $2k, got 0.8x roas

switched to manual cpc, got data, then moved to target roas

lesson: algorithm needs data before it works
