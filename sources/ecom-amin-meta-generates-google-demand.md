## why the customer journey is never a straight line

the fantasy version of meta ads attribution: buyer sees ad, clicks ad, reads landing page, buys product. one session, one touchpoint, one conversion.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HNCkHbIa8AAcnzA?format=png&name=large)

the reality: the buyer sees the ad on instagram at 7am. the product looks interesting but they're not ready to spend $45 on something they've never heard of. they keep scrolling.

three days later, they're talking to a friend who mentions the exact problem your product solves. the buyer remembers the brand. they open google and type something close to your brand name. a google shopping result appears. they recognise the product image. they click. they buy.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HNCkJ4xbYAEqQMt?format=png&name=large)

who gets the attribution? if you're running meta only, meta's attribution model gives that conversion to the 7-day view-through window. the dashboard looks fine. but the actual conversion happened on google. and if you weren't running google ads when that buyer searched, you either got lucky with organic or you lost them entirely.

this is the demand leak. and it exists in every single meta-first ecom account.

## the 24-hour fix: part 1 (branded search, 1 hour setup)

branded search is the simplest campaign in google ads. it's also the most valuable per dollar spent.

keyword list:

- your brand name (exact match)
- your brand name (phrase match)
- brand name + product type
- brand name + review
- brand name + discount
- brand name + buy
- common misspellings of your brand name

ad copy:

- headline 1: your brand name
- headline 2: your core product benefit in under 30 characters
- headline 3: your strongest trust signal (star rating, number of reviews, free shipping)
- description 1: one line on what the product does and why it's different
- description 2: one line with a risk-reversal (money-back guarantee, free returns)

landing page destination: your homepage or the specific PDP your meta ads are driving traffic to. match the destination to whatever the meta campaign promotes.

bid strategy: maximise clicks to start. switch to target CPA or maximise conversions once you have 30+ conversions in the campaign, which typically happens within 2-3 weeks for any brand running meta at scale.

budget: start at $20-30/day. branded search CPCs are low because the competition on your exact brand name is usually minimal.

negative keywords: add all generic category terms that aren't your brand name. you don't want the branded campaign showing for non-branded queries.

this takes about an hour to set up. publish the campaign and it goes into google's review queue.

## the google review period (24 hours)

once you hit publish, google reviews the ad copy, billing information, and URL compliance.

for standard ecommerce products, this review typically completes within one business day. sometimes faster, within a few hours.

## the traffic leak plugs (immediate after approval)

the second your campaign status changes to "approved", your ads are live in the auction.

because the search intent is extremely specific, google ranks your ad at the top of the page almost instantly. there's no learning phase for branded search the way there is for broad non-branded campaigns.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HNCkWJYbkAAWN4W?format=png&name=large)

clicks start hitting your site within minutes of approval.

branded search CPA is almost always the lowest CPA in any google ads account. the buyer already knows and trusts the brand. the conversion rate on branded search is typically 3-8x higher than cold traffic from any platform.

every branded search click you capture is a sale you were previously losing to organic variance, competitor bidding, or buyer drop-off.

## the 24-hour fix: part 2 (non-branded search, same session)

while the branded campaign is in review, build a non-branded search campaign for the category keywords your meta ads are priming buyers for.

keyword categories to target:

- product category keywords: "magnesium supplement", "dog anxiety supplement", "wireless earbuds"
- problem-oriented keywords: "best supplement for sleep", "how to reduce dog anxiety", "best earbuds for running"
- comparison keywords: "magnesium glycinate vs oxide", "best wireless earbuds 2026"

match types: phrase match to start.

landing page strategy: this is the critical difference from branded search. non-branded traffic is colder. sending them to a PDP is the most common mistake in google ads.

build a simple advertorial page (800-1200 words) that opens with the problem the keyword describes, explains why the common solutions don't work, introduces your product as the answer, includes social proof, and drives to the PDP with a clear CTA.

budget: $30-50/day to start.

## google shopping (the third layer, same session)

if you're selling a physical product, set up a google shopping campaign in the same session.

the quick wins on the shopping feed:

- rewrite product titles so the first 60 characters contain the non-branded keywords buyers actually search. "Magnesium Glycinate Sleep Supplement 500mg 120 Capsules" beats "Brand Name Magnesium 500mg" for discovery reach.
- upload at least one lifestyle image per product.
- make sure "free shipping" displays in the listing if your economics support it.
- connect google customer reviews so your star rating and review count appear on the listing.

campaign structure: standard shopping campaign to start, not PMax. maximise clicks bid strategy. let it run for 2-3 weeks before making bid strategy changes.
