## funnel 1: the advertorial

what it is: a hybrid between an editorial article and a sales page. reads like content from a health magazine. converts like a direct-response ad.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HMtRH9aaEAESgmy?format=png&name=large)

when to use it: cold TOF traffic from problem-oriented search queries.

the queries it converts: "why can't i sleep", "best natural remedy for joint pain", "how to reduce cortisol naturally", "why is my dog anxious".

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HMtRKlRaYAAhnae?format=png&name=large)

the structure that converts:

- the hook (first 100 words): opens with the exact pain the buyer searched for, in their language.
- the agitation (200-300 words): deepens the problem. explains why it happens. uses physiological or mechanical detail that makes the reader feel understood.
- the mechanism (300-400 words): introduces the category of solution, not the brand. explains why previous solutions didn't work.
- the solution (200-300 words): the brand enters as the logical conclusion of everything above.
- the social proof (150-200 words): two specific testimonials with names, details, and outcomes that couldn't be fabricated.
- the CTA: soft, educational, drives to the PDP.

the metric that tells you it's working: CVR from advertorial to PDP click above 25%. CVR from advertorial visitor to purchase above 2% on cold traffic.

## funnel 2: the comparison page

what it is: a direct side-by-side comparison between your product and the alternatives in your category.

when to use it: MOF traffic from evaluation queries and conquest campaigns.

why most comparison pages fail: they're dishonest. they show the brand winning on every metric. the buyer knows this is propaganda. they leave.

the structure that converts:

- the opening: frames the comparison around the buyer's decision criteria, not the brand's strengths.
- the comparison table: 5-7 factors that matter to the buyer. acknowledge where competitors win.
- honest assessment: the page converts at 4.1% in our accounts for conquest traffic because the buyer in evaluation mode trusts transparency more than advocacy.
- social proof: review volume comparison.
- CTA: drives to the PDP with the specific product the comparison recommended.

the metric that tells you it's working: CVR from comparison page to PDP above 30%. overall CVR above 3% for conquest traffic.

## funnel 3: the quiz funnel

what it is: a 5-8 question interactive quiz that narrows the recommendation to a specific product based on the buyer's answers.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HMtRNHibsAEI4A8?format=png&name=large)

when to use it: high-consideration purchases where the buyer knows they want something in the category but doesn't know which specific product is right for them.

why it works: the quiz builds micro-commitments before the sale. each question increases the buyer's investment in the outcome. by the time they reach the recommendation, they've spent 60-90 seconds telling you about their needs.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HMtROpsbQAETjd4?format=png&name=large)

the critical detail most quiz funnels miss: show the logic. the buyer needs to see why the quiz recommended this specific product based on their answers.

the metric that tells you it's working: quiz completion rate above 65%. CVR from results page to purchase above 5%.

## funnel 4: the listicle

what it is: "top 5 \[product category\] for \[specific use case\]" presented as editorial content.

when to use it: MOF traffic from ranking and comparison queries.

the structure that converts:

- the intro: establishes what the ranking criteria are and why the reader should trust this evaluation.
- position #1 (the brand's product): the most detailed breakdown. 300-400 words. covers the mechanism, the test results, the pros, the cons.
- positions #2-5 (category alternatives): 100-150 words each. honest assessments.
- the conclusion: a summary table with the top 3 side by side on the key factors.

the critical detail: the listicle must feel like editorial content. include genuine downsides for the brand's product and genuine upsides for competitors.

the metric that tells you it's working: time on page above 3 minutes. CTR from listicle to PDP above 20%. CVR from listicle visitor to purchase above 2.5%.

## funnel 5: the PDP

what it is: the product page where the purchase happens.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HMtRV_gbcAAdfbk?format=png&name=large)

when to use it: BOF traffic only. never for cold traffic.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HMtRV-Mb0AAj7oI?format=png&name=large)

what a high-converting PDP includes that most don't:

- above the fold: the product name, the one-line benefit statement, the star rating and review count, the price, and "free shipping" if applicable. all visible without scrolling.
- the mechanism section (below the fold): a 100-150 word explanation of how the product works. not the ingredient list. the mechanism.
- social proof block: the 3-5 best reviews, with photos if available, displayed prominently.
- objection handling: a FAQ section that addresses the top 5 objections explicitly.
- trust signals: money-back guarantee, return policy, certifications, press mentions.

the metric that tells you it's working: CVR from PDP to purchase above 4% for branded traffic. above 3% for shopping traffic. above 2% for traffic arriving from an advertorial or comparison page.
