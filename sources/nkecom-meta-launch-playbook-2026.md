**Here’s the most up to date guide to launching a new brand on Meta in 2026:**

First, you need to understand how outdated most advice on this topic actually is.

Which is why I believe it’s important to mention that the last time I launched a brand successfully was 3 months ago…

I remember, when I launched my first 7-figure successful drop-shipping store in 2016 - we started with 3 creative:

- 2 static ads (AliExpress images)

With the following campaign structure:

- 10 ad sets at $5/day
- Same 3 ads in each ad set

After two days, I increased the budget on the profitable ad sets, killed the ones that weren’t performing & refreshed them with new audiences

A few weeks later we were spending $1,000/ day at a 12 ROAS.

It was fucking insane.

**Yet I still see people teaching the same strategies I stopped doing years ago? Which is why I wanted to create this guide.**

**Here’s how we're launching new brands / product tests now:**

**1) We never, ever use interest targeting.**

I don’t care what anyone says. Interest targeting died years ago. There is no such thing as “helping your pixel” - it’s bullshit. When you launch a new campaign - keep it broad as possible. If I’m selling women’s products, I won’t even bother narrowing the gender down to women.

The algorithm knows best & creative is the new targeting.

**2) We only ever launch 1 ad set to start**

I don’t understand why anyone would need more than 1 ad set on a test. All you are doing is fragmenting Meta’s learning. You need 50+ conversions / week on an ad set to exit the learning phase (trust me - it matters). The best thing you can do (especially on a lower budget) is consolidate all of your ads in one ad set in order to **exit the learning phase as quickly as possible.**

You need to understand that on a small budget, there is so much inconsistency & variance in results.

You need to stack all the odds in your favour. Once you start increasing spend, you can start introducing new campaigns. But I wouldn’t even consider doing that until at least $300/day in ad spend.

**3) We spam tf outta ads**

This is probably the most important part. When launching a new product test in 2026, I’m a big believer that you have to give the machine as many ads as possible. Anyone who tells you that you are “spreading yourself too thin” is stupid & doesn’t know what they are talking about.

It’s important to note that not all 50 ads are going to spend. Meta's machine will predict the best 1-5 ads & concentrate spend there (yes, it's smart enough to do that). This is what is meant to happen.

Luckily, in this AI age, it has never been easier to produce so many high quality ads with such little effort. When we launched our new brand on Jan 1st, every single ad in the ad account was a static ad made with AI.

We launched around 50. 1 of them got spend. The one that got spend was the 48th we created. Do you understand why volume is important now?

**If you only want to launch with 5 ads - then you are going to get lapped every single time by people like me that launch with 50.**

**Tip: when creating the 50 ads, try & do 10 from each awareness stage.**

**4) You need $150-200 to test.**

Once you have created all of your ads, you need to throw them all into one ad set & set a $50-100 budget to go live at midnight.

I think $50 is realistically the minimum starting budget you need. I personally like to start at $200/day but if you are a beginner, then $50 is fine.

**5) Analyze your metrics**

Once your ads have spent a full day’s budget, you need to analyze your metrics so that you know your next move. This will tell you **one of three things:**

1. Kill the test
2. Carry on the test
3. Test complete - scale

**When to (1) kill the test (the warning signs):**

- **Add to cart rate is below 5%** - this means there is not enough demand for your product
- **CPC over $2** - unless your AOV is $100+, you probably won’t be able to scale at this level
- **CPMs over $75 -** this usually means you are entering a tricky market to navigate with FB policies (your ads/copy is too aggressive)

**When to (2) carry on the test:**

- **Add to cart rate 5-8%** - this means your offer needs improving, or you need to improve trust etc
- **You get conversions -** double down on the messaging in the ad that got conversion & upload similar ads with same angle

**When to (3) scale:**

- **Add to cart rate is above 8%** \- this means there is demand for your offer & if you pump enough budget, it should convert at 2-3%
- **If you’re ROAS is 1.5+** \- double down on the angle in the ad that got conversion & upload 20+ similar ads with same angle (in the same ad set)
- **If you’re getting subscribers** \-if you get subscriptions on the first day, it’s a winner

Now look, obviously these are very general rules & there will of course be exceptions - but this is pretty much the playbook we follow.

When we launched our new brand, the ROAS was 0.8 first day but we had a lot of add to carts & 2 subscribers so we knew it would be a winner.

Our ROAS is now 1.6+ on that brand, which shows that performance will improve (as you keep testing new creatives & upgrading your funnels) - but I don’t think I’ve ever seen a brand become successful without immediately getting conversion in the first 24 hours (or at least a 8.5% ATC rate). If you don’t get conversions, strengthen your offer or test a new product.

It’s as simple as that. I hope this helps. **GOOD LUCK & HAPPY SCALING**
