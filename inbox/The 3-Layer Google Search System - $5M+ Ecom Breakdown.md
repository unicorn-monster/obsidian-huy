The full breakdown of the 3-layer google search system behind $5M+ in ecom revenue. Each layer gets its own keyword strategy, ad copy, landing page, and bidding logic - and every diagnostic you need to know if it's working.

🗺️ The 4-Layer System Overview

Every search query falls into one of four categories based on what the buyer knows at the moment they search. Mix the layers in one campaign and they cancel each other out. Separate them and each layer compounds.

The 4 query types:

TOF - Problem-aware: Know they have a problem. Don't know a product category solves it. Searching "why do i wake up at 3am" or "leg cramps at night causes."

MOF - Solution-aware: Know the category exists. Figuring out which brand to buy. Searching "magnesium for sleep" or "best magnesium supplement."

BOF - Brand-aware: Know your brand. Ready to buy. Searching "\[brand name\]" or "\[brand name\] discount."

Conquesting - Competitor-aware: Know the category and a competitor brand. Mid-comparison. Searching "AG1 alternative" or "competitor A vs competitor B."

Each layer gets: its own campaign, its own keywords, its own ad copy, its own landing pages, its own bidding strategy, its own success metrics.

Applying the same ROAS target across all of them is the structural equivalent of having one campaign.

Revenue attribution from $5M+ accounts:

| Layer | Revenue Share | ROAS Profile |
| --- | --- | --- |
| MOF Transactional | 35–40% | Highest ROAS |
| MOF Category | 25–30% | Moderate ROAS, most volume growth potential |
| BOF Branded | 15–20% | Highest absolute ROAS, limited ceiling |
| TOF | 5–10% direct | Significantly more via branded lift + remarketing pool quality |
| Conquesting | 5–10% | Variable by category competitiveness |

Brands running only BOF + MOF transactional leave 40–50% of revenue on the table.

📣 Layer 1 - Top-of-Funnel (Problem-Aware Search)

The largest pool of demand on Google for most ecom categories - and the most ignored. Keyword volume is massive. CPCs are low. Conversion rate on a standard product page is basically zero.

The reason it doesn't convert on product pages: someone who searched "can't fall asleep at night" doesn't know magnesium supplements exist. Showing them an add-to-cart page asks them to skip four mental steps they haven't taken yet.

The solution is the advertorial.

The TOF advertorial - 5 stages:

Hook - Mirror the exact keyword. "if you wake up at 3am every night and can't get back to sleep, you're not alone." Reader needs to feel understood in the first 2 sentences. Time on page data consistently shows: if readers leave before 90 seconds, the problem is always in the first 200 words.

Problem agitation - Make the cost visible. Fatigue compounding over weeks. Mental fog. Performance dropping at work. The reader needs to understand they can't keep ignoring this.

Mechanism - Explain why the problem happens. Cite research. Give them a model they didn't have. This is the educational core and what separates advertorials that convert from those that don't.

Solution category - Introduce the category that solves it, not the brand. Build the case for the category before naming any product. Pre-sells the product type so when your brand appears, the buyer is already convinced they need the category.

Product positioning - Introduce your product as the right version. Form, dose, manufacturing standard, social proof, clear CTA. By this point the reader has been educated - not sold to - and the CTA feels like the natural next step.

Target length: 1,500–2,500 words. Shorter doesn't work (reader needs time through all 5 stages). Longer loses them. Page should read like a magazine article, not a sales letter.

Campaign structure:

Separate campaigns per problem cluster (sleep / stress / pain / energy) - audiences are different even if the product is the same

Exact and phrase match only - broad match on symptom keywords burns budget on irrelevant queries

Bidding: manual CPC to build clean data → tROAS at 30+ conversions, set 30–40% below MOF target

Ad copy formula:

H1: \[Problem stated back to them\] e.g. "waking up at 3am every night?" H2: \[Curiosity gap\] e.g. "there's a reason most people miss" H3: \[Soft CTA\] e.g. "read the full breakdown" Desc: Educational, not commercial e.g. "new research from sleep specialists points to a single mineral deficiency behind most cases. read the full guide and what cyclists, athletes, and over-50s are doing to fix it."

Metrics:

New visitor rate: 90%+ (below = remarketing drift, verify exclusions)

Time on page: 3+ minutes (below 90 seconds = hook failure, fix first 200 words - not the middle of the page)

Remarketing pool growth: increasing weekly (this is the real ROI of TOF)

Branded search lift: visible within 4–8 weeks of TOF running

🔍 Layer 2 - Mid-Funnel (Solution & Product-Aware Search)

The volume layer. Searcher already knows the solution category exists - now figuring out which brand to buy. MOF splits into 3 sub-types, each needing different treatment.

Sub-type 1: Category search

Searcher is browsing the category. "magnesium for sleep" / "best magnesium supplement" / "natural sleep supplement."

Keywords: exact + phrase match; broad only after a keyword theme has 10+ purchases on exact match

Bidding: tCPA once 30+ conversions in 30 days

Landing page: category-specific advertorial or comparison page - reader knows the category but not why yours is different; lead with absorption, form, manufacturing, third-party testing

Ad copy: H1: category benefit - "magnesium that actually helps you sleep" H2: differentiator - "lab-tested bisglycinate form, 400mg per serving" H3: proof - "trusted by 50,000+ customers"

Sub-type 2: Comparison search

Searcher is mid-decision. "magnesium bisglycinate vs glycinate" / "best form of magnesium" / "magnesium gummies vs tablets."

Keywords: exact + phrase match (comparison searches are precise by nature)

Bidding: max conversions with cap → tCPA

Landing page: comparison article - lead with the answer, build with research and data, end with your product as the version that gets the comparison right (doubles as organic SEO)

Sub-type 3: Transactional search

Searcher is ready to buy. "buy magnesium bisglycinate" / "magnesium supplement free shipping" / "magnesium subscribe and save."

Keywords: exact + phrase match

Bidding: tROAS (revenue signal is strong and consistent)

Landing page: direct to PDP - price above fold, star ratings, clear add-to-cart, trust signals, subscription option, bundle upsells. An advertorial here adds friction and kills conversions.

Ad copy: H1: lead with offer - "free shipping on magnesium gummies" H2: social proof - "4.8 stars from 2,000+ reviews" H3: friction removal - "30-day money-back guarantee"

MOF metrics:

Auction insights: monitored weekly for competitors on the same queries

CVR by sub-type: transactional should be 2–3x category search CVR (if transactional CVR is low, the page is adding friction)

Search term report: reviewed weekly - surfaces new comparison and transactional queries constantly

🏆 Layer 3 - Bottom-of-Funnel (Branded Search)

The most defensible real estate in the account. Every branded query not captured is a warm buyer intercepted by a competitor.

Goal: 95%+ search impression share at the lowest possible CPC.

Bidding: manual CPC only. Smart bidding on branded terms overpays for conversions the algorithm would have captured at a lower price. Never hand this to the algorithm.

Campaign structure:

Ad setup:

H1: pinned to brand name

H2: pinned to primary USP

Fill every extension: sitelinks (hero product, subscription offer, reviews page, shipping & returns), callouts (lab tested, free shipping, money-back guarantee), seller ratings from GMC reviews

Key metrics:

SIS: 95%+ - below this means competitors are capturing branded queries

IS lost to budget: zero - branded search is never budget-constrained

CTR: 25–40%

Avg CPC: monitored weekly for spikes (signals a competitor entering the auction)

Competitor defense protocol:

Pull auction insights on the branded campaign to see which competitors are bidding on your brand name

Check ads transparency centre - search your brand name to see their exact headline and description

If they run a price comparison angle → add a counter-message to your branded ad rotation

If they use your brand name in their headline → file a trademark complaint via Google's policy tool

⚔️ Layer 4 - Competitor Conquesting

Different economics from every other layer. Searcher knows the category and knows a competitor brand by name - they're mid-comparison and your brand isn't in their consideration set yet.

The economics:

Conversion rate: 50–70% of MOF

Customer LTV: typically higher - these buyers are already committed to the category

Measure CAC against LTV, not first-purchase ROAS

Structure:

Dedicated campaign - not an ad group within MOF. Own budget, own bidding, own reporting

Exact + phrase match only. No broad match on competitor brand names

Bidding: manual CPC or low tROAS to start

Ad copy formula:

H1: comparison framing without naming the competitor e.g. "looking for a better option?" H2: your differentiator e.g. "bisglycinate, not oxide" / "third-party tested, no fillers" H3: offer or proof

Landing page:

Comparison page or editorial listicle - structured as a ranking with your brand first. Each competitor assessed on the same criteria: ingredient form, dosage, testing, price per serving, subscription terms.

Competitor names in the page copy improve quality score on conquesting keywords. The same page doubles as organic SEO content.

🔄 How the Layers Feed Each Other

This is where the system becomes more than the sum of its parts.

The compounding loop:

TOF captures problem-aware searchers → some convert immediately via advertorial → the rest enter your remarketing pool (warm targets for PMax, YouTube retargeting, display at lower CPA)

Conquesting captures people about to buy from a competitor → redirects them → over time they become loyal customers who search your brand directly for repeat purchases → feeds BOF volume

Net result:

TOF fills remarketing pool → improves PMax efficiency → increases branded search volume → converts at highest ROAS

Brands running only BOF + MOF transactional are capturing existing demand that has a hard ceiling. The ones adding TOF and conquesting are creating new demand and stealing competitor demand - that's how you break through the ceiling and scale.

📊 Advanced Diagnostics

| Layer | Target SIS | If below | If above |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| BOF | 95%+ | Immediate action: budget, bid, or quality score | Healthy |
| MOF | 50–70% | Build conversion data, push bids on proven themes | Healthy |
| TOF | Varies by angle | Sub-20% with strong data → increase budget | 80% with poor data → pause and rebuild the page |

IS lost to budget = increase budget on profitable campaigns.

IS lost to rank = quality score problem - fix the weakest component first, don't raise bids blind.

Auction insights:

Run at campaign level across all layers.

BOF: shows which competitors bid on your brand and how aggressively

MOF: shows which brands compete for research-phase queries

Conquesting: shows how strong the competitor's own-traffic defence is (tells you how expensive conquesting will be)

Combine with ads transparency centre - near-complete competitive picture, no research tools needed.

Quality score targets:

| Layer | Target QS | If below |
| --- | --- | --- |
| BOF | 8–10 | Structurally wrong - diagnose by component |
| MOF | 6–8 | Fix weakest component first |
| TOF | 5–7 initially | Well-matched advertorial can push to 7+ |
| Conquesting | 3–5 | Expected - don't chase higher |

At $10k/day spend, a 1-point QS improvement across TOF campaigns = 10–15% CPC reduction. On $300k/month = $30–45k in savings.

The 3 QS components (each diagnosed and fixed independently): expected CTR + ad relevance + landing page experience.

Search term report - weekly protocol:

Identify converting queries not yet added as exact match → add them

Identify high-spend zero-conversion queries → add as negatives immediately

Identify new keyword themes appearing across campaigns → build a dedicated campaign with its own page

💰 Bid Management + Ad Testing System

Bidding strategy by layer:

| Layer | Strategy | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| BOF | Manual CPC always | Never hand branded terms to the algorithm |
| MOF Category | Max conversions with cap → tCPA at 30+ convs | Set tCPA 20% above current actual CPA |
| MOF Comparison | Max conversions with cap → tCPA | Comparison searches converge well on tCPA |
| MOF Transactional | Max conversions with cap → tROAS | Strong, consistent revenue signal |
| TOF | Max conversions with cap → tROAS | Set 30–40% below MOF target |
| Conquesting | Manual CPC or very low tROAS | CPCs structurally high - manual prevents overpaying |

Switching trigger: conversion volume, not time. 30 conversions in 30 days is the minimum threshold for smart bidding to function.

When switching to tROAS: set the initial target above current performance. If the campaign delivers 4x on max conversions, set tROAS at 3x. Tightening too early restricts the algorithm before it learns.

Scaling bids: increase targets in 10–15% increments over 2-week periods. Aggressive jumps cause the algorithm to cut spend and lose auction learning.

Black Friday / promotional periods: loosen targets. A campaign running normal tROAS during peak windows underbids the highest-value traffic of the year.

RSA testing:

2–3 RSAs per ad group. Vary H2 and H3 - that's where the test lives

Descriptions: one benefit-led / one proof-led / one urgency-led

Minimum 1,000 impressions per RSA before evaluating (below this = noise)

Primary metric: conversion rate, not CTR. A high-CTR ad that doesn't convert is worse than a lower-CTR ad that does

Low-volume ad groups: give it 4–6 weeks before evaluating - pausing too early destroys learning

Once one RSA has a meaningful CVR advantage over 2–4 weeks: pause the loser, replace with a challenger testing a different angle. Winner stays. Continuous improvement, not one-time optimisation
